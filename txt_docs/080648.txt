Aural amplifier
1615

The aural amplifier was a device worn on the head, which allowed the wearer to hear from a great distance by amplifying sound waves originating from the direction of the wearer's interest while dampening ambient noise. The creation of aural amplifiers marked a change in Durkish Corporation's policy, as they abandoned visual detection entirely in favor of aural.
