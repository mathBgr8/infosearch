It was the source of the earliest lightsaber crystals, known as Adegan crystals, and was also home to venomous creatures known as Adegan eels. thumb|left|250px|Adega and its surroundings Adega was a terrestrial planet located within the Adega system, one of the Auril systems, and a part of the Auril sector in the Slice portion of the Outer Rim Territories. It orbited the twin stars Adega Prime and Adega Besh. Adega was situated near the planet Ossus and the Perlemian Trade Route. Adega was located in the Adega system, which was once the site of an important ancient Jedi stronghold. Before the discovery of the planet Ilum around 22,800 BBY, the earliest lightsaber crystals came from Adega. The construction of the first lightsabers would have been impossible without the use of Adegan crystals. Even after Ilum succeeded Adega as the primary source of lightsaber crystals, the three most common varieties&mdash;kathracite, mephite, and pontite—were still referred to as Adegan crystals. By 20 BBY, during the Clone Wars between the Galactic Republic and the Confederacy of Independent Systems, Adega was undecided on its affiliation to either the Republic or the Confederacy. Adega was home to the slithery fish known as Adegan eel, a venomous creature with vicious fangs and a slime-covered body. Adega was introduced in the 1998 video game Star Wars: Rebellion, which provided the planet's only visual depiction&mdash;an image also used to illustrate twelve other worlds in the game. Star Wars: Rebellion and its accompanying strategy guide placed Adega in the Moddell sector.Star Wars: Rebellion: Prima's Official Strategy Guide The 2009 reference book The Essential Atlas subsequently placed the Adega system, and therefore the planet itself, in grid square R-6. The Essential Atlas described Adega as being a "world near Ossus" in its entry for Ilum. However, the reference book did not list Adega among the planets of the Adega system in its entry for Ossus.
