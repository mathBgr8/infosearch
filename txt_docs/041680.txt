|gender=Male |pronouns=He/him |height= |mass= |hair= |feathers= |eyes= |skin=Mottled green |cyber= |families= |parents= |partners= |siblings= |children= |affiliation= |masters= |apprentices= }} Yee Moh was a rather lonely-looking Neimoidian male who lost his job by 22 BBY.The New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY During a night in that year, Moh visited the Outlander Club, a nightclub and gambling hall in the Uscru Entertainment District on the planetStar Wars: Complete Locations Coruscant, and found companionship with the Neimoidian sisters Ko and Rina Itub. The trio stood together just as the Jedi Anakin Skywalker walked past them, searching for the bounty hunter Zam Wesell after her assassination attempt on Senator Padmé Amidala. What Moh was doing at the club was a mystery, as members of his species preferred gaudier locations. Moh had mottled green skin, and he wore gray robes and a hat while at the club. He also wore data goggles, which provided constant holographic data readouts. }} thumb|right|250px|Ko Itub was identified as a Neimoidian previously identified as Yee Moh. Yee Moh first appeared in the 2002 prequel trilogy film Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones. The Outlander Club scenes featuring Moh, who was portrayed by an uncredited extra, were shot on July 17, 2000 at Fox Studios, Sydney.Mythmaking: Behind the Scenes of Attack of the Clones The characters name was first mentioned in an image in the StarWars.com article "Cantina Roll-Call: Shedding Light on Some Alien Aliases," where the name was applied to an image of the character Tey How. The web article was posted on May 2, 2003. In the article "Who's Who in the Outlander Club: It's Ladies' Night," written by Daniel Wallace and published in the seventy-fifth issue of Star Wars Insider on April 6, 2004, the author makes a connection between Moh, his companions, and How. The article also identified the Neimoidian wearing a mouthpiece as Moh. In the 147th issue of Insider, the "Rogues Gallery" department, written by Tim Veekhoven, Kevin Bentjes, and Sander De Lange, identifies Moh as a different Neimoidian without a mouthpiece. A January 19, 2014, post by the Star Wars Sourcebooks Facebook account identifies Ko Itub as the Neimoidian identified as Moh in Wallace's article. This article assumes the "Rogues Gallery" article provides the correct identification of the character, as the article was published on January 21, 2014.
