After his defeat during the Battle of Naboo, the experimental starfighter was appropriated by Maul's master, Darth Sidious. thumb|left|250px|Multi-angle view of Scimitar with wings folded The Sith Infiltrator featured folding wings around its spherical cockpit, and was secretly modified from a Star Courier manufactured by Republic Sienar Systems. It was suspected that the Scimitar was designed by the Sith Lord Darth Tenebrous. The craft featured six laser cannons, a proton torpedo launcher, and a minelayer. Along with its deadly armament, the Scimitar was equipped with a cloaking device, powered by rare stygium crystals from the planet Aeten II,TIE Fighter Owners' Workshop Manual that allowed it to disappear from view and any pursuing ship's sensors. Owing to the craft's experimental ion engines, radiator fins on the ship's wings were required to be open during flight in order to expel excess heat. The Scimitar was heavily-modified with numerous added capabilities, including six laser cannons, spy and surveillance gear, interrogator droids, and a cloaking device. The ship carried three DRK-1 Dark Eye probe droids and the Bloodfin, a Sith speeder utilized by Darth Maul during his mission on Tatooine. The Scimitar was capable of tracking a ship's signature through hyperspace, something considered impossible for other ships. thumb|left|250px|The Scimitar travels to Tatooine. At the time of the Invasion of Naboo, the Scimitar was the personal starship of Darth Maul, who used it in an attempt to track Queen Padmé Amidala on Tatooine. After his defeat, the ship fell into the possession of Maul's master, Darth Sidious, while on its way to be studied at Kuat Drive Yards. During the Clone Wars, the Scimitar was used to transport Sidious and General Grievous to the surface of Dathomir, where the Sith Master had a duel with his resurfaced former apprentice and the Nightsister Mother Talzin. Many years later, the Scimitar was still in the possession of Sidious, who now ruled the First Galactic Empire as Emperor Palpatine. It was held in Imperial Facility 729-D, located somewhere in the Inner Rim, and curated by the droid O-66. At some point before the Battle of Hoth, the bounty hunter Chanath Cha was entrusted with the modified Star Courier in order to retrieve the stolen Imperialis, Palpatine's luxury pleasure craft. thumb|right|250px|O-66 pilots the Scimitar away as the Imperialis explodes. Chanath and O-66 used the Scimitar advanced tracking systems to find the Imperialis location. While O-66 waited aboard the Scimitar, Chanath boarded the Imperialis. O-66 also transmitted the Imperial yacht's operational codes.Lando 4 After Chanath had set the ship to self-destruct, she ordered O-66 to dock the Scimitar so that she, Lando Calrissian, and Lobot could flee the doomed Imperialis. However, O-66 refused to obey her orders because his primary directive was to protect and honor the Scimitar. Despite Chanath's threats, O-66 revealed he had removed her pulse bomb and flew the Scimitar away.Lando 5 The Scimitar first appeared in the 1999 film Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace. De Agostini's Star Wars: Build the Millennium Falcon 44 magazine states that the Scimitar is equipped with a Class 3.0 SSDS 11-A hyperdrive, as was originally the case in the Star Wars Legends continuity,Star Wars: Episode I Incredible Cross-Sections before later being amended to a Class 1.5 hyperdrive by the Star Wars Roleplaying Game The Dark Side Sourcebook.The Dark Side Sourcebook This fact is contradicted by Star Wars Encyclopedia of Starfighters and Other Vehicles, which indicates that the ship has a Class 2 hyperdrive.
