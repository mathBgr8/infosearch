Adriana was a gas giant&ndash;type planet that was located within the Tatoo system of the Outer Rim Territories. A part of the Arkanis sector, which was located in the regions known as the Spice Triangle and the Slice, Adriana lay on the crossroads of the Old Corellian Run and the Triellus Trade Route hyperlanes. The Old Corellian Run connected the planet to the Utaruun and Obana systems, while the Triellus Trade Route linked Adriana to the Andooweel and Ooo-temiuk systems. A smaller hyperspace route known as The Crystal Passage linked the gas giant to the Geonosis system. Adriana occupied the third and outermost orbit around the binary star pair of Tatoo I and Tatoo II. Four lifeless moons circled the gas giant itself. Adriana was also orbited by several rings composed of ice. By the time of the Galactic Civil War, Adriana had never been able to support life. Adriana's rings were often mined for ice, chunks of which were transported to the desert planet Tatooine&mdash;the innermost world of the Tatoo system. The ice was subsequently sold to Tatooine's inhabitants as the planet's main source of water. Adriana was introduced in Galaxy Guide 7: Mos Eisley, a West End Games Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game supplement book that was authored by Martin Wixted and published in 1993. The article "The InQuest World Guide to Tatooine" in the March 1997 twenty-third issue of the InQuest magazine misspelled the planet's name as "Adrianna." Since that article was released outside of the Lucas Licensing process, it was considered non-canonical with respect to the Star Wars Legends continuity. Similarly, the sixteenth issue of De Agostini's The Official Star Wars Fact File magazine, which was written by Iain Lowson and published around April 17, 2002, erroneously spelled the name as "Adiana." That typo was also carried over to the magazine's 2013 and 2014 remake editions that were also written by Lowson.
