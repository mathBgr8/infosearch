Nebula orchid
3170

Nebula orchids were plants that grew on Yavin 4 and could be seen supported by Massassi trees. They had woody, lanky roots and flowers colored with brilliant swirls. When the vines of a nebula orchid were severed, they would writhe and leak the plant's deep purple sap. A short time later, the vines would stop writhing and start to contract. The nebula orchid's showy blossoms measure up to a meter and a half in diameter in full bloom.Galaxy Guide 2: Yavin and Bespin The flower's labellum is most commonly deep magenta with maroon markings, ruffled and recurved into a colorful froth. The sepals are divided into airy filaments in shades of white, rose, and lavender that fringe and curve like tendrils of gas in a nebula, giving the plant its name. When not in bloom, the nebula orchid is recognizable by its glossy green leaves overdappled with spots. Blossoms typically last for 20 days before subsiding and being replaced by another, the show lasting the length of Yavin 4's dry season. During the wet season, seed pods appear. Ripe seed pods measure around half a meter long and 10 centimeters in diameter. The pods are a deep waxy carmine in color. These edible seeds are a frequent food source for land-based grazers in the planet's jungles. The pods yield an edible, spicy paste rich in nutrients and oils and keeps very well in storage. If smashed, an average nebula orchid seed pod yields nearly a liter of flammable oil that burns with a clean blue flame.
