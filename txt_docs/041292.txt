Mantid and her acquaintances Nyrat Agira and Wilst Molan visted the Outlander Club in the Uscru Entertainment District of the planet, seeking out a rich and powerful mate. In 22 BBY, she was in the club at the same time as bounty hunters Jango Fett and Zam Wesell. Shortly after, she was with her acquaintances in the club on the night that Wesell ran into the club trying to escape from the Jedi Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker. Di Mantid was a Human female native to the planetStar Wars: Complete Locations Coruscant who was a connoisseur of fashion and a participant in Coruscant's party scene. Around 22 BBY, states that Di Mantid frequented the Outlander Club some ten years after the Battle of Naboo, which The New Essential Chronology dates to 32 BBY. Therefore, she frequented the club around 22 BBY. Mantid frequented the Outlander Club, a nightclub and gambling hall located in the Uscru Entertainment District of Coruscant. Mantid and her acquaintances Nyrat Agira and Wilst Molan formed a regular group that visited the club in hopes of finding a rich and powerful mate. In 22 BBY,The New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY. The scene from Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter in which Jango Fett is in the Outlander Club takes place immediately before the film, therefore meaning it must be set in that year. At the club, she attempted to surpass the efforts of Agira's look. While her outfit was eye-catching, it was not competition to Agira's. thumb|right|350px|Di Mantid walked past Anakin Skywalker. As the Jedi Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker entered the club, searching for Wesell, who had ran into the club trying to escape from the Jedi, Mantid talked to two Humans near one of the club's turbolifts before walking away from them. While Skywalker looked for Wesell in the club, Mantid walked past him with a drink in hand. As Wesell was being taken out of the club after being disarmed, Mantid stood at the bar of the club, between a Human and a Klatooinian and stared at Skywalker. Mantid then turned to look at the Human. Mantid was acquainted with Nyrat Agira and Wilst Molan. While the three appeared to be friends, they were in fierce competition with each other for the unofficial title of "best dressed Outlander patron," a title Agira often won. Mantid had light skin and brown eyes. Her feminine first name and her surname were of Human origin.Galactic Campaign Guide Mantid usually wore heavy facial makeup, which included blue lipstick and silver rouge. While at the Outlander Club, Mantid wore a sleeveless dress made from wroosha fabric, black gloves, black pants, black shoes, mascara, silver rouge, grey-blue lip paint, and a blonde wig made from Human hair.Star Wars: Attack of the Clones: The Visual Dictionary While the outfit was eye-catching, it was no serious competition to Agira's outfit. thumb|right|200px|Di Mantid appeared in Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter prior to her film appearance. Di Mantid was created for the prequel trilogy film Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones, which was released on May 16, 2002.Star Wars Year by Year: A Visual Chronicle The Outlander Club scenes, where Mantid was portrayed by an uncredited extra, were shot at Fox Studios, Sydney on July 17, 2000.Mythmaking: Behind the Scenes of Attack of the Clones The extra playing Di Mantid, in costume, introduced the fourteenth episode of the Episode II behind-the-scenes video series on location. The episode, titled "From Now On," was released on October 31, 2000. Prior to the film's release, Mantid was identified in the StarWars.com article "Making Up is Hard to Do: Lesley Vanderwalt," which was published on February 15, 2002. The character first appeared in illustrations in the upcoming films' storybook, written by Jane Mason and Sarah Hines Stephens;Star Wars: Attack of the Clones Movie Storybook its young readers book Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter, written by Eric Arnold and illustrated by Valerie Reckert; and the film's junior novelization, written by Patricia C. Wrede. All three books were released on April 23, 2002. The images of her in Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter and the junior novelization were taken from the then-upcoming film. In the April 6, 2004 article "Who's Who in the Outlander Club: It's Ladies' Night," published in the seventy-fifth issue of the Star Wars Insider magazine, author Daniel Wallace described Mantid as hard to glimpse in the film and that the character had enough mascara to rival Tammy Faye Bakker. *Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter *Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones junior novelization *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones Movie Storybook *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones: The Illustrated Companion *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones Scrapbook *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones: The Visual Dictionary *Mythmaking: Behind the Scenes of Attack of the Clones *Star Wars Insider 72 *Star Wars Insider 73 *Star Wars Chronicles: The Prequels *Creating the Worlds of Star Wars: 365 Days *Star Wars: I Want to Be a Jedi *The Complete Star Wars Encyclopedia *Star Wars Character Encyclopedia *Star Wars: The Ultimate Action Figure Collection *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones: The Ultimate Illustrated Guide }} Category:Coruscanti Category:Females Category:Humans Category:Individuals with she/her pronouns
