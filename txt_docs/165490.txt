The name of the Vorpal Demise is the same as the nonsense word vorpal, coined by author Lewis Carroll in the poem "Jabberwocky" from his 1871 novel Through the Looking-Glass, and What Alice Found There. From the context of the poem, vorpal is recognized as an adjective meaning "sharp" and "deadly", often with implication of use for decapitation (the fate of the Jabberwock).
