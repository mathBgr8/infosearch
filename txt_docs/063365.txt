Vob Bitas lived during the reign of the Galactic Republic and was the owner of an Umgullian blob named Blob 14. In 22 BBY, Bitas entered Blob 14 in the third blob race at the world Umgul's Fondine Blob Raceways. Blob 14 was not expected to win, but Bitas introduced prohibited viscosity-enhancing substances into the creature's system, an offense that was punishable by death. Bitas then bet over one million credits on Blob 14, which defied expected odds and won the race. The Umgullian Racing Commission immediately investigated the unlikely victory, and when the banned substances were discovered two hours later, Bitas was taken into custody and executed. Slish Fondine, the owner of the raceways, used Bitas's winnings to recuperate financial losses incurred by the scandal. As Bitas was the fourth cheater caught at the raceways that year, Fondine considered deterring further fraud by instituting a harsher penalty than death. Bitas's victory and execution were soon thereafter reported on by an edition of HoloNet News CoCo District Edition. Vob Bitas was a gambler who was willing to use illegal viscosity-enhancing substances on Blob 14 in order to win a large sum of credits. However, the gambler's untoward ways were revealed by an investigation a mere two hours after the conclusion of the race. thumb|right|200px|Vob Bitas was named after Bob Vitas (pictured). Vob Bitas was first mentioned in "Blob Racing Cheater Killed," an article published in HoloNet News Vol. 531 #48 on the HoloNet News website on March 21, 2002. Though the site content was written by Pablo Hidalgo and Paul Ens, Ens was responsible for the creation of Bitas. The character was named after Bob Vitas, the creator of the fan-made The Completely Unofficial Star Wars Encyclopedia (CUSWE), and he considered it an honor to have been made into a Star Wars character. In 2005, author Abel G. Peña included Bitas as an example in a blog entry titled "How to Become a Star Wars Character in 5 Easy Steps." As Vitas was a notable figure in the Star Wars fan community, Peña humorously asserted that he had been able to skip some of the steps.
