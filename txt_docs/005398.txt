|death= |listedpronouns= |nationality=American |occupation=*Screenwriter *Director *Producer *Actor |companies= |website= }} }} Sofia Coppola (born May 14, 1971) is an American screenwriter, director, producer, and actor who has a cameo as Saché, one of Queen Amidala's handmaidens, in the 1999 film Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace. She is the sister of Roman Coppola, and daughter of director Francis Ford Coppola, a longtime friend of George Lucas. She is known for playing Mary Corleone in the 1990 film The Godfather Part III and for writing and directing titles such as the 1999 drama film The Virgin Suicides, the 2003 comedy-drama Lost in Translation, the 2006 historical drama Marie Antoinette and the 2017 thriller drama The Beguiled. Coppola considers director George Lucas, a long-time friend and working partner of her father Francis Ford Coppola, "like an uncle to me&mdash;he used to send me and my brother Roman Star Wars toys when we were little," and upon hearing he was going back to the saga, "thought it'd be so fun to see how they make them" as she was preparing her own directorial debut The Virgin Suicides, released that same year. When she asked Lucas if she could attend the shoot, he offered her the role of Saché, and she accepted as "it seemed like a good vantage point to watch without getting in the way." *Star Wars: The Skywalker Saga – The Official Collector's Edition Category:Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace actors
