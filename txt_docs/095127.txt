PRP-502 hold-out dart shooter
2773

*25 meters |inscription= |markings= |purpose= |heritage= |affiliation= }} The Protector PRP-502 hold-out shooter, was a small, light, hold-out dart shooter manufactured by Prax Arms. This weapon had a range of only about twenty-five meters, but could be broken down into four pieces for easy concealment and transportation. Manufactured by Prax Arms, the Protector PRP-502 was a typical model of dart shooter. It used a high-tension spring within its design to propel a dart towards a target. The Protector PRP-502 was loaded with a clip of six darts, and a dart could be fired every three seconds. Clips could be changed in seconds using the weapon's sliding release lever. The weapon had a maximum range of twenty-five meters, and an optimum range of ten meters. The Protector PRP-502 was noiseless in its operation, and was exceptionally light, weighing only half a kilogram. It was easily concealable and hard to detect as it had no energy emitters or power cells. Furthermore, the Protector PRP-502 could be broken into four separate pieces, allowing it to be hidden and to avoid being detected by security sensors.
