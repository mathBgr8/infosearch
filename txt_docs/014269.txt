The Bakur Mining Corporation also originated there, and its Cosmic Balance&ndash;practicing executive officers believed that the world's affluence was disruptive. To remedy that state, the Corporation eventually led the settlement of the Wild Space planet Bakura. By more than a century later, the Cosmic Balance had ceased to be practiced on Hemei IV. Hemei IV, also referred to simply as Hemei, was a terrestrial planet that occupied the fourth orbital position in the Hemei system, a part of the Southern Core portion of the Core Worlds. Its ecology mirrored that of Bakura, a lush world of forests, plains, mountains, and oceans located in Wild Space. thumb|right|300px|Hemei IV's ecology was similar to that of its colony planet Bakura (pictured). The exploratory mining consortium Bakur Mining Corporation was at one point formed on Hemei IV. The top executive officers of the corporation, who were adherents of the local Cosmic Balance religion, believed that Hemei IV's concentration of wealth was disruptive to their faith's concept of "Balance." Consequently, in an attempt to remedy the situation, Bakur Mining Corporation scouts searched along the edge of the known galaxy for a star system that could host a self-sufficient colony that could in time become a mirror image of Hemei IV. Eventually, Bakura was determined to be a fitting candidate world, and in 149 BBY,The Truce at Bakura Sourcebook, which is set during the events of The Truce at Bakura and therefore in 4 ABY per The Essential Reader's Companion, dates the settlement of Bakura to 153 years prior to its setting. it was settled by a Bakur Mining Corporation colony shipThe Truce at Bakura Sourcebook of Humans led by Captain Deredith Arden. Bakura's capital city, Salis D'aar eventually featured the site known as the Old Hemei Gardens, with Bakuran historians tracing the colony's origins back to Hemei IV. By the time of the Yuuzhan Vong War of 25 ABY&ndash;29 ABY,The New Essential Chronology the Cosmic Balance had become extinct on Hemei IV. Hemei IV was an affluent world inhabited by Humans that served as the base of operations for the Bakur Mining Corporation. The Cosmic Balance religion emerged on the planet; however, the faith eventually ceased to be practiced there. Hemei IV was first mentioned in Kathy Tyers and Eric S. Trautmann's 1996 The Truce at Bakura Sourcebook of West End Games' Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game.
