Hayde Gofai was a Human female thrill-seeking club-hopper native to the planetStar Wars: Attack of the Clones: The Visual Dictionary Coruscant. She was a participator in Coruscant's party scene. In 22 BBY,The New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY. Gofai visited the Outlander Club, a nightclub and gambling hall in the Uscru Entertainment District of Coruscant. She walked in the club, drinks in hand, with the fellow Human Sne Wo as the Jedi Padawan Anakin Skywalker and his master Obi-Wan Kenobi entered, searching for the bounty hunter Zam Wesell The pair stood near the entrance of the club as she looked at the Padawan. Gofai could not help but notice Skywalker as he surveyed the crowd in the club. Elsewhere in the club, she chatted with Wo before trying her luck with Skywalker. She affixed a bold, seductive gaze to him as he passed by. Unfortunately for her, he was looking for Wesell and not a date. The club-hopper was a thrill seeker who was not shy. Gofai, who was considered beautiful, had brown eyes, light skin and blonde hair. Her feminine first name and her surname were of Human origin.Galactic Campaign Guide She was romantically interested in Anakin Skywalker. Hayde Gofai was created for the prequel trilogy film Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones, which was released on May 16, 2002.Star Wars Year by Year: A Visual Chronicle The Outlander Club scenes including Gofai, who was portrayed by an uncredited Fiona Johnson, were shot at Fox Studios, Sydney on July 17, 2000.Mythmaking: Behind the Scenes of Attack of the Clones The extra introduced the first episode of the Episode II behind-the-scenes video series on location, and then the eighth episode alongside the extra playing Sne Wo and another extra. The first episode, "Production Offices", was released on August 1, 2000. The character first appeared in the first issue of the Star Wars: Episode II — Attack of the Clones comic series, penciled by Jan Duursema and published by Dark Horse ComicsStar Wars: Episode II — Attack of the Clones 1 and released on April 17, 2002. Gofai was incorrectly portrayed with dark hair in the comic.
