Thunder drum
1071

The thunder drum was a large, two-meter-high percussion instrument that required two people to operate it by swinging huge mallets at the drum heads. Traditionally a Weequay instrument, thunder drums were often found in Sriluurian monasteries devoted to Am-Shak, god of thunder.
