In 22 BBY,The New Essential Chronology dates the events of Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones to 22 BBY. The scene from Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter in which Jango Fett is in the Outlander Club takes place shortly before the film's assassination attempt on Senator Padmé Amidala. she was on the planetStar Wars: Attack of the Clones: The Visual Dictionary Coruscant, visiting the Outlander Club, a nightclub and gambling hall in the Uscru Entertainment District of Coruscant. She was in the company of the con artist Dannl Faytonni and the anarchist criminal Reina March. The group was at the club the same time as the bounty hunters Jango Fett and Zam Wesell, who were there planning an assassination on Senator Padmé Amidala. Shortly afterThe New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY. at night, Save was once again in the Outlander Club with Faytonni and March when Wesell ran inside the club to escape from the Jedi Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker after trying to assassinate Amidala. Save drank at the club's bar and talked with Faytonni. When Kenobi made his way to the bar, a Human joined the trio and talked with March as Save watched her. When the Human left, Save turned her attention back to Faytonni, with whom she was holding hands with. A short while later, Save stared when Wesell's arm was cut off by Kenobi in an unsuccessful ambush. As Wesell was being helped up by the Jedi, a smiling Save started talking with Faytonni again. Skywalker then told the clubgoers to go back to their drinks. Save was an enigmatic and mysterious individual who was considered beautiful. She had light skin, dark hair, and brown eyes. It was unclear whether or not she was an associate of the con artist or if she was taken in by his security officer ruse. Unlike other Outlander Club patrons, Save wore a dark, hooded outfit during her visits to the club. thumb|right|300px|The uncredited extra playing Save on set. Whimper Save was created for the prequel trilogy film Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones, which released on May 16, 2002.Star Wars Year by Year: A Visual Chronicle The Outlander Club scenes, where Save was portrayed by an uncredited extra, were shot at Fox Studios, Sydney on July 17, 2000.Mythmaking: Behind the Scenes of Attack of the Clones Prior to the film's release, Save was first pictured in the documentary "A Twinkle Beyond Pluto," released on July 31, 2001. Save then first appeared in illustrations for both the film's storybook, written by Jane Mason and Sarah Hines Stephens,Star Wars: Attack of the Clones Movie Storybook and the young readers book Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter, written by Eric Arnold and illustrated by Valerie Reckert.Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter Both books were published on April 23, 2002. Whimper Save was first identified though the Image Attack feature on StarWars.com on November 26, 2002. In the May 2, 2003, StarWars.com web article "Cantina Roll-Call: Shedding Light on Some Alien Aliases," Save is incorrectly identified as a different Outlander Club patron. In the article "Who's Who in the Outlander Club: It's Ladies' Night," published in the seventy-fifth issue of the Star Wars Insider magazine on April 6, 2004, author Daniel Wallace pokes fun at Save's name, writing that Save beat out Jedi Knight Bultar Swan to win the "Best Name in Episode II" competition. *Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones Movie Storybook *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones Cine-Manga *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones: The Illustrated Companion *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones: The Visual Dictionary *Star Wars: Attack of the Clones Scrapbook *Mythmaking: Behind the Scenes of Attack of the Clones *Dressing a Galaxy: The Costumes of Star Wars *Star Wars Chronicles: The Prequels *Creating the Worlds of Star Wars: 365 Days *Star Wars: The Complete Visual Dictionary *The Complete Star Wars Encyclopedia }} Category:Females Category:Humans Category:Individuals with she/her pronouns
