|length=5.49 meters |wingspan= |mass= |skincolor=Blue |haircolor= |feathers= |eyecolor=Green |distinctions=Paddle-like limbs, transparent eyelids |lifespan= |origin=Utapau |habitat=Freshwater lake, rivers, and sinkholesStar Wars: The Visual Encyclopedia |diet=Carnivore |language= }} Nos monsters were large aquatic creatures that dwelled in the freshwater lakes, underground rivers, and sinkholes of the planet Utapau. The Nos monsters possessed a pair of large front fins that were used to paddle through the waters of their homes and stomp on any prey that wandered into the monsters' hunting grounds. Nos monsters also had large green eyes that helped them see underwater and a tail that they swung as a weapon. left|thumb|Digital model of the nos monster The Nos monster was created for the 2005 film Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith for the scenes on Utapau,The Art of Star Wars Episode III: Revenge of the Sith although they didn't appear in the final film. Concept art of the "Grotto monster" was created by Iain McCaig and Michael Murnane and was presented to Star Wars creator and director George Lucas on April 11, 2003, who approved of the design and requested for it's eyes to be made larger. A digital model of the monster was then created. The Nos monster made it's first appearance in the Legends novelization of Revenge of the Sith by Matthew Stover.Star Wars: Revenge of the Sith novelization They later reappeared in the episode "A Death on Utapau" for the sixth season of the animated series Star Wars: The Clone Wars. Although the show was canceled before the episode aired, it was released as unfinished story reels on StarWars.com in 2014 as part of The Clone Wars Legacy and is still considered canon. The creature's model for the episodes was created by David Le Merrer and finished on March 26, 2012.
