Nardi Shodu was a Rodian female who frequented the Outlander Club, a nightclub and gambling hall in the Uscru Entertainment District of the planet Coruscant. During a night in 22 BBY,The New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY. Shodu and the Human woman Booton Piton approached the crash site of the bounty hunter Zam Wesell's Koro-2 all-environment exodrive airspeederStar Wars: Complete Cross-Sections in the Uscru Entertainment District, shortly after the hunter's assassination attempt on Senator Padmé Amidala. Shortly after, Shodu traveled on an errand to the Outlander Club with Piton. The women entered the club arm in arm just in front of the Jedi Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker, who were there to capture Wesell. Shodu and Piton then walked behind a turboliftStar Wars: Complete Locations in the club. Being a Rodian, her species added to the interplanetary feel of the club that night she visited. Shodu had green skin and black eyes. She had a Rodian first name of interchangeable gender that meant "dramatic." Her surname, which could be used as a first name, meant "gracious" in a feminine context, and "vengeful" in a masculine context.Galactic Campaign Guide On the night she visited the Uscru Entertainment District, Shodu wore a black coat, black pants, and black shoes. }} Nardi Shodu first appeared in the prequel trilogy film Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones, and in its video game adaptation.Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones video game Both released on May 16, 2002.Star Wars Year by Year: A Visual Chronicle The Outlander Club scenes including Shodu, who was portrayed by an uncredited extra, were shot at Fox Studios, Sydney on July 17, 2000.Mythmaking: Behind the Scenes of Attack of the Clones Like most masked extras playing aliens, the extra playing Shodu was guided around by an extra playing a Human. Prior to the film's release, the character was first pictured in the documentary "A Twinkle Beyond Pluto," released on July 31, 2001. In the documentary, the extra playing Shodu comments that they are a different person as the character.
