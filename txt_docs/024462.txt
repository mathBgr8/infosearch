Sling racer
1873

thumb|Sling racers in a rocky canyon. Sling racers were modified airspeeders very similar to podracers, with repulsors only being used to hover above ground. Another similarity was that each pilot had to concentrate more on operating and controlling the individual jets and engines, thus concentrating less on the actual racecourse. The speeders were built from ultra-light alloys and ceramics to be as lightweight as possible, typically 200 kg. Each could have as much as 6 small repulsorlift drive units individually throttled; number of drives depended onthe pilot's status and experience.Shadows of the Empire Planets Guide They were maneuverable with wing and airfoil mechanics, taking advantage of the canyon winds; the pilot adjusted them accordingly to the currents as well as individually throttled the drives. The crudeness and difficulty of maneuvering was part of the challenge. Racers were equipped with ejector seats and maneuvering jets, used only in emergency. Amateur racers were slower, more refined (usually with maneuvering jets), with more protective measures. On Bothawui one could be rented for 15 credits/hour. Two popular models of Sling racer were the SkyFoil Sling Racer (for amateurs) and AirDomination Sling Racer, both of which were manufactured by Bespin Motors. *Shadows of the Empire Planets Guide
