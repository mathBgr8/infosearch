It occurred concurrently with Boba Fett's assassination attempt on Techno Union Foreman Wat Tambor. After retreating from a Republic offensive in the Core and Colonies, the Confederacy of Independent Systems began marshaling the majority of its fleet at its strongholds in the Outer Rim Territories in an attempt to draw in the Republic. Xagobah was one of these fortress worlds, serving as the retreat of Techno Union Foreman Wat Tambor and the location of his Mazariyan citadel. Eager to capture Tambor, the Republic sent a force led by Jedi Master Glynn-Beti to lay siege to Xagobah. Unbeknownst to the Republic, a high-ranking Senate official had placed bounties on the heads of leaders of the Separatists, and Wat Tambor had become the target of the young bounty hunter Boba Fett. The Republic positioned an Acclamator-class assault ship to coordinate the battle from high orbit, while Glynn-Beti and her Padawan Ulu Ulix led the ground assault and starfighter ace Anakin Skywalker provided air support. thumb|left|220px|The Battle of Xagobah Xagobah was one of the most brutal battlefronts of the Clone Wars. The planet's sticky mucus ground hindered movement, while the thick purple-black clouds of fungal spores pumped out of Mazariyan reduced visibility and obscured the battlefield. Wat Tambor used Malvil tree spores to camouflage his vessels. Swarms of Vulture droids hid in these fungal clouds and strafed the clone trench line. The fungal forests surrounding the citadel contained carnivorous Flimmel trees that snatched any beings that wandered too close. The Republic's forces unsuccessfully besieged Mazariyan for weeks. A network of trenches were dug 500 meters away from the fortress. The waves of clones that charged from the trenches were met by the hordes of battle droids that marched out of the citadel's entrance. The few clones that reached the walls of the citadel were repelled by its biological defenses. Javelin-like spines shot out to impale troopers that got too close, while spools of razor-sharp fungal vines blocked the entrance. Very few clones made it inside, but none survived. Master Glynn-Beti tasked a team of ARC troopers with infiltrating the citadel. They were paralyzed by Xabar fungal tentacles, cocooned in the walls, and had their bodily fluids slowly drained to feed the living citadel. When Boba Fett arrived on Xagobah to assassinate Wat Tambor, Master Glynn-Beti launched a large assault against the citadel. Legions of clone troopers, walkers, LAAT/is, and starfighters charged the fortress and bombarded it from dug-in positions. Wat Tambor had hidden the full power of his forces and launched a counterattack during the Republic's push. Thousands of battle droids, droidekas, and heavy vehicles streamed out of Mazariyan to meet the outnumbered clone army and break the siege. The clones managed to hold their own against the seemingly endless swarm of droids, but were unable to advance. In an attempt to eliminate the Republic's air support, Wat Tambor loaded a drone ram ship with explosives under the cover of a fungal cloud and programmed it to ram the Republic's assault ship in orbit. Anticipating such a move, Ulu Ulix took a swoop and raced toward the ramship, but he was shot down by enemy fire. Boba Fett intercepted the falling Ulu and saved the young Jedi's life, bringing him back to Glynn-Beti. As Boba Fett drew closer to the citadel walls in a stolen swoop bike, he became caught up in Tambor's counterattack. A droideka shot him off his swoop and he crashed 200 meters from the citadel. As he charged towards the citadel's entrance, he was forced to defend himself from both clones and droids. At this point, Anakin Skywalker led a squadron of Jedi interceptors in a strike against the fortress, managing to blow a large hole in the structure. A Hailfire droid fired a volley of missiles at Anakin's starfighter, but the experienced pilot was able to evade the missiles and cause them to impact the ramship, triggering its detonation. The energy pulse from the explosion temporarily scrambled the battle droids' circuits, allowing Boba Fett to infiltrate the Mazariyan citadel through the hole carved by Republic fire. Spines and razor vines blocked his path, but he used a Malvil spore-globe to force the fungal defenses to wither and retreat. While the battle continued outside, Boba Fett confronted and attempted to assassinate Wat Tambor, but the Techno Union Foreman was able to escape thanks to the help of General Grievous, who had arrived on Xagobah to extract him. Despite being only 12 years old, the young bounty hunter managed to put up a fight, and narrowly escaped with his life. At some point during the battle, General Grievous cut down the Iron Knight Firkrann in Xagobah's fungal forest. Repeated bombardment of the citadel overwhelmed its ability to repair itself with nanotechs. Large portions of the citadel were blown away, and clone troopers managed to breach the entrance with thermal detonators. While under fire from clone troopers, Boba launched a liquid-cable into a faraway tree and escaped the citadel. Boba Fett left in Slave I and attempted to shoot down Wat Tambor as he escaped in his Harcell-class interstellar trasnport, but he was attacked by a Ginivex-class starfighter (which Boba Fett believed was piloted by Asajj Ventress). Slave I was severely damaged, but repaired by Anakin Skywalker on a nearby asteroid. After this departure, the Siege of Xagobah dragged on for several months, but eventually the Confederacy was forced to surrender the world to the Republic. The Battle of Xagobah in the Boba Fett novel series was identified as the Second Battle of Xagobah in The Clone Wars Campaign Guide.
