MixRMastR
4071

|mass= |gender= |sensor= |plating= |armament= |equipment= |affiliation= |firstmade= |retired= }} The MixRMastR was a series of bartender droid designed and built by Cybot Galactica. It was the most common bartender model on the market, followed closely by LeisureMech Enterprises' C5. Vaguely humanoid in appearance, the MixRMastR was a common sight in taverns located within or near busy starports, where repeat business was valued less than efficient service. MixRMastR bartender droids were just less than one meter in height and designed to be fixed directly onto a bar, table, or countertop. Once in place, tubes ran from the base of the droid into the floor, connecting to containers that were filled with common alcoholic beverages and other liquids such as soda water. A MixRMastR's droid brain had several separate components rather than a single processor. Each had a particular function; one module was an accounting database that tallied customer's bar tabs and kept them up to date. Another part was the drinks database that processed a customer's order. thumb|left|250px|A mobile repulsorlift model of MixRMastr Drink orders were taken by the MixRMastR, and the droid consulted the extensive library of drink recipes programmed into it to draw the appropriate ingredients from the underground vats into an internal mixer before being poured into a glass. The drinks were dispensed through a hatch on the front of the droid, and money was collected in the same manner. In the event that a patron attempted to take the drink without paying, manipulator arms were used to hold the individual until they paid up. The MixRMastR was designed to be affixed to every table throughout a bar, resulting in multiple bartender droids serving the clientele. However, some businesses could not realistically afford to purchase a large number of MixRMastRs. To cater to this need, Cybot Galactica manufactured a mobile variant of the MixRMastR that was equipped with a repulsorlift unit with a three-and-a-half meter flight ceiling to navigate busy bars. Since this version of the droid was not connected to the underground drums of alcohol, the mobile MixRMastR had to carry a supply of drink with them. These supplies had to be replenished on a regular basis.
