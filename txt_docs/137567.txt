Sensing a trap, Zann formulated a plan to get rid of Xizor while keeping the tibanna gas for himself by planting evidence in the computer systems pointing to Black Sun. Not long after the Imperial occupation of Bespin began, the Zann Consortium took on a job from Black Sun to steal tibanna gas from Cloud City in return for the latter party arranging a meeting with Emperor Palpatine, or one of his envoys. Believing it to be a trap, crime lord Tyber Zann planned to leave evidence that would point the Imperial forces' Supreme Commander, Darth Vader, into the Underlord Xizor's direction. Thus, Zann hired the assassin droid IG-88 prior to the raid in order to slice Imperial computers of Bespin central server cluster. When Tyber Zann, his bodyguard Urai Fen and IG-88 landed on a Bespin platform, the trio were greeted by two stormtroopers. Urai sneaked up behind a stormtrooper and killed him, and Tyber shot the other. The Zann Consortium's troops, soon arrived and were given a task. Zann's soldiers killed the stormtrooper guards and destroyed the patrolling AT-ST's as Urai led five collector droids to collect the gas. Meanwhile, Tyber Zann planted evidence at four security outposts would incriminate Xizor, as IG-88A sliced into the Imperial database and gained the information on Tyber's so-called hit list. Shortly after the successful completion of the mission the forces were leaving when Lord Vader himself landed on the Bespin platform, with several Dark Side Adepts standing by. The cyborg then killed the Field Commander for not preventing the theft of the tibanna gas. Knowing his forces were no match for Vader, and that he needed them to find the evidence that framed Xizor, Zann and his forces quickly retreated to the landing zone for pickup. From the evidence planted on the platform the Dark Lord of the Sith was convinced that it was the Black Sun's Underlord who had orchestrated the crime. Tyber Zann made a large amount of credits from selling part of the tibanna gas on the black market, and he kept part of the gas to power his own ships. Eventually Grand Admiral Thrawn contacted Tyber sometime after the battle revealing that while the evidence he planted had convinced Lord Vader of Prince Xizor's guilt, he noticed the unique signature on it. Rather than telling his superior, the Chiss commander allowed the Falleen gangster to be killed. It is possible to kill Darth Vader and his Adepts after he lands by simply swarming the area in front of the landing zone with units and targeting them. Early in the mission, a reference to a scene in Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope is made when a stormtrooper asks for Zann's identification, to which Zann responds that the stormtrooper "doesn't need to see" his identification. The stormtrooper then says "this one thinks he's a Jedi". The player may also bring the heroes Bossk and Silri to the mission, though they are not required.
