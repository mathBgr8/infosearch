*April 28, 2015 *November 17, 2015 |closed= |upc= |engine= |genre=Racing game |modes=Single player, Multiplayer |ratings=ESRB: Everyone (E) |platforms=PlayStation 2, PlayStation 3, PlayStation 4 |series= |basegame= |expansions= |director= |producer= |designer= |programmer= |artist= |writer= |composer= |timeline=24 BBYThe New Essential Chronology |prev=Star Wars: Episode I Racer |next= }} Star Wars: Racer Revenge is the sequel to the podracing game Star Wars: Episode I Racer. The game takes place in 24 BBY and focuses on Sebulba getting his revenge on Anakin Skywalker. The game features 13 tracks with 22 pilots in all. There are some changes from the original game, such as refinements on the ability to knock out other pilots. There are also five hidden characters in the game. It was was originally released on February 13, 2002 for the PlayStation 2. It was made available to play via PlayStation Network 13 years later, first for the PlayStation 3 on April 28, 2015 and later the PlayStation 4 on November 17, 2015. Racer Revenge features seven new podracers not appearing in the previous game: Knire Dark, Shrivel Braittrand, Occo Ninebar, Kraid Nemmeso, Wan Sandage Jr., "Scorch" Zanales and Tzidik Wrantojo. Some podracers from The Phantom Menace were left out of Racer Revenge. These podracers are Ratts Tyerell, Neva Kee, Elan Mak, Ark "Bumpy" Roose, Ebe Endocott, and Boles Roor. Neva Kee was featured in many images of early versions of the game. None of the secret characters who appeared in Star Wars: Episode I Racer are present, and Tatooine is the only world to be featured in both games.
