It was connected to the Kegan and Norval systems via the hyperlane known as the Triellus Trade Route. During the Galactic Civil War, a base of the Galactic Empire's Navy was located on Gestrex III. The guard droid Armx&mdash;in reality an espionage unit for Imperial Intelligence&mdash;was assigned to the base at one point. However, sometime between 2 BBY and 1 BBY,Galaxy Guide 10: Bounty Hunters is set between the events of and , which corresponds to the time period between 0 ABY and 4 ABY per The New Essential Chronology. The former book further specifies that Armx became a bounty hunter at least five years prior and following the establishment of the Rebel Alliance, which The New Essential Chronology dates to 2 BBY. Armx must have therefore become a bounty hunter sometime between 2 BBY and 1 BBY. only three days after the droid's posting on Gestrex III, the facility was decimated by a team of Rebel Alliance commandos. The base was subsequently abandoned, with only Armx remaining amidst its wreckage. The droid underwent an alteration in its original programming&mdash;whether due to radiation leakage or damage sustained during the raid&mdash;over the following days and eventually became a bounty hunter. Gestrex III was introduced in the 1994 sourcebook Galaxy Guide 10: Bounty Hunters. The book was authored by Rick D. Stuart and published by West End Games for use with Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game.
