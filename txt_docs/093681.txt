|class=Cephalopod |subspecies= |races= |height= |length= |wingspan= |mass= |skincolor=Reddish-brown with black stripes |haircolor= |feathers= |eyecolor=Hazel |distinctions=*One eye on a single eyestalk *Mouth at base of eyestalk with small appendages on sides *Anterior flipper-like appendages *Long, flexible tail |lifespan= |origin= |habitat= |diet= |language= }} A sentient cephalopod species existed that had one eye mounted on a single eyestalk and a mouth with appendages on both sides at the base of their eyestalk. The species had anterior flipper-like appendages and a long, flexible tail. At least one member had reddish-brown skin with black stripes and a hazel eye. They were similar to the dianoga, a species native to the planet Vodran. The species' culture controlled their environment through terraforming abilities. Niado Duegad was a Force-attuned member of the species who hailed from the dianoga's homeworld. During the Galactic Civil War, Duegad worked as a mercenary, and his enhanced adrenal glands allowed for short bursts of incredible strength. The species originated as one of several alien maquettes created for possible inclusion in the scenes taking place in Jabba the Hutt's desert palace during the production stages of 1983's original trilogy film, Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi. Known as "Alien 18," the maquette was among the ones scrapped before the film's release as its non-humanoid nature made it too difficult to realize as a puppet.Star Wars Chronicles In 1998, the Special Edition Limited expansion set of Decipher's Star Wars Customizable Card Game made a card for Niado Duegad, showing an image of the maquette and thus bringing the species into the Star Wars Legends continuity. *Star Wars Chronicles Category:Cephalopod sentient species
