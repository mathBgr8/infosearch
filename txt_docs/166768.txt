*Heather Antos |publisher=Marvel Comics |release date=March 1, 2017 |pages=32 |upc= |timeline=*c. 40 BBY Star Wars: Timelines *0 ABY The frame story seen in Star Wars (2015) 26-30 occurs immediately after the events of Star Wars (2015) 25, which Star Wars: Timelines dates to 0 ABY. |series=Star Wars (2015) |issue=29 }} Star Wars 29 is the twenty-ninth issue of the Marvel comic book series Star Wars. The issue is written by Jason Aaron, with art by Salvador Larroca. It is the fourth issue in a story arc set prior to Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace that features the character Yoda. The story was published on March 1, 2017. *A problem, Yoda has. A big problem.' *Strong with the stonepower, this problem is.' *But judge it not by its size, will he.' Yoda's journey within the mountain deepens as he realizes the "heart of the mountain" is actually a living creature. He suspects the blue stones are not merely pieces of a mountain but are part of a barely-living being. He spends nine days meditating and sharing his life force with the stone, eventually hearing a faint voice that grows louder each day until words pour out like an avalanche. Yoda learns the mountain was once a giant of living stone that protected the world until people, driven by greed and fear, butchered its kind and drove them into the ground. Only one giant remained, which became the last mountain. Yoda and Garro attempt to leave the mountain, but Garro hesitates, still feeling banished by his people for his perceived cowardice. Yoda encourages him, and they descend together, surprising everyone. When others demand to know the mountain's secret, Yoda refuses, stating they are unworthy. Just as they are about to attack, the mountain moves, and Garro explains it is a living thing. Garro's tribe returns, telling Yoda to leave. Yoda then returns to the other tribe of children and instructs them to go to war. He teaches them to commune with the mountain, but it only seems to feel anger, hatred, greed, and thirst for revenge. The children notice that the mountain appears closer than before. Once again, the Jedi Master sets out alone, sensing his work is far from done. He tells the mountain to fight against the hatred and anger controlling it. The mountain, now in the shape of a massive humanoid, scoffs at Yoda, calling him less than a bug. Yoda stands his ground, reminding the creature that in the Force, all life is sacred and equal. The mountain laughs, its laughter shaking the ground, but Yoda does not move. Meanwhile, Luke pilots his X-wing to the Vagadarr system. He must navigate a dangerous asteroid field to reach the planet, where he hopes to find the "blue mountain" from the story. Upon landing, he sees a giant stone hand buried in the sand. A hooded figure emerges from beneath it, introducing himself as an adult Garro who has been waiting for another Jedi to help him finish the war.
