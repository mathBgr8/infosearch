|death=March 27, 2004 |listedpronouns= |nationality=British |occupation=*Actor *Stunt Coordinator |companies= |website= }} Peter Diamond (August 10, 1920s#1929 &ndash; March 27, 2004) was the stunt coordinator on Episodes IV and V, and assisted with stunts on Episode VI. He created the lightsaber combat styles of Djem So and Soresu for Luke Skywalker, Darth Vader and Obi-Wan Kenobi. thumb|left|210x210px|Peter Diamond, during the filming of A New Hope. He also played A'Koba, Garouf Lafoe, a stormtrooper, a Death Star Trooper named Corporal Prescott in the Legends continuity, a snowtrooper, a scout trooper, a Weequay skiff master, and a rebel commando in a subsequently deleted scene. thumb|right|210x210px|Peter Diamond, With [[Anthony Daniels in as a Light Bulb Creature in Return of the Jedi.]] Additionally, he wore a black suit covered with lightbulbs inside Jabba's Palace that was to be transformed into a creature in post production, however the idea was scrapped and never fully realized.I Am C-3PO: The Inside Story thumb|left|210x210px|Peter Diamond at the [[Celebration II|Star Wars Celebration II.]] Unfortunately in Indianapolis during the Star Wars Celebration II Peter Diamond is shown holding a custom commemorative plaque presented to him by the 501st Legion to honor his induction as an Honorary Member. Diamond, who famously portrayed numerous characters including the Tusken Raider that attacked Luke Skywalker and the "cantina snitch" Garouf Lafoe, was recognized at the event for his extensive contributions as the "original Stormtrooper" and stunt master. He was among the people interviewed for the documentary Empire of Dreams: The Story of the Star Wars Trilogy, which was released six months after his death. {| ! Year !! Title !! Contribution(s) !!
