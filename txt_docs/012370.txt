A successor of the Galactic Empire, it was largely isolationist, with the New Republic for the most part ignoring the Alignment. However, in 12 ABY, the Pentastar Alignment was annexed into the Imperial Remnant, another successor state of the Old Empire. The Pentastar Alignment was formed from a splinter faction of the Galactic Empire led by Ardus Kaine in what was the "Velcar Free Commerce Zone" in 4 ABY after the Pentastar Talks. Seceding from the collapsing Empire, the Pentastar Alignment sought not to join the newly-formed New Republic and be left alone without interference from it. In 8 ABY, the Pentastar Alignment contributed some forces to Grand Admiral Thrawn, but did not formally join him during his campaign in 9 ABY. One year later, the resurrected Emperor Palpatine began to reunite various Imperial splinter factions and rump states, as part of pre-Operation Shadow Hand, in an effort to topple the New Republic. The Pentastar Alignment allied with Palpatine's resurrected clone. However, Kaine was killed by the Republic during the campaign over the planet Palanhi, leading to it being weaken and finally absorbed into the Imperial Remnant. The Inquisitor Jerec served as a major figure within the Pentastar Alignment. thumb|left|120px|[[Grand Moff/Legends|Grand Moff Ardus Kaine founded the Pentastar Alignment.]] After the death of Emperor Palpatine during the Battle of Endor in 4 ABY, the Galactic Empire fragmented into many pieces. Instead of trying to keep control of the complete Outer Rim, Grand Moff Ardus Kaine, who had earlier replaced Grand Moff Tarkin at the head of the Oversector Outer, concentrated his forces in fourteen sectors of the New Territories. Kaine used his personal Executor-class Star Dreadnought, the Reaper, for some none-too-subtle intimidation as a venue to hold talks with representatives of multiple star systems and five major Imperial corporations. The talks were successful, and the Pentastar Alignment was founded in 4 ABY. thumb|right|250px|The [[Reaper (Executor-class)|Reaper leading the Pentastar forces.]] In 8 ABY, Grand Admiral Thrawn returned from the Unknown Regions in a last-ditch attempt to reunite and consolidate the faltering Empire. The Alignment contributed some forces to Thrawn's efforts but did not formally join him, preferring to continue its militarization in anticipation of conflict with the New Republic. One year later, the resurrected Emperor Palpatine began to reunite various Imperial splinter factions as part of Operation Shadow Hand, an effort to topple the New Republic. The Emperor was more successful than Thrawn in recruiting Kaine, and the Pentastar Alignment forces eventually attacked the New Republic. Joining Thrawn's remnant forces, Ardus Kaine made a successful campaign against the New Republic forces, winning a dozen victories until penetrating the Core Worlds. However, the conflict was a failure, with Kaine killed and Palpatine defeated for the last time. The Pentastar Alignment was annexed by Admiral Gilad Pellaeon in 12 ABY, becoming part of the Imperial Remnant. The Reaper became Pellaeon's flagship until its destruction at the Battle of Celanon in 13 ABY. Before 19 ABY, Kaine's homeworld Sartinaynian&mdash;renamed Bastion&mdash;became the Imperial Remnant's capital, remaining thus for over a century.Legacy Era Campaign Guide The Pentastar Alignment was a corporatist dictatorship that was administratively split in two: "Order" and "Enforcement." Order comprised the Politorate, Insurrection, Judgment, and Protectorate branches. The Alignment utilized the services of several former Inquisitors in its Judgment branch. They were known as the Great InQuestors of Judgment and included the Dark Jedi Jerec among their roster. The Pentastar Patrol was the primary military of the Alignment, which would design, along with Kuat Drive Yards and Sienar Fleet Systems, the Enforcer-class picket cruiser). The Alignment largely maintained an isolationist position, and sought to be on peaceful terms with all parties. This lead to the New Republic reciprocating and largely ignoring the Alignment; although pirates and mercenaries such as the Red Moons continued to harry the Alignment.
