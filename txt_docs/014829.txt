It lay along the Rago Run hyperlane, which connected it to the Moro and Gilatter systems. Sinton was populated by non-Humans, and at some point by 3 ABY,The Subjugation of Sinton must have occurred at some point by the events of Star Wars: Episode V The Empire Strikes Back, as evidenced by , which is set during the film's events. The New Essential Chronology dates the events of The Empire Strikes Back to 3 ABY. the planet was subjugated by the Galactic Empire, which subjected the planet's inhabitants to slave labor. By 12 ABY,The Essential Reader's Companion dates the events of Darksaber, which includes Sulamar claiming to have participated in the Subjugation of Sinton, to 12 ABY. Sulamar, a fraudulently self-titled Imperial general, claimed to have participated in the Subjugation of Sinton, among many other purported military conquestsDarksaber&mdash;some nonexistent. In reality, Sulamar was a low-level Imperial technician with a failed military career. By that same year, Sinton lay within the borders of the New Republic. Sinton was first mentioned in the 1995 novel Darksaber, written by Kevin J. Anderson, in which the character Imperial General Sulamar alleges to have played a part in the Subjugation of Sinton. Sulamar's claim is later called into doubt once he is exposed as a fraud. Both the planet and its subjugation were subsequently elaborated upon through the 1997 Dagobah Limited expansion set of the Star Wars Customizable Card Game, thus proving Sinton's in-universe existence.
