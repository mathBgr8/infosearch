Kell Borean was a Human female native to the planetStar Wars Chronicles: The Prequels Coruscant. A participant in her homeworld's party scene, Borean began to frequent the Outlander Club, a nightclub and gambling hall located in the Uscru Entertainment District of Coruscant, prior to the start of the Clone Wars, a conflict between the Galactic Republic and the Confederacy of Independent Systems that started in 22 BBY. Though Borean was not an employee of the club, she seemed to be a permanent fixture around its gambling tables. During a night in 22 BBY,The New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY. Borean stood next to the Human Eos Morne near the Outlander Club's entrance as the Jedi Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker walked in searching for the bounty hunter Zam Wesell after her assassination attempt on Senator Padmé Amidala. Later, she was at a gaming table surrounded by several Humans. When Skywalker walked by the table, Borean and the other Humans clapped as she gave an elated cheer to a big payout from the house. Borean had black hair worn in a long braid, brown eyes, and light skin. While at the Outlander Club, Borean wore a light blue top, loose see-through light blue pants worn over shorts, white boots, and a large white and black hat, which possibly gave her luck. thumb|right|250px|Australian actor Eliana Dona portrayed Kell Borean. Kell Borean first appeared in the prequel trilogy film Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones, released on May 16, 2002.Star Wars Year by Year: A Visual Chronicle In the film, Borean was portrayed by Australian actor and make-up artist Eliana Dona, though she was left uncredited for her work. The Outlander Club scenes including Dona were shot at Fox Studios, Sydney on July 17, 2000.Mythmaking: Behind the Scenes of Attack of the Clones Prior to the film's release, the character was first pictured in the documentary "A Twinkle Beyond Pluto," released on July 31, 2001, and was identified in the StarWars.com article "Making Up is Hard to Do: Lesley Vanderwalt", which was published on February 15, 2002.
