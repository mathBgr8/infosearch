|genre= |issues= }} Star Wars Insider is the official Star Wars magazine and traces its lineage back to the Official Star Wars Fan Club Newsletter and its successor, Bantha Tracks. In 1994, the Lucasfilm Fan Club Magazine was rebranded the Star Wars Insider to coincide with the release of the Star Wars: TIE Fighter video game from LucasArts. Issues 62&ndash;76 were published by Paizo Publishing, from October 2002 to May 2004. Issues 77&ndash;92 were published by IDG Entertainment, from August 2004 to February 2007. {| | Issues 1&ndash;22 | The Lucasfilm Fan Club Magazine | Hyperspace |- | Issues 23&ndash;50 | Star Wars Insider | Hyperspace |- | Issues 51&ndash;61 | Star Wars Insider | Wizards of the Coast |- | Issues 62&ndash;76 | Star Wars Insider | Paizo Publishing |- | Issues 77&ndash;92 | Star Wars Insider | IDG Entertainment |- | Issue 93&ndash;237 | Star Wars Insider | Titan Magazines |} Issues 107 to 174 have been edited by Jonathan Wilkins. At issue 175, Will Salmon took over as editor, and was in turn, succeeded by Christopher Cooper. The North American system of numbering issues differs significantly from its UK counterpart but aligned the numbering from Issue 150 onwards. There has also been a Star Wars Insider in France since April 21, 2015. The magazine contains sections that focus on what is new in the Star Wars universe, be they events, fiction, excerpts from comics, exclusive previews, articles that explore the Star Wars universe in detail, questions and answers, or interviews. A popular section of the magazine is Bantha Tracks, overseen by Lucasfilm's Pete Vilmur, who replaced the section's previous editor Mary Franklin in mid-2015. It is created entirely by fans of Star Wars, with art and photographs submitted by fans.
