It's a Trap
7822

"It's a Trap" is a comic strip written by Archie Goodwin and illustrated by Al Williamson. It was published by the Los Angeles Times Syndicate from November 1982 to January 1983. It has also been posted online on Hyperspace. The comic documents several events, including Admiral Gial Ackbar and the Mon Calamari joining the Rebel Alliance and Darth Vader taking command of his recently built Super Star Destroyer, Executor. The story introduced the character of Admiral Ackbar and the Mon Calamari species. At the time the strip was created, Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi was still in production. The story ended about four months before the film's release.Star Wars Year By Year: A Visual History, Updated and Expanded Edition 250px|thumb|"Iceworld" panel teasing the story Though the story originally had no official title, fans began calling it "Revenge of the Jedi" based on a misreading of the teaser for the story printed in the comic strip's previous installment. That text was only making reference to the fact that Ackbar was in that upcoming film. Revenge of the Jedi was a working title of the third installment of the original Star Wars trilogy, used very late into the promotion of the film.Star Wars: The Classic Newspaper Comics Vol. 3 When the story was reprinted in Star Wars: The Classic Newspaper Comics Vol. 3 in 2018, it was finally named "It's a Trap," in honor of Admiral Ackbar's famous line in Return of the Jedi. Though months later in 2019, Star Wars Legends Epic Collection: The Newspaper Strips Vol. 2 did refer to the story simply as "Untitled."Star Wars Legends Epic Collection: The Newspaper Strips Vol. 2
