C. Crispin. The trilogy includes the books The Paradise Snare (1997), The Hutt Gambit (1997), and Rebel Dawn (1998), which tell the backstory of Han Solo from his early years right up to the moment he walks into the cantina during the events of the film Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope. *The Paradise Snare , May 5, 1997 *The Hutt Gambit , August 11, 1997The Hutt Gambit *Rebel Dawn , March 9, 1998 During the development of Star Wars: The Han Solo Trilogy, author A. C. Crispin was requested and instructed by Lucasfilm to not cover Han Solo's time at the Imperial Academy or his first encounter with Chewbacca. Despite this, she was allowed to write a brief explanation of how Han had saved Chewie's life when he was enslaved by the Empire, and had gotten cashiered for his pains with a life debt. Crispin was also faced with the challenge of trying to explain how Han discovered his cousin, Thracken Sal Solo, without ever knowing anything about his parents, even their names. The three books were originally released individually. They were later rereleased together as a Science Fiction Book Club edition by the title The Han Solo Trilogy'The Han Solo Trilogy hardcover and as an audiobook by the title The Han Solo Omnibus.The Han Solo Omnibus abridged audiobook collection The Sci-Fi Book Club edition is missing the epilogue of The Hutt Gambit. Crispin became known for creating fascinating "backstory" for media icons, such as Han Solo in The Han Solo Trilogy as well as Mr. Spock's father Sarek in Star Trek: Sarek. When an editor working for Disney was seeking an author to write a novel dealing with the backstory of Captain Jack Sparrow in Pirates of the Caribbean, they contacted Crispin's agent and contracted her to write Pirates of the Caribbean: The Price of Freedom after reading The Han Solo Trilogy. When releasing the fifth excerpt, Crispin spoke how she was privileged to write the scene where Han Solo first beheld&mdash;and fell for&mdash;the Millennium Falcon in The Han Solo Trilogy. She assured fans it was every bit as much of a thrill to write the scene with Jack Sparrow and the Wicked Wench, the East India Trading Company merchant vessel that became the pirate ship Black Pearl. A Barnes & Noble Collectible Edition leather-bound hardcover was released on October 20, 2017. Category:Legends novel series
