The Last One Standing
5311

"The Last One Standing" is a short story written by Jude Watson and appearing in the Legacy of the Jedi/Secrets of the Jedi omnibus. Obi-Wan Kenobi is contemplating the death of Anakin Skywalker, though still feeling as if he were alive in some way. Later, Obi-Wan is monitoring the Lars homestead to protect the infant Luke Skywalker. However, when Owen and Beru Lars discover that the Sand People have stolen twenty of their moisture vaporators, Owen decides to gather together a group of fellow moisture farmers to battle the fearsome Sand People. Obi-Wan, not wanting Luke's protective "father"-figure to die as he surely would, decides to track the Sand People on his own. He eventually tracks them down by following their tracks, while contemplating his memories of Anakin, and then a group of uli-ah back to their camp. Things go well there for a while: he finds the vaporators in the Sand People's tents and loads them onto a bantha. However, as he's about to leave, he is spotted by a lone Tusken Raider, who attacks him. Though Obi-Wan disarms him easily, the Raider then howls, which alerts all the other males in the camp that they have an intruder. They all attack him, and Obi-Wan slowly starts to hate them, causing him to slip to the dark side slightly before he hears the voice of Qui-Gon Jinn, which brings him back to the light. Instead of murdering the entire clan as Anakin had done in the past, Obi-Wan cuts their clothes off to reveal their skin&mdash;a taboo to the Sand People, which would usually result in the others of their tribe murdering them. This gives him time to escape on the bantha with the vaporators and causes him to become a legend for the Sand People.
