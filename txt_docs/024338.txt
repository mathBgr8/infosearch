Calonica was a terrestrial astronomical object located in the Calonica system, a part of the Vensensor sector in the Western Reaches portion of the Expansion Region. It was situated on the Corellian Trade Spine super-hyperroute, which connected it to the Tar Morden system as well as the Bomis Koori system of the Mid Rim. During the Galactic Civil War, the Galactic Empire had a presence on Calonica, with an Imperial capital there having fifty thousand Merr-Sonn Munitions LX-2 comlink mines buried around it. At some point, Rebel Alliance spies managed to remotely deactivate the mines, after which Rebels dug them up, stole the explosive charges from more than ninety percent of the mines, and then re-buried them. A spot inspection subsequently discovered the lack of charges, which eventually led to the development of the LX-4 proton mine that could only be deactivated by detonating it. Shortly after the Battle of Yavin, the incident on Calonica was mentioned in a compilation of Imperial documents made by Rebel historian Major Arhul Hextrophon for Alliance Supreme Commander Mon Mothma and other Rebel officers. thumb|right|250px|The article "Twinkle, Twinkle Little Star, How I Wonder Where We Are" placed Calonica in the Tau Sekar sector. Calonica was first mentioned in Greg Gorden's 1989 Imperial Sourcebook that was published by West End Games for use with Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game.Imperial Sourcebook "Twinkle, Twinkle Little Star, How I Wonder Where We Are," a 1990 roleplaying game source article published in the thirteenth issue of the Voyages SF magazine, placed Calonica in the Tau Sekar sector. The article is considered noncanonical with respect to the Star Wars Legends continuity.
