|death= |listedpronouns= |nationality=American |occupation=Author |companies= |website= }} Marina Fitch (born April 11, 1957) is an American author of Science Fiction and Fantasy. She wrote "Let Us Prey: The Whiphid's Tale" in collaboration with Mark Budz as part of Tales from Jabba's Palace, which was published by Bantam Spectra in January 1996. Fitch graduated from the University of California, Santa Cruz in 1981, and her first short story was published in L. Ron Hubbard's Writers and Illustrators of the Future in 1986. Not long afterward, she briefly worked for Kristine Kathryn Rusch at Pulphouse Publishing. Fitch has written such novels as The Seventh Heart and The Border. Category:Short story authors
