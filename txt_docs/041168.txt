Immi Danoo was a Human female native to the planetStar Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones junior novelization Coruscant who participated in her homeworld's party scene. She club-hopped with her friends Nyrat Agira and Rosha Vess, who were members of the religious cult Zealots of Psusan. In 22 BBY,The New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY. The scene from Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter in which Jango Fett is in the Outlander Club takes place immediately before the film, therefore meaning it must be set in that year. Danoo and her friends were inside the Outlander Club,Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter a nightclub and gambling hall located in the Uscru Entertainment District of Coruscant. They visited at the same time as Jango Fett and Zam Wesell, two bounty hunters who were preparing to assassinate Senator Padmé Amidala. thumb|right|300px|Danoo with Agira and Vess inside the Outlander Club. During a night shortly after,The New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY. Danoo, who was an eligible bachelorette, was once again in the club with her friends. The group drank and talked with another Human near the club's bar when two Jedi&mdash;Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker&mdash;entered, searching for Zam Wesell after she failed to kill Amidala. The trio's fleeting attention was drawn by Kenobi as he cut off Wesell's arm when she attempted to ambush him, turning to stare as he did so. Skywalker told the clubgoers to go back to their drinks and the Jedi left with Wesell. Danoo was friends with Nyrat Agira and Rosha Vess. The bachelorette was stylish and ditzy, had light skin, blonde hair, and was tall. Danoo was considered beautiful and otherworldly. On the night she visited the Outlander Club in 22 BBY,The New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY. she had on an eye-catching but impractical outfit consisting of a purple top with puffy sleeves, an ankle-length cape, a white belt, light purple shorts, and black heels. thumb|right|250px|Concept art of Immi Danoo was done by Dermot Power. Immi Danoo was created for the prequel trilogy film Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones, which was released on May 16, 2002.Star Wars Year by Year: A Visual Chronicle The character was created by artist Dermot Power in concept art. The Outlander Club scenes including Danoo, who was portrayed by an uncredited extra, were shot at Fox Studios, Sydney on July 17, 2000.Mythmaking: Behind the Scenes of Attack of the Clones Danoo was first revealed on March 10, 2002 with the release of the trailer for Episode II. Danoo then first appeared in illustrations in both the young readers book Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter, written by Eric Arnold and illustrated by Valerie Reckert, and in the film's junior novelization, written by Patricia C. Wrede. Both released on April 23, 2002 with the images of her in the books being taken from the then upcoming film. For the expansion pack to the Ensemble Studios video game Star Wars: Galactic Battlegrounds, Star Wars: Galactic Battlegrounds: Clone Campaigns,Star Wars: Galactic Battlegrounds: Clone Campaigns photos of Danoo and the character Dyemma Reel were used to create the character Naat Reath. The expansion pack released on May 15, 2002. Concept art of Danoo was erroneously used in the "Behind the Scenes" section in the StarWars.com Databank entry for Nyrat Agira, published on June 14, 2002. The character was then first given a name on October 25, 2002, through the Image Attack feature on StarWars.com. In the April 6, 2004, article "Who's Who in the Outlander Club: It's Ladies' Night," published in the seventy-fifth issue of the Star Wars Insider magazine, author Daniel Wallace describes Danoo and her friends as the "intergalactic embodiment of the Valley Girl stereotype."
