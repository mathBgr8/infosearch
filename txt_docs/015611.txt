Alpha (Advanced Recon Commando)

|birth= |death= |species=Human |gender=Male |pronouns=He/him |donor=Jango Fett |height=1.83 meters |mass= |hair=Black |feathers= |eyes=Brown |skin= |cyber= |families= |parents= |partners= |siblings= |children= |affiliation=*Galactic Republic **Squad Seven |masters= |apprentices= }} Alpha&mdash;a name that came from his designation Alpha-17, which in turn could be shortened to A-17&mdash;was an Alpha-class ARC trooper in the Republic's clone army during the Clone Wars. thumb|left|180px|Alpha-17 during the First Battle of Kamino. Born and bred on Kamino as a genetic copy of the infamous bounty-hunter Jango Fett, a Mandalorian, Alpha-17 was among a grouping of 100 individual clones of Fett that comprised a line of "Alpha" ARC troopers (Advanced Recon Commandos), personally chosen by Fett to be trained by him for the toughest missions that were to be outside the parameters of the regular clone soldier; they were a successor to the Null-class, the initial batch of clones produced whom the Kaminoans ultimately deemed unstable and slated for termination. As a result, the Alpha ARCs were to have personalized equipment and they retained part of the personality and independence of Jango in comparison to the rank-and-file, set to take positions of command in what was to be the Grand Army of the Republic, and to act indepently when needed, by their own initiative. Fearing them, however, the Kaminoans ultimately had the Alpha ARCs placed in stasis; sometime before this, with the growing of the Separatist crisis, the ARC troopers were all personally given an emergency contingency by Jango that in such event the Separatists ever successfully captured Kamino itself, the cloning facilities were to be set to self-destruct and kill all the succeeding generations of clones, to prevent their use by the enemy. Alpha, as was the case with the rest of the ARCs, first saw action with the Separatist invasion of Kamino. As the Confederacy's droid forces pushed towards the heart of Tipoca City, the Prime Minister of Kamino, Lama Su, activated the ARCs and authorized their deployment to hot-spots across Kamino in a last-ditch effort to save the planet's precious cloning facilities. During the fighting to push the droids from Tipoca's central armory, Alpha fought to save three young clones captured by some separatist droids, along with two other ARC clones. However, due to the attack of a Droideka, only A-17 survived. After being rescued by Jedi Master Shaak Ti, he teamed up with her and Jedi Knight Obi-Wan Kenobi, and Kenobi's Padawan, Anakin Skywalker. When the city's clone stocks became compromised, Alpha, following with the contingency Jango had given, rigged the lab to self-destruct and terminate all the remaining clones, much to the dismay of the Jedi. After remarking, "They grow up loyal to the Republic, or they don't grow up at all," it was clear to the Jedi that they were not dealing with an average clone trooper. Ti wished there was another way, so Alpha made a strategy for Obi-Wan and Anakin to collapse the transparisteel walls linking the facility while Ti held back the ocean before they sealed the place off. When the plan worked and the attack was successfully repelled, Alpha then aborted the detonation sequence. When Naboo lost contact with the Gungan colony moon of Ohma-D'un, a Separatist attack was suspected, and General Kenobi and Skywalker were sent to investigate. Kenobi, having witnessed his lethal effectiveness on Kamino, decided to have Alpha join for the operation. When the trio arrived on the surface, they found the Gungan colony in ruins and bodies for as far as the eye could see. Before they could complete their investigation into the cause of the genocidal slaughter, however, they fell under attack from the bounty hunter Durge and the Dark Jedi Asajj Ventress. Under Kenobi's orders, Alpha went with Skywalker to find and destroy the bio-weapon responsible for Ohma-D'un's destruction before it could be let loose in Theed. It was then that Skywalker, uncomfortable with calling him simply "clone," after the ARC told him his designation, named the trooper "Alpha". Both not only managed to blow the ships, but also to locate Obi-Wan in time and save him, forcing Ventress to flee. thumb|180px|Alpha on Jabiim Following the mission to Ohma-D'un, Alpha was once again chosen by Kenobi and Anakin to accompany them on another important mission. This time, the three were to lead the assault on the obscure Outer Rim world of Jabiim, which had recently declared its allegiance to Count Dooku's Confederacy. The initial days of the assault saw victory after victory for the Republic, but, as the rain continued to pour, their assault vehicles became bogged down in the mud and became an easy target for the Jabiimi guerrillas. As Republic casualties mounted, the rebels, their forces now bolstered with the addition of Separatist battle droids, stepped up their attacks. The Republic was dealt its greatest blow with the apparent death of Obi-Wan and Alpha while they were clearing out a downed All Terrain Armored Transport. thumb|left|200px|Alpha is tortured on Rattatak by Asajj Ventress. Neither Obi-Wan nor Alpha, however, died in the explosion that supposedly took their lives. In truth, Alpha had managed to save Obi-Wan, and later, both fell into Separatist custody and were transferred to a secret prison on Rattatak. Once there, Asajj Ventress, the dark Jedi from Ohma-D'un, tried to beat and torture the two of them into revealing sensitive information regarding Republic troop movements, especially Alpha, but he would only tell her his identification, to her frustration. Before she got the information she wanted, however, she threw him at Obi-Wan's cell. There, the two of them, working together, managed to escape under the guise of a prison break. Having fought their way off Rattatak and away from Ventress with the help of Anakin and Master Ki-Adi-Mundi, the two found their way back to the Republic and to the Grand Army. Following the ordeal, and after several weeks in a bacta tank, Alpha was sent back to Kamino, where he was to train current and future generations of clone commanders. He followed Anakin's previous suggestion that he give them names, was promoted to the rank of captain, and became lead instructor of the Tipoca City training facility. Commanders Cody of the 212th Attack Battalion, Neyo of the 91st Reconnaissance, Bacara of the 21st Nova Corps, Gree of the 41st Elite Corps, Appo of the 501st Legion, Rex of the 501st Legion, Bly of the 327th Star Corps, Faie, Wolffe, and many more were among the first hundred to be enrolled in and graduate from his experimental training regimen, known as the ARC training program. Of those first hundred, a few were hand-picked by Alpha and assigned to Squad Seven, with Commanders Davijaan and Cody as its leaders. thumb|200px|Alpha-17 is wounded by General Grievous. Alpha's duties on Kamino, however, did not mean an end to service in the field. Alpha was chosen to accompany Senator Bail Organa on his tour of the Outer Rim Sieges aboard the Star Destroyer Intervention. When the Intervention was commandeered by General Kenobi in order to lead the assault on Boz Pity, Alpha readied himself for yet another battle. During the fighting, Alpha was critically wounded by General Grievous himself, so much so that it was thought he'd never walk again. As hostilities wound down, Alpha, along with the supposed corpse of Asajj Ventress, was loaded onto a medical transport and sent back to Coruscant for treatment. En route, however, Ventress proved very much alive when she took control of the transport and ordered its pilots to take her into the unknown.Star Wars: Obsession Unlike the common clone soldiers, Alpha had a strong and distinctive personality. He behaved as Jango Fett sometimes, and obviously saw himself as a professional soldier. He had little patience for those which didn't reach his standards, and had no problems with sharing his opinion openly with others, they wanted to hear it or not. That caused him friction, specially on Jabiim, when he complained loudly about the Padawan Team, a group of young Jedi masterless, which he saw as amateurs and undisciplined. Sometimes, Alpha also proved to be very cynical and sarcastic, but eventually those which worked with him ended appreciating him. According to the Star Wars: The Clone Wars DVD's commentary, the creators at first had decided to put in Alpha-17 as the Clone Wars series' prime clone character.
