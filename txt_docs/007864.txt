Sel Maa was a humanoid female socialite native to the planetStar Wars: Attack of the Clones: The Visual Dictionary Coruscant. She was a gambling club patron during the last decades of the Galactic Republic at the Outlander Club, a nightclub and gambling hall in the Uscru Entertainment District of Coruscant. Maa spent a great deal of time with the Human Daro Willits when at the club. During a night in 22 BBY,The New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY. she visited the club, drinking next to the bar and talking with two Rodians and a Pacithhip just before two Jedi, Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker, entered searching for the bounty hunter Zam Wesell after her assassination attempt on Senator Padmé Amidala. As the two walked in, she moved to a private alcove of the club near the entrance and held a willing audience with Willits, flirting with him and conversing with fellow patron Reha Logg. Just moments after, she returned to the bar and stood with the aliens. Maa then went back to talking with Logg and Willits as Skywalker searched the club for Wesell. Maa had blonde hair styled in a beehive hairdo and brown eyes. Her skin color comprised of red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple, but was light-colored around her face. She had a sympathetic ear for Daro Willits, flirting with him on the night she visited in 22 BBY. While in the club, Maa wore silver-colored heels and a sequined bodysuit. Sel Maa first appeared in the prequel trilogy film Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones, which was released on May 16, 2002.Star Wars Year By Year: A Visual History, New Edition The Outlander Club scenes including Maa, who was portrayed by an uncredited extra, were shot at Fox Studios, Sydney on July 17, 2000.Mythmaking: Behind the Scenes of Attack of the Clones Maa's rainbow body makeup was done by Makeup Supervisor Lesley Vanderwalt and her team of eight makeup artists. Maa and other patrons of the Outlander Club were named by George Lucas and his film production company, Lucasfilm, during the production of the movie in anticipation for spin-off fiction opportunities and licensing. The character was first revealed when the extra playing Sel Maa, in costume, introduced the twelfth episode of the Episode II behind-the-scenes video series on location. The episode, "Jedi Beverage Break," was released on October 17, 2000. The character was first identified in an Episode II Depth Commentary available on StarWars.com that could be accessed through the DVD-ROM home release of Attack of the Clones on November 12, 2002. In the April 6, 2004, article "Who's Who in the Outlander Club: It's Ladies' Night," published in the seventy-fifth issue of the Star Wars Insider magazine, author Daniel Wallace described Maa as the finest of retro-futurism.
