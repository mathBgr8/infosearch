|pages=32 |upc= |timeline=10 ABYThe New Essential Chronology'The Complete Star Wars Encyclopedia dates the destruction of the Pelagia (Vol. III, "Pelagia") to 10 years after the Battle of Yavin (set in 0 BBY). Moreover, The Complete Star Wars Encyclopedia (Vol. I, "Bonadan") and A Guide to the Star Wars Universe, Third Edition, Revised and Expanded ("Nespis VIII") date the destruction of Nespis VIII to 6 years after the Battle of Endor (set in 4 ABY). |series=Star Wars: Empire's End |issue=1 |prev=Dark Empire II 6 }} Empire's End 1 was the first issue of the two-part Star Wars: Empire's End series of comics. It was released on October 24, 1995 by Dark Horse Comics. The Emperor is back&mdash;and this time he's out for blood! Following his battle with Luke Skywalker in Star Wars: Dark Empire II, Emperor Palpatine was forced to occupy an inferior clone-body. That body is now aging rapidly, and Palpatine's final, irrevocable end is near unless he can infuse his line of clones with genetic material from a Jedi bloodline. His target: Han and Leia's youngest child, Anakin! The events of this issue were adapted in the Empire's End (audio) radio drama, which featured the music of John Williams' score from the films.
