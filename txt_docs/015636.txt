During the race, the fathier was ridden by the jockey Arca Yroca, who whipped the mount's hind quarters with an electrocrop as they raced around the racetrack at the Canto Casino complex. The human Finn briefly watched the race while on a mission to Canto Bight using a pair of electrobinoculars mounted to the railing of a casino in the same complex. While viewing the race, Finn focused on Hoth Stuff, and the electrobinoculars displayed the fathier's name in Aurebesh. Hoth Stuff had brown fur, black eyes, and shared their name with the planet Hoth.Star Wars: Absolutely Everything You Need to Know, Updated and Expanded }} Hoth Stuff appeared in the 2017 film Star Wars: Episode VIII The Last Jedi, the second installment in the Star Wars sequel trilogy. The fathier's name was one of several possible names offered by Lucasfilm Story Group member Pablo Hidalgo to director Rian Johnson. The other options included Kessel Runner and Mynock Minute. The phrase "Hoth Stuff" originated in the Star Wars Legends continuity where it appeared as the title for the seventy-eighth issue of the 1977 comic series Star Wars.
