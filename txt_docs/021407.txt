|penciller=Jim Baikie |inker=Jim Baikie |letterer=Lois Buhalis |colorist=Jim Baikie |cover artist=Dave Dorman |editor=Bob Cooper |publisher=Dark Horse Comics |release date=November 28, 1995 |pages=32 |upc= |timeline=11 ABYStar Wars: The Comics Companion |series=Star Wars: Empire's End |issue=2 |next=last }} Empire's End 2 was the second and final issue of the Star Wars: Empire's End series of comics. It was released November 28, 1995 by Dark Horse Comics. Death stalks Emperor Palpatine! The Emperor's aging process is rapidly accelerating and his doom is foretold by the oracle stone. The key to his problem, the solution to his aging, is close at hand; Han and Leia's youngest child, Anakin, can reverse the process. Palpatine must kidnap young Anakin and claim the boy's body as his own! But for the sake of their son, and for the sake of the galaxy, Han and Leia cannot let that happen&mdash;even if it means losing their own lives. The events of this issue were adapted in the Empire's End (audio) radio drama, which featured the music of John Williams' score from the films. In Empire's End 2, which is set in 11 ABY, Darth Sidious says, "It has been ten years since I last stood on this lost world" while on Korriban, but he goes to Korriban in Star Wars Battlefront:
