Long abandoned by its inhabitants, the world eventually became the site of an Imperial base in its stone ruins. By 0 BBY, the base was assaulted by the Rebel Four, an Alliance to Restore the Republic team, but they were defeated by machinations of the Sith Lord Darth Vader. Vatleria was a dying terrestrial planet with a breathable atmosphere covered in fog and darkness, located near more prosperous colony worlds. The planet was within its eponymous star system, which itself was located in the Calaron sector inside the Slice portion of the Outer Rim Territories. thumb|right|300px|The Rebel Four attack Imperials on Vatleria. Decaying conditions on Vatleria led to its abandonment by its inhabitants long before the Galactic Civil War between the Galactic Empire and the Alliance to Restore the Republic. By 0 BBY,Following the mission to Vatleria in "The Rebel Four," the comic ends with the statement that Darth Vader will fulfill his wish for combat with a Jedi soon, referencing his duel with Obi-Wan Kenobi during the Rescue of Princess Leia in , which takes place in 0 BBY according to The New Essential Chronology. the Empire began to establish a base within Vatleria's stone ruins due to the world's proximity to successful colonies, taking advantage of the planet's dark and foggy atmosphere. An informant passed along information regarding the base to the Rebel Four, a team of Alliance operatives that included F4-MF, Falaem Onn, Grimgrim, and Soo Rcharrz. The four travelled to the ruins, where F4-MF observed Imperial stormtroopers patrolling the area outside their base. The Rebel Four charged into the base, attacking the stormtroopers within. However, the Rebels were caught in traps activated by the Sith Lord Darth Vader, who was observing them through a screen. Ropes emerged that bound each of the Rebel Four and pulled them into death chambers, where they were each dispatched gruesomely. Afterward, Vader lamented to an Imperial officer that he had not been challenged and wished to fight a Jedi again. The Sith then choked the officer when they attempted to highlight the Empire's victory. Vatleria appeared in "The Rebel Four," a comic story written and illustrated by Jay Stephens and published in the ninth issue of the anthology series Star Wars Tales by Dark Horse Comics on October 10, 2001. The planet's name is an anagram of Latveria, the homeland of the Marvel Comics Fantastic Four supervillain Doctor Doom. In spite of the comic's non-canonical status within the Legends continuity, the Vatleria system was still mentioned in the 2009 reference book The Essential Atlas by Daniel Wallace and Jason Fry, which placed the system, and therefore Vatleria, in grid square T-9.
