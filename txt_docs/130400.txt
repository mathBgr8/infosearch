Elan Sel'Sabagno was a male Balosar who was active on Coruscant, the capital of the Galactic Republic, during the Separatist Crisis. A small-time criminal,Star Wars: I Want to Be a Jedi (2017) Sel'Sabagno made money as a slythmonger,Star Wars: Complete Locations tending to tour Coruscant's nightclubs to sell his addictive death sticks.Star Wars: The Visual Encyclopedia In 22 BBY, shortly before the outbreak of the Clone Wars, Sel'Sabagno was present in the Outlander Club and attempted to sell his death sticks in the drinks bar to Jedi Knight Obi-Wan Kenobi, who was searching for the Clawdite bounty hunter Zam Wesell. Kenobi used a mind trick to convince Sel'Sabagno that he did not want to sell the Jedi any death sticks and instead wanted to go home and rethink his life. However, Sel'Sabagno was weak-minded, as Obi-Wan Kenobi performed a mind trick on him and convinced him to not sell death sticks to the Jedi Knight and to go home to rethink his life, with Sel'Sabagno deciding to take up an honest life. This decision stuck, resulting Elan eventually becoming a family man. }} Elan Sel'Sabagno was portrayed by Matt Doran in the 2002 film Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones. The character was named by Star Wars creator George Lucas. In Star Wars Legends, the character was first identified as Elan Sel'Sabagno, a name that was canonized by the Coruscant encyclopedia. His alternative last name, Sleazebaggano, was canonized by the StarWars.com Databank. Although Sel'Sabagno's ears were replaced by antennapalps in post-production of Attack of the Clones, the reference photography didn't have the digital alteration, causing early Sleazebaggano toys to have ears instead of antennapalps. Sel'Sabagno later made a cameo in the background of the 2019 comic Star Wars Adventures (2017) 25 by Delilah S. Dawson, who confirmed the character featured was indeed Sel'Sabagno via Twitter. Dawson considered his cameo among her greatest contributions to the Star Wars galaxy as it confirmed he had, indeed, turned his life around.
