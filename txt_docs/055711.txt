|death=August 18, 2024 |listedpronouns= |nationality=American |occupation=Writer |companies= |website= }} Kenneth C. Flint (June 23, 1947&mdash;August 18, 2024) was an American author. He contributed two short stories to the Star Wars universe: "Doctor Death: The Tale of Dr. Evazan and Ponda Baba" for Tales from the Mos Eisley Cantina and "Old Friends: Ephant Mon's Tale" for Tales from Jabba's Palace. Flint was also the author of a novel in which Luke Skywalker returned to Tatooine in search of an artifact belonging to Obi-Wan Kenobi.
