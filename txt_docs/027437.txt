|published in=Tales from Jabba's Palace |reprinted in= |pages=18 |timeline=4 ABYThe Essential Reader's Companion |series= |preceded by= |followed by= }} "And Then There Were Some: The Gamorrean Guard's Tale" by William F. Wu tells the tale of Gartogg&mdash;a dim-witted, even by Gamorrean standards, Gamorrean guard&mdash;as he tries to solve a series of murders in Jabba's Palace. The short story appeared in Tales from Jabba's Palace, which was published by Bantam Spectra December 1995. Gartogg is a Gamorrean guard in Jabba's Palace who dreams of one day serving aboard Jabba's barge. He is disappointed because he is always assigned to patrol the dark back hallways. Gartogg is continuously looking for something important that will promote his status among the other guards. He attempts to curry favor with the Hutt by solving the mystery behind a rash of murders in the palace. Unfortunately, he is too stupid to find the killer or, indeed, to correctly identify dead bodies when he stumbles across them. The method by which he draws his final conclusion is somewhat erroneous but nonetheless is accurate.
