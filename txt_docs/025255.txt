|other date= |media type=Trade paperback |pages=112 |isbn=9781593071950 |isbn2= |timeline=22 BBY / 21 BBY |series=Star Wars: Republic |issues=4 |preceded by=Star Wars: Clone Wars Volume 3: Last Stand on Jabiim |followed by=Star Wars: Clone Wars Volume 5: The Best Blades }} Star Wars: Clone Wars Volume 4: Light and Dark is the fourth volume in the Clone Wars editions of the trade paperbacks for the comic series Star Wars: Republic. It collects issues 54 and 63, Jedi: Aayla Secura, and Jedi: Count Dooku. It was published by Dark Horse Comics on June 9, 2004. Volume 4 was reissued as the Star Wars: 30th Anniversary Collection Volume 5: Light and Dark hardcover. The Jedi are taught to use the Force for good; to avail themselves only to the light side. But the dark side can be a dangerous temptation to even the strongest Jedi, as Aayla Secura learns when she battles former Jedi-in-training Aurra Sing, and Quinlan Vos discovers when he crosses swords with a true master of the dark, Count Dooku. Set against the backdrop of the Clone Wars, this novel-length adventure is filled with espionage, betrayal, and amazing lightsaber battles. It all begins with a dangerous undercover assignment that leads to&hellip; well, we dare not reveal the shocking ending!
