At the time of the Battle of Yavin, Reytha was governed by Governor Antes Belladar. thumb|left|Muja bushes on Reytha Reytha's microclimates ensured that it had suitable growing conditions all year round. At the peak of the Empire's power, genetic modifications like special synthesizers and genetic inducer-droids were introduced on a greater scale, and the planet became known as the 'breadbasket of the Empire,' producing nearly 10% of the food for the Imperial military. Its major crop was muja fruit; Reythan berries were also produced there. Reytha's seas and oceans were teeming with fish and were almost as productive as its agricultural fields. Reytha was an agriworld which belonged to the Galactic Republic. Reytha's main settlement was the city of Yeere, which held the only spaceport on the planet and was also garrisoned with a meager number of troops. During major conflicts, when agriworlds became prime targets, Yeere had been the location of reinforced fortresses and strongholds. The large Montrosa Ocean kept the planet cool and drove the bountiful rains that nourished the soil. The ocean was the location of an island chain popular with Mon Calamari star tours.Star Wars: Galactic Battlegrounds DataBank, Reytha entry Shortly after the Battle of Yavin, when Antes Belladar was the Governor, the Rebel Alliance captured Reytha, depriving the Empire of valuable food resources. The Empire's Executor, Darth Vader, learned of the strike from a mind probe of Rebel General Jan Dodonna, was sent to take it back. As Mara Jade looked on in secret, Vader eventually recaptured Reytha, despite Governor Belladar's many delaying tactics. Convinced Beladar was either a Rebel sympathizer, or was too incompetent to evict all Rebel presence on the planet, Vader executed him. By 4 ABY, the planet had become an Imperial fortress world. Reytha was conquered by the New Republic Third Fleet under command of Admiral Willham Burke in 6 ABY.The Essential Guide to Warfare Reytha was first mentioned in the strategy guide for the 2001 video game Star Wars: Galactic Battlegrounds. It serves as the location of two consecutive Darth Vader missions, which were also included on the game's 2002 expansion pack, Star Wars: Galactic Battlegrounds: Clone Campaigns.
