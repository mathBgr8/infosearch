|listedpronouns= |nationality=American |occupation=Author |companies= |website= }} Peter Allen David (September 23, 1956 - May 24, 2025) was an American writer, who wrote "Skippy the Jedi Droid" for the first issue of Star Wars Tales. he also wrote the teen spy adventure SpyBoy for Dark Horse Comics, but his best-known comic work was on The Incredible Hulk. David was also known for writing various Star Trek novels, including the original Star Trek: New Frontier series. He often jokingly described his occupation as "Writer of Stuff". Other Marvel Comics titles he wrote for included Spider-Man, Spider-Man 2099, X-Factor, Fantastic Four, Captain Marvel, Scarlet Spider, and the adaptation of Stephen King's The Dark Tower. He also wrote for Halo: Helljumper and the 2011 video game Spider-Man: Edge of Time. At DC Comics, David wrote for Aquaman, Young Justice, Supergirl, and Fallen Angel, as well as a few episodes of Cartoon Network's Young Justice cartoon adaptation. He also wrote for other TV shows and video games, namely Babylon 5, Ben 10: Alien Force. David also co-created the kids' sci-fi series Space Cases for Nickelodeon in the 1990s, which guest-starred actors such as Star Wars legend Mark Hamill and future Star Wars voice actor George Takei. *Peter David at Memory Alpha Category:Comic authors
