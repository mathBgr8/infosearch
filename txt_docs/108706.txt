Reina March was a young Human female criminal who hailed from the planetCoruscant and the Core Worlds Coruscant. March was the leader and head lieutenant of the anarchist organization Edge-9 during the last decades of the Galactic Republic. Her organization called for the dismantling of Coruscant's government and the abolition of its noble upper class. Although the groups motives seemed noble, March and other members of the group turned to violence to get their message across. She occasionally accompanied the con artist Dannl Faytonni in the Outlander Club, a nightclub and gambling hall in the Uscru Entertainment District of Coruscant. In 22 BBY,The New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY. The scene from Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter in which Jango Fett is in the Outlander Club takes place immediately before the film, meaning it must be set in that year. March visited the club while bounty hunters Jango Fett and Zam Wesell met there. She was accompanied by Faytonni andJango Fett: Bounty Hunter the woman Whimper Save. Shortly after, March, Faytonni, and Save visited the club the night of Wesell's attempted assassination of Senator Padmé Amidala. As the Jedi Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker entered to club trying to find the bounty hunter, the trio talked with each other at the bar. When Kenobi made his way to the bar, March talked with a Human who joined their group before they departed as she waved at them. Not long after, Kenobi disarmed Wesell at the bar, causing March and her companions to look at the altercation. Skywalker told the clubgoers to go back to their drinks and March turned her attention back to her group. March wore a perpetual scowl and had brown hair kept short, light skin, and dark colored eyes. With the fervor of youth, she believed in the cause of Edge-9 and used violence to get the groups message across. March was a martial artist who was known for her excellent hand-to-hand fighting skils. She was proficient in the use of blaster pistols, vibro-weapons, and simple weapons. March was also skilled in demolitions, disguising, and gathering information. The anarchist was knowledgeable on her homeworld and its politics. She was able to speak, read, and write in Basic. March possessed an encoded comlink, a frequency jammer, a vibroblade, and a heavy blaster pistol. March favored inconspicuous black clothing to blend into a crowd with. During her two visits to the Outlander Club in 22 BBY, she wore a dark top paired with dark gloves. thumb|right|300px|The uncredited extra playing March on set. Reina March was first mentioned in Coruscant and the Core Worlds, a 2003 sourcebook for the Star Wars Roleplaying Game published by Wizards of the Coast. In Dressing a Galaxy: The Costumes of Star Wars, a 2005 book written by costume designer Trisha Biggar,Dressing a Galaxy: The Costumes of Star Wars March was identified as a character appearing in the prequel trilogy film Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones, which released on May 16, 2002.Star Wars Year by Year: A Visual Chronicle The Outlander Club scenes, where the character was portrayed by an uncredited extra, were shot at Fox Studios, Sydney on July 17, 2000.Mythmaking: Behind the Scenes of Attack of the Clones March was first shown with the release of the trailer for Episode II on March 10, 2002. The character then made her first appearances in illustrations in the upcoming films' storybook, written by Jane Mason and Sarah Hines Stephens;Star Wars: Attack of the Clones Movie Storybook its young readers book Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter, written by Eric Arnold and illustrated by Valerie Reckert;Jango Fett: Bounty Hunter and the film's junior novelization, written by Patricia C. Wrede. All three books were released on April 23, 2002, and the images of her in the books were all taken from the then-upcoming film. In Coruscant and the Core Worlds, March is featured in the roleplaying adventure "Panic in the Plaza." In the adventure, set during the time of the Galactic Empire on Coruscant, March and other Edge-9 members plan to sabotage the Imperial Fair on the Glitannai Esplanade by commandeering an armored vehicle and steering it into crowds of people. The parade hijacking serves as a cover for March's storming of the Imperial Palace's observation balcony, where she would kill the high-level politicians there.
