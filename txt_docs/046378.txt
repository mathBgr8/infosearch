|founded=1932 |founder=Ole Kirk Kristiansen |dissolved= |headquarters=Billund, Denmark |parent= |subsid= |website= }} }} The LEGO Group is a company that produces LEGO branded toys. Although LEGO Star Wars is non-canon, products and stories have tied in to material from both the former Legends and new canon continuities, such as adapting or parodying the events and characters. thumb|right|150px|A LEGO Star Wars Imperial AT-AT Walker The LEGO brand of plastic toy bricks is produced by LEGO System A/S, better known as the LEGO Group, which was founded in 1932 by Ole Kirk Kristiansen and is headquartered in Billund, Denmark. Beginning in 1999, the LEGO Group began releasing LEGO Star Wars products as their first licensed property. The LEGO Star Wars construction sets and minifigures have included characters, locations, and vehicles. Further tie-ies include books, short films, television series, and video games. Jens Kronvold Frederiksen, who is LEGO's design director for Star Wars, has been involved with LEGO Stars Wars since 1998. He frequently collaborates with Chris Gollaher, Lucasfilm's director of product development. The LEGO Star Wars short films and television series have included adaptations of the feature films along with original stories. They have been broadcast on Cartoon Network, Disney XD, and Disney+. The animation for the video games is produced by TT Games. Earlier releases did not have character dialogue. Beyond the Star Wars theme itself, there are Star Wars references in two LEGO Indiana Jones sets; 7621 The Lost Tomb, and 77013 Escape from the Lost Tomb, with both sets depicting C-3PO and R2-D2 in hieroglyphs.
