|intl release= |rereleased= |media type=Paperback |pages=309 |isbn=0345463102 |isbn2= |isbn3= |timeline=20 BBYThe Essential Reader's Companion (24 months ABG) |series=MedStar Duology |preceded by= |followed by="MedStar: Intermezzo" }} MedStar I: Battle Surgeons is the first of two novels in the MedStar Duology, and written by Michael Reaves and Steve Perry and published by Del Rey on June 29, 2004. It takes place in 20 BBY, 2 years after the Battle of Geonosis. The book is part of the Clone Wars multimedia project. An abridged audiobook adaption narrated by Jonathan Davis was released the same day. As civil war between the Republic and the Separatists rages across the galaxy, nowhere is the fighting more fierce than on the swamp world of Drongar, where a beleaguered mobile hospital unit wages a never-ending war of its own&hellip;. A surgeon who covers his despair with wisecracks; another who faces death and misery head-on, venting his emotions through beautiful music &hellip; A nurse with her heart in her work and her eye on a doctor &hellip; A Jedi Padawan on a healing mission without her Master &hellip; These are the core members of a tiny med unit serving the jungle world of Drongar, where battle is waged over the control of a priceless native plant, and an endless line of medlifters brings in the wounded and dying&mdash;mostly clone troopers, but also soldiers of all species. While the healers work desperately to save lives, others plot secretly to profit from the war&mdash;either by dealing on the black market or by manipulating the events of the war itself. In the end, though, all will face individual tests, and only those of compassionate hearts and staunch spirits can hope to survive to fight another day. Jedi Padawan Barriss Offee is sent by the Republic to the planet Drongar, home to the rare plant Bota, important because of its high medicinal value. Her task was to use her Jedi healing techniques in order to assist the medical staff aboard the Rimsoos, the medical facilities tending to wounded clone troopers during the Battle of Drongar. If Barriss is successful in healing the clone troopers, she will return as a Jedi Knight. She makes friends with Republic surgeons Jos Vondar, Tolk le Trene, Klo Merit and Zan Yant, as well as the protocol droid I-5YQ and Republic news reporter Den Dhur. I-Five's purpose on Drongar is to find a way back to Coruscant to find and look after Jax Pavan, a young Jedi apprentice who was the son of I-Five's late business partner, Lorn Pavan. Lorn died twelve years earlier by the Sith known as Darth Maul for being in possession of a Sith Holocron that would have revealed vital details to the Blockade of Naboo that followed shortly after Lorn's death, and plans for the downfall of the Jedi Order. Den Dhur begins to associate himself with CT-914, a clone trooper whose friend died during the battle. CT-914 would later be killed by a Separatist ambush. Barriss finds an antagonistic enemy in Phow Ji, a Republic-hired mercenary who kills Separatist sentient beings on Drongar not just for money, but for his own sadistic pleasures. They clash over their polarized ideals. Meanwhile, in orbit around Drongar, Galactic Republic Admiral Tarnese Bleyd makes a shady deal with Filba the Hutt to smuggle bota offworld in exchange for the restoration of his family's honor and the end of his exile; however, in doing so, he betrays his previous deal with Black Sun. Amid all of this, a spy lurks in the corners, sabotaging the Republic's operations on the planet. Bleyd's deal with Filba is ruined when the Hutt is poisoned and dies. Later, Bleyd is confronted by Kaird the Nedij, a Black Sun assassin, who discovered Bleyd's betrayal and kills him. Phow Ji sacrifices himself by using a bomb to wipe out an entire platoon of Separatist sentient beings, and he has already recorded this to have sent to Den Dhur as an impressive news report to the Republic. Eventually, the spy, who goes by the alias Column under the leadership of the Separatists and the alias Lens under Black Sun, sabotages the Rimsoo that Barriss and her friends reside in. Though they successfully evacuate to another Rimsoo, Zan Yant is killed, throwing Jos Vondar, Dr. Yant's friend, into a deep grief, even despite finding the comfort of a new girlfriend from Tolk le
