*1,300 credits |length= |width= |height=1.6 meters |mass= |gender= |sensor= |plating= |armament= |equipment=*Auditory sensors *Olfactory sensorsThe Essential Guide to Droids *Photoreceptors *Simulated tastebuds *Vocabulator speech system |affiliation= |firstmade= |retired= }} The SE4 servant droid, also known as the SE4 Series Protocol Droid, was a droid line constructed by Industrial Automaton to help with the planning of social occasions and parties. SE4 droids possessed a bipedal Humanoid frame standing just under 1.6 meters tall. Their heads contained standard sensors including a pair of photoreceptors, two auditory sensors, and a vocabulator. SE4s were programmed to understand around one hundred of the galaxy's most common languages, allowing them to function as translators in certain circumstances. The droids were also equipped with two sensitive olfactory sensors; the primary sensor was located at the droid's collar, while the secondary was located on the unit's face and doubled as a cosmetic "nose." The left index finger was lined with simulated tastebuds for the proper preparation of recipes. The SE4 servant droids was programmed with knowledge matrices to be an expert in several fields relating to domestic service. Secondary skill modules could be installed in the droid through a slot on the droid's chest, broadening their range of knowledge and tailoring their skills to be of more use to their owners. The personality of an SE4 droid was uptight, even more so than a standard protocol droid. They were considered to be meticulous, but also persnickety, fretful, and were internally horrified to see breaches of etiquette being made which could cause the droid to shut off its own central processor. New SE4s retailed for around twenty-six hundred credits on the open market, while used models could be purchased for thirteen hundred credits. SE4 servant droids were designed to perform domestic duties, serving in the role of a butler and general household aid with specialities in cooking and entertaining. As such, they were programmed to be experts at party planning, home economics, the culinary arts, and even flower arranging. SE4s were well versed in minutiae such as knowing that diners should not use a three-pronged fork while eating bruallki, that steam pockets needed to be released from boiled suuri by cracking the shell, and that one was never to serve chilled Algarian wine with plicto steak. With the addition of extra skill modules, SE4s could take on a wider array of knowledge bases, ranging from more detailed knowledge of the local cuisine, to the ability to manage the maintenance for a palatial estate, or to operate as cleaners, handymen, or even babysitters. SE4 droids were fully capable of performing minor repair tasks, but complicated work was still best left to dedicated maintenance droids. The SE4 servant droid was one of Industrial Automaton's oldest product lines, and was first manufactured before the company began producing their astromech droid line. Originally marketed towards the wealthy, the SE4 soon fell into general use and was a common sight in lower-income homes as the decades passed. Throughout its lifecycle, the model proved to be resilient, and sales were not affected by the changing tastes of buyers or the ever-shifting galactic economy. As long as SE4s were being bought, Industrial Automaton continued to manufacture them. As the droids were capable of operating for generations, learning new customs as they went, most buyers were happy with the droids and felt they got value for their money. An SE4 servant droid named R02-E was employed by Drayk in the Dim-U monastery on Tatooine.
