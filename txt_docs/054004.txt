The T4-B was armed with two fire linked laser cannons, with three concussion missile tubes placed between them, the firepower of which was devastating against infantry units. Typically, missiles were used at long range, against any target, then the tanks would close in and engage the enemy with their powerful anti-armor laser cannons.Star Wars: Empire at War left|thumb|A T4-B guarding a Rebel outpost. The T4-B provided the Rebels enough of a punch in ground conflicts to withstand significant Imperial ground forces. They were capable of defeating most Imperial ground vehicles of the time, including AT-STs, 2-M repulsor tanks, and TIE Maulers, as well as being capable of battling AT-ATs more effectively than lighter vehicles. Although powerful, the unit did have significant drawbacks. Its tracked design limited its mobility (though its mass and power more than offset this flaw) and was expensive to produce, leading the Alliance to limit its use. The vehicle, along with the T2-B repulsor tank, was one of many new designs acquired by the Alliance during a raid on an Aridus manufacturing plant, and was among the heaviest armored units used by the Rebels during the course of the Galactic Civil War. thumb|A vacant T4-B sits within the [[Battle of Boz Pity (Galactic Civil War)/Legends|Battle of Boz Pity.]] A T4-B was present inside the Rebel's Main Base during the Evacuation of Yavin.
