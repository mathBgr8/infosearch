As Incom Corporation is founded prior to the events of Light of the Jedi, it must have been founded by 232 BBY |dissolved= |affiliation=*Galactic Republic *Galactic Empire *Alliance to Restore the Republic }} }} Incom Corporation was a starship manufacturer whose designs included starfighters, airspeeders, and transports. In the High Republic Era, the Incom Corporation manufactured starfighters like the Z-24 Buzzbug, the Z-28 Skywing,The High Republic: Light of the Jedi and the Z-29 Skyhawk.The High Republic: The Rising Storm They later gained additional prominence during the last years of the Galactic Republic, developing the multi-purpose and durable Z-95 Headhunter starfighterTarkin in cooperation with the Sublights Products Corporation ("Subpro"). With the outbreak of the Clone Wars, Incom benefited from the instability brought on by the conflict and produced vehicles for the Republic Navy. Even if the pilots who flew its craft died in great numbers during the fighting, Incom itself was able to reap the profits of the war. It produced a variant of the Z-95 that saw prolific use with the Republic Navy during the conflict, as well as the Aggressive ReConnaissance-170 starfighter, or ARC-170, which also saw service with the Republic. Incom later went on to develop at least two airspeeder designs for both military and civilian use; the T-47 airspeeder that the Rebel Alliance modified into "snowspeeders" on Hoth, and the popular T-16 skyhopper was used by youths on many worlds. thumb|left|250px|Incom's UT-60D However, Incom's most notable product was the T-65B X-wing starfighter used by the Rebel Alliance. Designed with the Z-95 and ARC-170 Clone Wars-era fighters in mind, the venerable snub-fighter, though initially in public use, was extensively fielded by the Rebel Alliance throughout the Galactic Civil War against the Galactic Empire.Lost Stars Shortly thereafter, Incom went go on to produce the UT-60D U-wing starfighter/support craft, as well as a civilian counterpart, the BT-45D U-wing. However, along with many other corporations during this period, Incom was Imperialized in order to be directly administered by the Empire, thus cutting short the production lines of craft like the U-wing.
