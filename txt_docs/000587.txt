Great ReSynchronization
2618

}} The Great ReSynchronization was a reform of the Galactic Standard Calendar that was adopted in 35 BBY by the Republic Measures & Standards Bureau to recalibrate the disparate dating systems used by the Galactic Republic. The reform established the year 35 BBY as the zero point of the new calendar. The dating scheme for the Great ReSynchronization calendar was introduced in West End Games' Star Wars Adventure Journal for its recurring in-universe articles Galaxywide NewsNets. This dating system used the year 35 BBY at its zero point, which the 1994 reference book A Guide to the Star Wars Universe, Second Edition, Revised and Expanded established as the end of the Clone Wars.A Guide to the Star Wars Universe, Second Edition, Revised and Expanded As later sources placed the Clone Wars one decade later, Pablo Hidalgo's HoloNet News eventually retconned the year 35 BBY as the Great ReSynchronization to justify its use as a calendar zero point.
