Epsi Nadir was a terrestrial planet located in the Epsi Nadir system, a part of the Expansion Region's Epsi Collective sector. It shared its name with the sector's Epsi Zenith system. thumb|right|300px|Epsi Nadir was owned by the Most Honourable Guild of Armourers (an auction of the Guild pictured). At some point, the organization of weapons dealers known as the Most Honourable Guild of Armourers used its considerable resources to purchase Epsi Nadir in order to bypass interference in its business by the Galactic Empire. The guild then established there its auction house, the Guild Auction Hall, and acquired legally binding planetary waivers in order to sell weapons&mdash;classified as "collector's items" or "historical artifacts"&mdash;to its clients. Dej Vennor, a bounty hunter, who specialized in hunting wanted pirates and was active byThe Far Orbit Project 0 ABY, purchased the Imperial IT-series interrogation droid IT-904 from the Guild at an auction on Epsi Nadir. At one point between 0 ABY and 4 ABY,Galaxy Guide 9: Fragments from the Rim is set in the time period between the Battle of Yavin and the Battle of Endor. The New Essential Chronology dates those two battles to 0 BBY and 4 ABY, respectively, and also establishes that the Battle of Yavin was the final event before the beginning of 0 ABY. a group of individuals attended an auction at the Guild Auction Hall, with a number of high-profile bounty hunters also being in attendance. The Guild Auction Hall of the Most Honourable Guild of Armourers was located on Epsi Nadir. thumb|right|200px|Epsi Nadir was first mentioned in Galaxy Guide 9: Fragments from the Rim. Epsi Nadir was first mentioned in the 1993 Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game supplement Galaxy Guide 9: Fragments from the Rim, which was written by Simon Smith and Eric S. Trautmann for West End Games. The 2009 reference book The Essential Atlas placed the Epsi Nadir system, and thereby the planet itself, in grid square M-15. In real-world astronomy, the nadir is the point on the celestial sphere that is the opposite of the zenith, or the point directly above the observer. Galaxy Guide 9 includes a roleplaying adventure idea that involves the player characters attending an auction on Epsi Nadir. The book suggests that it be highly dangerous for a firefight to break out during the event, with such bounty hunters as Boba Fett, IG-88, and Zardra potentially being present.
