It was situated on the Overic Griplink hyperspace route, which linked it to the Florn and Pakuuni systems. At some point between 2 BBY and the 0 BBY,The Essential Guide to Warfare dates the Battle of Ton-Falk to before the Battle of Yavin, which took place in 0 BBY per The New Essential Chronology. the Battle of Ton-Falk of the Galactic Civil War was fought over Ton-Falk. The battle was an embarrassing defeat for the Galactic Empire due to the loss of an entire Imperial Navy battle group to a small number of Rebel Alliance starfighters, and it led to Kuat Drive Yards designing the Ton-Falk-class escort carrier for Imperial use. Ton-Falk's name was introduced via a mention of the Battle of Ton-Falk in Greg Gorden's 1989 Imperial Sourcebook that was published by West End Games for use with Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game.Imperial Sourcebook "Twinkle, Twinkle Little Star, How I Wonder Where We Are," a 1990 roleplaying game source article published in the thirteenth issue of the Voyages SF magazine, described Ton-Falk as a location. Since that article was released outside of the Lucas Licensing process, its canonicity within the Star Wars Legends continuity was never confirmed. thumb|right|250px|"Twinkle, Twinkle Little Star, How I Wonder Where We Are" placed Ton-Falk in the Heterkus sector. Stephen J. Sansweet's 1998 Star Wars Encyclopedia subsequently provided the first unambiguously canonical description of Ton-Falk being a location in Star Wars Legends,Star Wars Encyclopedia and the 2009 reference book The Essential Atlas by Daniel Wallace and Jason Fry placed it on a galactic map in grid square T-5. Ultimately, Stay on Target, a 2014 sourcebook published by Fantasy Flight Games for use with the Star Wars: Age of Rebellion line of Star Wars Roleplaying products, more firmly established Ton-Falk as a distinct celestial body. "Twinkle, Twinkle Little Star, How I Wonder Where We Are" also placed Ton-Falk in the Heterkus sector, but the StarWars.com Online Companion to The Essential Atlas overrode that placement by establishing that the Ton-Falk system, and therefore the celestial body, was part of the Pakuuni sector instead. *Imperial Sourcebook *Imperial Sourcebook, Second Edition *Galaxy Guide 3: The Empire Strikes Back, Second Edition *Star Wars Encyclopedia *The Complete Star Wars Encyclopedia *The Essential Atlas *Star Wars: The Official Starships & Vehicles Collection 29 *The Essential Guide to Warfare *Stay on Target Category:Astronomical objects in the Outer Rim Territories Category:Astronomical objects in the Slice Category:Locations along the Overic Griplink Category:Pakuuni sector locations
