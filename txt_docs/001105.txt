*Aspyr Media (Nintendo Switch and PlayStation 4 ports) |release date=February 28, 2005 |closed= |upc= |engine= |genre=First-person shooter |modes=*Single Player *Multiplayer |ratings=ESRB rating: Teen (T)USK: 16 |platforms=Nintendo Switch, PC, PlayStation 4, PlayStation 5, Xbox, Xbox 360, Xbox One, Xbox Series X/S |series= |basegame= |expansions= |director= |producer= |designer= |programmer= |artist= |writer= |composer= |timeline=22 BBY - 19 BBYThe New Essential Chronology |prev= |next= }} Star Wars: Republic Commando is a first person shooter Star Wars computer and video game, released in the US on February 28, 2005. It was developed and published by LucasArts for the Windows and Xbox platforms, and a port developed and published by Aspyr Media was released for Nintendo Switch and PlayStation 4 on April 6, 2021. It is also playable on PlayStation 5, Xbox 360, Xbox One, and Xbox Series X/S via backwards compatibility. The game uses the "Unreal Engine" developed by Epic Games. It was designed partly to bridge the gap between Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones and Star Wars: Episode III Revenge of the Sith. The developers stated that whereas the films and novels are seen from the point of view of the Jedi, Republic Commando is told from the point of view of the troops. It follows the tale of 'Delta Squad', an elite group of clone commandos, through three missions during the Clone Wars. Trained in squads of four by Mandalorian mercenaries such as Walon Vau and Kal Skirata, the commandos were more capable than their rank-and-file clone brethren, having undergone live-fire training as opposed to mere computerized simulations. All clone commandos were proficient with a large variety of weapons, computer systems, and demolitions equipment. The game covers Delta Squad's activities in three locations, the planet Geonosis, a derelict Acclamator-class assault ship designated the Prosecutor, and the planet Kashyyyk. In each location, the player leads the squad through a series of objectives while battling against enemy forces ranging from droids to Trandoshans. The player is assisted throughout the game by a clone advisor designated 01/425, who regularly updates mission objectives as each level progresses. The game is played from a first person perspective &ndash; the player sees through Boss's helmet Heads-up display and controls Boss's actions. Players can also choose to use either the default display, which is meant to represent the actual helmet's interior, or use a standardized display similar to Halo 2, with Health and Shields on the left side only. Like Halo, Boss has non-regenerating health and regenerating shields. Health can only be restored at Bacta stations around the map, with this also applying to the rest of Delta Squad. Orders can be issued to the rest of Delta squad through squad commands, such as "secure area" and "search and destroy". Certain objects open up special commands &mdash; doors can sometimes be either breached (noisily blown open, a "loud" option) or sliced (quietly hacked, a "silent" option) while squad members can be ordered to man fixed weapons positions. The player's primary weapon is the DC-17m Interchangeable Weapon System, which has rifle, sniper and grenade launcher attachments. The DC-15S is the player's secondary weapon, and a retractable Knuckle plate vibro blade is available for melee combat (though it is only available when using the DC-17M and other full-sized weapons. Some weapons are dropped by enemies, and can be picked up and used by the player, such as the Geonosian elite beam weapon, Accelerated Charged Particle Array Gun or Wookiee Bowcaster. These weapons are in an additional slot, and only one of these can be held at any one time. If a squad member or the player's health reaches zero, they will be incapacitated, instead of dying. They can be revived back to half health by another squad member if available. However, if the player cannot be revived due to all squad members being incapacitated when the player is incapacitated, or the player dies out of reach of the squad (such as falling into bottomless pit), then the squad dies and play may be resumed from a previous save. Republic Commando is notable for being the first official Star Wars game to feature licensed music. The song "Clones," performed by the band Ash, is played during the credits roll at the end of the game but does not feature on the game's soundtrack. Ash are also known to be avid Star Wars fans themselves; their debut album was entitled "1977", and contained numerous Star Wars references. The game's music takes on a high-paced, gritty, and vocal theme compared to the traditional Star Wars score, helping to immerse the player even further in the game. It features the in-universe Mandalorian battle song Vode An (Brothers All), heard throughout the game, including the main menu and several battle sequences. Other Mandalorian battle songs also appear: Gra'tua Cuun, Kote Darasuum, Rage of the Shadow Warriors and Ka'rta Tor. thumb|left|150px|Concept art for Republic Commando The game features uncharacteristically high levels of violence for the franchise, such as blood spatters with melee knife kills, Geonosian exploding when enough damage is dealt and head removal via sniper head shots. Infanticide is possible when the player journeys through a Geonosian hatchery and there are instances of dismembered bodies and blood stains. As such it was given an USK 16 rating in Germany. This was all done to give the player a different view of the Star Wars universe, through the eyes of "A hardened Clone Commando", as most other games had the player play as a jedi. thumb|right|150px|Concept art for Delta Squad A map editor was released with one of the patches to Republic Commando.
