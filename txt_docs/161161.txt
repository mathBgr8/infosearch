|death= |listedpronouns= |nationality=American |occupation=Writer |companies= |website= }} Mark Budz is an American science fiction writer, who co-wrote "Let Us Prey: The Whiphid's Tale" for Tales from Jabba's Palace with Marina Fitch. After graduating from the University of Colorado in 1982, Budz began writing full-time in the late 1980s. According to his official website, "Let Us Prey: The Whipid's Tale" still produces "enough royalties a year to buy a six-pack of expensive microbrew beer or bottle of cheap wine." His first novel, Clade, was published by Bantam Spectra in 2003, and was a finalist for the Philip K. Dick Memorial Award. It also won the second annual Norton Award for the best science fiction novel of the year set in the San Francisco Bay area. Later novels of Budz's include Crache and Idolon.
