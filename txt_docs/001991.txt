An anomaly in itself, Almas was a molten, terrestrial planet orbiting beyond the usual range for its kind, and due to this it received little sunlight. Despite its hot core, Almas was not very geologically active. It had only two small mountain ranges. The planet's atmosphere was initially filled with poisonous gases like phosphorus and methane, until Sith Lord Darth Rivan, aiming to settle there, developed the kaluthin grass to help alter the atmosphere. Almas had no surface water, but there were underground lakes upon which the kaluthin drew and to which settlers later built wells. The kaluthin was a success, given that all Almas' water was located underground, and thrived there for centuries. On the leaves of the kaluthin were phosphorus deposits that glowed with a faint light. When the kaluthin covered the planet, these deposits gave the planet an "aura." Over the centuries, the kaluthin spread across the planet and created a highly oxygen-rich atmosphere. However, trace amounts of phosphorus and methane could still have detrimental effects on those who spent extended time outside without a breath mask. The plants' kilometers-deep taproots also brought up heat from the planet's core, warming the surface. In addition, the purple-green grass gave the planet a distinct aural glow, mainly from the phosphorus it consumed. This living growth was unusually countered by the dark-side energy on the planet, which originated in the desert region and slowly spread. The Jedi measured the spread to be one meter per year. Darth Rivan built his Sith fortress there before 1250 BBY. The castle was located on the desert side of the planet. It was built on this planet with the hopes of gathering dark-side energy from the Force-dense Cularin system. Here Darth Rivan tried to create a corps of Sith battlelords, but his apprentice betrayed him before he could do so. After decades of living there, Darth Rivan was expelled by the Jedi Knights during the New Sith Wars in the Battle of Almas. Parts of the fortress were destroyed, but its core resisted blaster fire. The Jedi left the planet, ignoring the remains of the building. thumb|left|250px|A Believers camp erected around the Almas Sith fortress The kaluthin, thriving even without Rivan, kept Almas from being worth mining. It grew everywhere besides near the Sith fortress, and its roots delved deep. What little mineral content the planet had was not worth the added expense of removing the kaluthin from a mining site. However, the planet became a gardenlike site instead of the nightmare it had been&mdash;except around the Sith Fortress, where no life could thrive. The kaluthin also processed the phosphorus and generated a glow that illuminated the planet from its surface instead of from the sky. After Reidi Artom rediscovered the system in 232 BBY, two Jedi, master Qornah and his Padawan Kibh Jeen, came to Almas to research Rivan's fortress. The Sith fortress corrupted Jeen, who slew his master, took over the asteroid belt pirates, and started the Dark Jedi Conflict (188&ndash;181 BBY). After the Conflict, a Jedi school was founded, which grew into the Almas Academy, the headmaster of which was Jedi Master Nerra Ziveri, and then his former apprentice, Master Lanius Qel-Bertuk. The town of Forard grew up around the Academy. The Academy and Forard were built directly upon the kaluthin, which did not seem to affect the plant at all. There was also a kaluthin Forard research station near the town, where possible transplants of kaluthin to other planets were analyzed. Almas was the only planet in the system not occupied by clone troopers during the Clone Wars. In circa 19 BBY, the Dark Jedi Garth Ezzar attacked Almas and destroyed the Almas Academy, killing most of the Jedi there. Months after the Clone Wars, agents of Bail Prestor Organa infiltrated the ruined temple of the Almas Academy, and found it infested by dark-side spirits. They retrieved a holocron through which Jedi Master Denia learned Farseeing techniques which enabled her to learn the identity of the Nazren. The ruins and surrounding area were also home to the Dark Lizards of the Sith, a species of Hssiss dragon that was thoroughly permeated with the dark side of the Force. During the Galactic Civil War, the Rebel Alliance established a base on Almas, near the ruins of the Jedi Academy. Rumors of Alliance activity there reached the Galactic Empire, so Admiral Firmus Piett dispatched a group of Imperial agents to Almas to investigate. Shortly after the agents arrived, the stormtrooper platoon that had been assigned to protect them went missing. The agents soon found the Rebel base, but it was abandoned. It was covered under a large amount of foliage, and the Rebels had erected barricades to protect them from attack by Dark Lizards, Believers and t'salaks.
