Jerry Oltion
2276

|death= |listedpronouns= |nationality= |occupation=*Author *Amateur astronomer |companies= |website= }} Jerry Oltion (born 1957) is an author and amateur astronomer. For Star Wars publishing, he wrote At the Crossroads: The Spacer's Tale, a short story that appeared in the 1995 anthology Tales from the Mos Eisley Cantina, published by Bantam Spectra. In 2011, Oltion's eighty-fourth story was published in Analog magazine, setting the record for the most fiction contributions in the magazine's history. He has also written various original science fiction novels and novellas, and his 1997 novella Abandon in Place won the Nebula Award for Best Novella. Oltion is additionally the author of four Star Trek novels, and he has written various other tie-in fiction under the pen name Ryan Hughes. As an amateur astronomer, Oltion is the designer of the trackball telescope, which was featured in a 2006 issue of Sky & Telescope magazine.
