Anchor Blue fought in the Cauldron when Asajj Ventress entered the fray and ultimately emerged victorious over the other gladiators. This Houk lived during the final years of the Old Republic,The New Essential Chronology when he gained renown as a fierce combatant under the nickname Anchor BlueUltimate Sticker Book: Star Wars: Clone Wars in the gladiatorial pits on the planet Rattatak. Despite his natural strength, Houk's patron devised a way to further increase his ferocity through artificial means. Blue's patron secretly implanted a pain-inducing device in the base of the vibroblade attachment that had replaced his amputated hand. A remote control sent searing electrical charges through Houk's body, driving him into heightened rage. To subdue him afterward, the same device released powerful sedatives into his bloodstream. Anchor Blue developed a strong dependency on these illicit chemicals, which allowed his patron to exploit him even further. 280px|left|thumb|Anchor Blue and the other gladiators preparing to attack Asajj Ventress Eventually, shortly after the outbreak of the Clone Wars, Anchor Blue ended up in the Cauldron arena. It was the largest gladiatorial pit on Rattatak, filled with violent warriors, where war barons and generals sought out the best combatants who would win their wars. Therefore, a fierce Houk, like many other gladiators, entered a deadly free-for-all battle,The New Essential Guide to Droids but it was a rare gladiator indeed who could survive multiple fights there. During one battle, the Sith Lord Count Dooku attended the match, hoping to find a new apprentice among the fighters. Soon, the Dathomirian Asajj Ventress entered the arena and, in a short time, defeated most of the remaining gladiators. The survivors united for a single attack against Ventress, but she used the the Force to crush stone debris from the ceiling onto their heads. Anchor Blue was the first to be caught in the rockfall, which led to his death. 280px|right|thumb|Anchor Blue roars fiercely A fierce male Houk named Anchor Blue belonged to a species that had a well-deserved reputation for raw strength and short tempers. Their muscular bulk allowed them to dominate any tests of physical prowess. A Rattataki patron who owned Anchor Blue devised a way to further increase his ferocity through artificial means. The Houk's right hand was replaced with a vibroblade that concealed a frenzy-triggering implant within the weapon's attachment. Blue's patron used a remote device to drive him into violent rages or to calm him with a chemical reservoir that pumped powerful sedatives into his bloodstream. Anchor Blue developed a strong dependency on these illicit chemicals, which allowed his patron to exploit him even further. During the fight in the Cauldron, Anchor Blue fought naked, wearing only a tattered loincloth. Anchor Blue first appeared as an unnamed background character in the sixth chapter of Star Wars: Clone Wars, an animated television micro-series produced by Cartoon Network StudiosStar Wars: Clone Wars Volume One and originally aired on November 14, 2003. The character was later given a name in Ultimate Sticker Book: Star Wars: Clone Wars, a 2004 sticker book written by Simon Beecroft, and short backstory in The Complete Star Wars Encyclopedia, a 2008 reference book written by Stephen J.
