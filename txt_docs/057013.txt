|cover artist=Terese Nielsen |illustrator= |editor= |publisher=None (canceled Del Rey product) |first date=None |last date=None |media type=Novels |timeline=c. 26 ABYAlthough the Knightfall Trilogy was never published, it was set to follow The New Jedi Order: Balance Point, which was set at the beginning of 26 ABY. |num books=3 |preceded by=The New Jedi Order: Balance Point |followed by= }} The Knightfall Trilogy was a three-book cycle set to be published in 2001 as part of The New Jedi Order series. Author Michael Jan Friedman was contracted by Del Rey Books and Lucasfilm to write the trilogy, which would have contained three volumes: Jedi Storm, Jedi Fire, and Jedi Blood. Knightfall was going to star the scientist Danni Quee and the Jedi Knight Jorallen, both of whom were caught up in the New Republic and New Jedi Order's ongoing war with the extragalactic species of religious zealots known as the Yuuzhan Vong. After Friedman finished writing Jedi Storm, however, Del Rey and Lucasfilm Ltd. decided to cancel the trilogy. The Jedi Anakin Solo was slated to die in the upcoming hardcover novel Star by Star, and The New Jedi Order planning team wanted the books leading into Star by Star to focus on his character. Greg Keyes was thus contracted to write the Edge of Victory Duology, a pair of Solo-centric novels that replaced Knightfall. Although Jedi Storm had been completed, neither it nor either of its successors were ever published. thumb|right|150px|Preliminary cover art of Jedi Fire The Knightfall Trilogy was a planned three-book cycle within The New Jedi Order. Its first novel, Jedi Storm, was completed but unpublished, and it explores the ramifications of a declaration made by the Yuuzhan Vong Warmaster Tsavong Lah at the end of the novel Balance Point. Lah claimed that as-yet-unconquered worlds in the galaxy would be spared the full wrath of the invaders' war machine if they brought him Jedi, and Jedi Storm features Jedi Knights being hunted by citizens of those worlds and by a Yuuzhan Vong priestess. Luke Skywalker, Han Solo, and Leia Organa Solo, three primary characters from the original Star Wars trilogy,Empire of Dreams: The Story of the Star Wars Trilogy play a role in the book, helping to hide the hunted Jedi from such groups as the Peace Brigade. The scientist and former Yuuzhan Vong prisoner Danni Quee hopes to avoid the invaders, but she becomes wrapped up in an investigation into missing Jedi after meeting the mysterious Jedi Knight Jorallen. As the two move deep into enemy territory, Quee begins to discover secrets about Jorallen, as well. The next two novels in the trilogy, Jedi Fire and Jedi Blood, were never completed, but they were set to tell the story of Quee and Jorallen's investigation leading them to the priestess and defeating her. Meanwhile, Jedi across the galaxy would be slaughtered in an effort to appease the Yuuzhan Vong. When the planning team of The New Jedi Order decided to cancel the Knightfall Trilogy and focus more heavily on the character of Anakin Solo, a storyline involving the protocol droid C-3PO and droid sentience in the wake of the Yuuzhan Vong's harsh treatment of droids was ultimately dropped. The story had begun in Agents of Chaos I: Hero's Trial, the fourth book of the series. }} In 1999, Del Rey books and Lucasfilm began publishing The New Jedi Order, a multi-authored series of Star Wars novels that told the story of an alien invasion. Within the series, an extragalactic species of religious zealots called the Yuuzhan Vong invaded the galaxy, but their bloodthirsty efforts were repulsed by the New Republic and New Jedi Order.The New Jedi Order: Vector Prime'The New Jedi Order: Dark Tide I: Onslaught The series was set to be a chronological storyline covering a five-year span of galactic history and would see one hardcover novel published every year that chronicled a major event of the war. Additionally, several paperback books would be published throughout each year to add to the story and fill in any gaps. By the end of 2000, two hardcovers and four paperbacks had been published.The New Jedi Order: Dark Tide II: Ruin'The New Jedi Order: Agents of Chaos I: Hero's Trial'The New Jedi Order: Agents of Chaos II: Jedi Eclipse'The New Jedi Order: Balance Point thumb|left|200px|Michael Jan Friedman, the author of Knightfall Michael Jan Friedman, a veteran author of Star Trek novels, was contracted in 2000 to write a trilogy of books to follow Balance Point, the hardcover that was slated to cap off that year. Although no announcement of the trilogy appeared on StarWars.com, news began to reach several third-party websites when cover artist Terese Nielsen published a rough sketch of the cover of the trilogy's first volume on her website in June 2000. She also revealed that it had been christened the "Knightfall Trilogy." Over the next several months, third-party websites and the Random House online catalog revealed the names of the trilogy's individual volumes: Jedi Storm, Jedi Fire, and Jedi Blood. In August, an entry on Jedi Storm appeared in Publishers Weekly. The entry revealed that it would feature Danni Quee, who had been introduced in Vector Prime, the opening novel of The New Jedi Order; as well as the Jedi Knight Jorallen, a new character. Friedman completed writing Jedi Storm at some point during 2000. All three entries in Knightfall were to be paperbacks.The Essential Reader's Companion Around the time of Balance Point publication, Del Rey and Lucasfilm Ltd. decided to shift a large part of The New Jedi Order focus, which in turn necessitated the cancellation of the Knightfall Trilogy. The character of Anakin Solo was slated to die in 2001's hardcover, Star by Star, and the planning team wanted to spotlight him prior to his demise. Greg Keyes, who had been set to pen a single New Jedi Order novel, was thus contracted to write the Edge of Victory Duology, a pair of books that would replace Knightfall and focus heavily on Solo.The New Jedi Order: Edge of Victory I: Conquest'The New Jedi Order: Edge of Victory II: Rebirth No further New Jedi Order novels were published between Edge of Victory and Star by Star, which allowed Del Rey to highlight several novels set during the prequel era in their 2001 publishing schedule. Kathy Tyers, the author of Balance Point, also indicated that the cancellation was done in part to allow more attention to be paid to The New Jedi Order hardcovers. thumb|right|150px|Anakin Solo, the largest beneficiary of Knightfall' cancellation By October 2000, all entries for Knightfall had been removed from the Random House catalog and been replaced by entries for Keyes's duology. Confirmation that the trilogy had been canceled came at the end of the month from Tyers and again several days later from Nielsen, who stated on her website that Lucasfilm Ltd. had been unhappy with the direction of the trilogy and had axed it. She also posted black-and-white cover art of Jedi Fire on her site. Later in the month, StarWars.com officially announced the Edge of Victory Duology, and Shelly Shapiro of Del Rey released a statement regarding Knightfall cancellation. She regretted discarding a completed novel, but she was confident that the decision would produce a positive result for Star Wars publishing. Although the Knightfall Trilogy never saw the light of day, some of the storylines it would have included did. The Edge of Victory Duology dealt with public response to Tsavong Lah's declaration, and it featured a number of Jedi being killed by Yuuzhan Vong sympathizers across the galaxy. Meanwhile, Han and Leia Organa Solo began creating a Great River where Jedi could take refuge.
