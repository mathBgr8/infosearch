|pages=23 |upc=759606081134 |timeline=1 ABYStar Wars: Timelines dates the events of Star Wars Book X: Mutiny at Mon Cala to 1 ABY. |series=Star Wars (2015) |issue=44 }} Star Wars 44 is the forty-fourth issue of the Star Wars comic series. It was written by Kieron Gillen, illustrated by Salvador Larroca, and published by Marvel Comics on March 7, 2018. THE REBELLION NEEDS YOU! The Empire takes from all of us&mdash;our freedom, our dignity, our hopes and for some, even our lives. The Rebel Alliance fights to take back our galaxy from the forces of oppression&hellip;but we need your help! Be a part of the solution&mdash;JOIN THE REBELLION TODAY! On a Mon Cala island, Leia, Han, Luke, and their droids meet with Grand Admiral Urtya of the Mon Cala Mercantile Fleet. Han, who had smuggled them into the restricted nature zone, is confident they can handle any trouble. However, an Imperial patrol ship fires on their location, and their ship is destroyed. As stormtroopers chase them through the woods, Leia continues to brief Urtya, explaining that with the Death Star gone, now is the time to disrupt the Empire. She tells him that the Rebellion needs the Mon Cala fleet, the greatest merchant fleet in the galaxy, and that they can be refitted into warships and hidden across the galaxy. Urtya struggles with the decision to put his people in danger, and the heroes ultimately leave without his commitment. As they pilot the Millennium Falcon to a Rebel production center, a dejected Leia worries about the future of the Rebellion without the Mon Calamari fleet. With a sudden idea, she has C-3PO contact Queen Trios, asking her to grant them access to a high-security Imperial prison system to break out a king. The starship design of the Yavin's Hope type Mon Calamari cruiser is visually identical to a fan-made design from 2014, by an artist with the username "Jonathan" on 3D Warehouse. The work is titled "Croiseur Mon Calamari MC90 &mdash; Forme 3," while another starship design, the three-pronged Mon Calamari cruiser, is also visually identical to another design by Jonathan on . The work is titled as "Croiseur Mon Calamari MC90 &mdash; Forme 2."
