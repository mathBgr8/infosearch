The Essential Reader's Companion lists the Bounty Hunter Wars trilogy as between 0 and 4 ABY. |series=The Bounty Hunter Wars |preceded by=Slave Ship |followed by=The Truce at Bakura }} Hard Merchandise is the third and final novel in The Bounty Hunter Wars trilogy, written by K. W. Jeter and published in July 1999 by Bantam Spectra. Feared and admired, respected and despised, Boba Fett enjoys a dubious reputation as the galaxy's most successful bounty hunter. Yet even a man like Boba Fett can have one too many enemies&hellip;. When Boba Fett stumbles across evidence implicating Prince Xizor in the murder of Luke Skywalker's aunt and uncle, Fett makes himself an enemy even he fears: the unknown mastermind behind a monstrous deception, who will kill to hide his tracks. Fett also finds himself in possession of an amnesiac young woman named Neelah, who may be the key to the mystery—or a decoy leading Fett into a murderous ambush. Fett's last hope is to run through the list of Xizor's hidden enemies. And since Xizor's hidden enemies are almost as legion as Fett's, the chance of survival is slim—even for someone as skilled and relentless as Boba Fett. *; July 6, 1999, Bantam Spectra, 338-page
