Star Wars: Tales from Mos Eisley
4671

|pages=32 |upc= |timeline=3 ABY |series=One-Shot |issue=1 }} Star Wars: Tales from Mos Eisley is a one-shot comic that was published by Dark Horse Comics on March 26, 1996. It was written by Bruce Jones and illustrated by Bret Blevins. It contained the short comics Light Duty, Mostly Automatic, and Heggs' Tale (all of which had appeared in issues of the Star Wars Galaxy Magazine) as well as a collection of one-page image pinups. "Mos Eisley Spaceport. You'll never find a more wretched hive of scum and villainy." With those words, Obi-Wan Kenobi introduced the most famous, not to mention dangerous, drinking establishment in the history of science fiction. Now, you can get a table of your own. Belly up to the bar and let the patrons regale you with a tale of Tatooine, a story of interstellar romance, and a saga of Rebel triumph. And please be sure to check your droids and your blaster at the door.
