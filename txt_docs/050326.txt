Designed by Incom Corporation at its peak for stealth and speed, the A-24 was a long and narrow craft, with a distinctive flat and triangular aft that housed the ship's engines, weapons, and distinctive stabilizers. Amidships, the A-24 sported a pair of canards that supported the ship's powerful sensor and communications arrays, while the forward command deck, while cramped, sported a cockpit that was offset by panoramic sheets of thick photosensitive transparisteel. True to its role as a scout ship, the panoramic cockpit gave the pilot a nearly unobstructed view of the ship's surroundings, while the A-24's powerful Fabritech multi-role sensor suite combined active and passive sensors, optical and electronic enhancers, and a full suite of dedicated energy receptors, full-spectrum transceivers, and lifeform scanners to give the pilot a comprehensive readout and analysis of their surroundings. Ultimately, the A-24 was renowned for being small, fast, and hard to detect - qualities that made it highly regarded as a blockade runner and getaway ship. Possessing superb speed and being highly maneuverable and agile, the A-24 was considered one of the best-in-class for a ship under twenty meters. Routinely, the A-24 was used for scouting, spying, smuggling, and any other activity in which a small and incredibly fast ship was needed.
