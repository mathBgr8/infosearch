|death= |listedpronouns= |nationality=American |occupation=Writer |companies= |website= }} William F. Wu (born March 13, 1951) is an American author. He has written thirteen novels and over seventy short stories, including one for Star Wars publishing: "And Then There Were Some: The Gamorrean Guard's Tale," which appeared in 1996's Tales from Jabba's Palace. Wu became a member of the Science Fiction and Fantasy Writers Association in 1977 and recieved a Ph.D. in American Culture from the University of Michigan in 1979. He has been nominated for the Hugo and Nebula Awards twice each, and once for the World Fantasy Award. Some of his novels use robots and Isaac Asimov's Three Laws of Robotics.
