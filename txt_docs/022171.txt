In 19 BBY, he commanded a unit of Galactic Marines during a battle on the planet Toola, which is where he was issued Order 66, which marked the Jedi as traitors to the Republic. Keller and his men took their Jedi commanders by surprise and successfully killed General Simms, however, the other two Jedi escaped. The clones tracked the fleeing Hudorra and Na to Ithaqua Station where they searched for the Jedi. The two Jedi were able to evade Keller and his men and escape off world, leaving Keller to continue his search of the station for two Jedi who were no longer there. thumb|left|200px|Keller on Toola. Made from the genetic template of bounty hunter Jango Fett and born on the planet Kamino, the clone who would take on the name Keller was created to serve the Galactic Republic during the Clone Wars against the Confederacy of Independent Systems. With the rank of Clone Commander, he was brought into the ARC Commander program and trained in Advanced Recon Commando skills. Upon graduating, he was given the title of ARC commander. During the final stages of the Clone Wars, in 19 BBY,Republic 79 depicts the events and aftermath of Order 66, which The New Essential Chronology establishes to have been executed in 19 BBY. Keller and his unit of Galactic Marines were sent to the frigid Outer Rim worldRepublic 80 of Toola to combat recent Separatist incursions into the area. After weeks of fighting, the Republic forces won the conflict after a successful attack on the battle droids' main generators. Shortly after the victory, Keller and his unit received Order 66 from Supreme Chancellor Palpatine and the clones turned against their Jedi leaders. They managed to kill General Simms, but Kai Hudorra and Simms' Padawan Noirah Na, managed to escape and fled aboard STAPs to Ithaqua Station. thumb|right|200px|Inside a cantina, a fight breaks out between the patrons and Keller's troops. Now serving the newly established Galactic Empire, Keller attempted to hunt down the two Jedi before they could escape the planet. Keller and his men rode atop mastmots&mdash;large, furry creatures native to the planet&mdash;as they trekked through the snow-covered landscape. They eventually found the two STAPs the Jedi had taken, abandoned in the snow due to the fuel running out. One of Keller's marines took note of the footprints the Jedi left behind in the snow and informed Commander Keller that they were heading in the direction of Ithaqua Station. Keller ordered to comm ahead and inform the station's local garrison, then continued onward. Keller arrived at Ithaqua Station and eventually entered a cantina where he ordered the patrons to stay where they were and to have their identifications ready. Unbeknownst to Keller, Kai Hudorra was present in the cantina and incited a brawl between the cantina-goers and Keller's troops, allowing the Jedi to escape from the raid unnoticed by the clones. The frigid night soon came, forcing Keller and his troops off the streets until morning. Once dawn broke, Keller and his men continued their search, unaware that the two Jedi had boarded a ship and were currently leaving the planet. As a male clone of the Human Jango Fett, Keller stood at 1.83 meters tall and had tan skin. He also had black hair and brown eyes. A graduate of the ARC Commander program, Keller was a resilient soldier and skilled leader who utilized his expertise to hunt down the Jedi. During the Clone Wars, Keller wore white clone paratrooper armor with blue markings. He also wore a white cloak over his clone armor with brown straps around his upper arms. Keller was armed with two DC-17 blaster pistols, and a DC-15A blaster rifle. Keller first appeared in the seventy-ninth issue of the Star Wars: Republic comic series, released on November 9, 2005, as part of the Into the Unknown story arc. The comic story was written by Welles Hartley, and Keller was illustrated by artist Douglas Wheatley. Keller was ranked as number five on StarWars.com's top ten list of clones for 2005, published on December 23 of that year.
