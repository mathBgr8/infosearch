In 17 ABY, the wanted Devaronian former army captain Kardue'sai'Malloc fled to Peppel, where for the following two years he resided in a simple hut that was protected by a sophisticated security system. Eventually, the bounty hunter Boba Fett tracked Kardue'sai'Malloc to Peppel, where, eschewing his combat armor and wielding only a bow and knife in order to bypass the Devaronian's security system, Fett captured the fugitive and went on to deliver him to the world Devaron for execution. Peppel was a terrestrial planet located in the Peppel system, a part of the Corva sector in the New Territories portion of the Outer Rim Territories. A quiet and primitive world, Peppel featured on its surface an area with clearings and mud. thumb|right|300px|Boba Fett captured Kardue'sai'Malloc (pictured) on Peppel. In 17 ABY, establishes that Kardue'sai'Malloc fled to Peppel thirteen years after the Battle of Endor, which corresponds to 17 ABY per The New Essential Chronology. after killing on the planet Tatooine of the Outer Rim's Arkanis sectorThe Essential Atlas four mercenaries who had recognized him, the Devaronian Kardue'sai'Malloc, a former army captain on whom a bounty of five million credits had been posted and who was living under the alias "Labria," fled to Peppel. There, he resided in a simple hut where he ate slaughtered animals and spent his time drinking Merenzane Gold liquor and curating his extensive collection of very rare music. Two years later, the Human bounty hunter Boba Fett tracked Kardue'sai'Malloc to Peppel after tracing the sales of Merenzane Gold across the Arkanis sector. Leaving his starship, the Slave IV, outside the range of the sensors that formed a part of the security system installed around the Devaronian's abode, Fett approached the hut on foot, eventually also leaving a callback to the ship beyond the sensors' range, two kilometers away. Eschewing his suit of combat armor, the hunter made his final approach wearing nothing but shorts and armed with a compound bow and a crystal knife. Fett ultimately captured Kardue'sai'Malloc, and, after being forced by the latter to dig up his music collection, the bounty hunter led the Devaronian in boarding the Slave IV&mdash;which Fett had brought down for a landing closer to the hut. Subsequently, the vessel departed for the Colonies world Devaron, where Kardue'sai'Malloc would be executed. On Peppel, Kardue'sai'Malloc resided in a primitive but spacious hut that lay next to a muddy clearing and had an open-air toilet ten meters away. Another clearing was located a few kilometers from the hut. The dwelling was protected by an elaborate security system that included tripwires, light traps, and motion sensors. Outside the hut, Kardue'sai'Malloc had buried his collection of music under a few centimeters of dirt. Peppel first appeared in "The Last One Standing: The Tale of Boba Fett," a short story by Daniel Keys Moran that was published in the 1996 anthology Tales of the Bounty Hunters.
