By 4.3 ABY,According to , the events of the Star Tours can be dated to 4.3 ABY. R2-D7 tended to StarSpeeder 3000s at the Star Tours travel agency's Tomorrowland Starport. Using its scomp link, the droid also operated the track control for the Inventory Droid Assistant to bring replacement parts from inventory to the starport's Droidnostics Center under a Mon Calamari supervisor. However, the I.D.A. seemed to have a mind of its own and R2-D7 had a hard time getting it to work. It was also equipped with a scomp link. 250px|thumb|R2-D7 at work R2-D7 was originally created for the 1987 Disney simulator ride theme park attraction Star Tours. It was featured in the ride's queue at each of its locations, which included each of Disney's resorts excluding Hong Kong Disneyland and Shanghai Disneyland. The droid was first identified by a "Spot the Droid" poster displayed at Tokyo Disneyland's Star Tours attraction. An action figure of R2-D7 was released in 2002 as part of the Disney Parks exclusive Star Wars: Star Tours toy line.
