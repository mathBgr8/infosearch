|model= |type=Flight computer |culture= |creators= |created= |modifier= |destroyed= |discovered= |owners= |locations= |cost= |value= |structure= |size= |material= |length= |width= |height= |weight= |protection= |capacity= |range= |inscription= |markings= |purpose= |heritage= |affiliation= }} The RCS-1 was a flight control computer designed by Miradyne Limited. It was installed on the standard models of the Starfeld Industries Z-10 Seeker. The RCS-1 (like all RCS systems) was notoriously difficult to modify due to a rigid set of inter-connectivity protocols. Non-standard personal modifications often failed to work. The problem was exacerbated by Miradyne's bankruptcy. Since replacement parts became extremely hard to come by, any breakdown in the RCS-1 system had the potential to be catastrophic.
