Ship's Articles
1438

The Ship's Articles was a set of rules of pirate conduct authored by Celis Mott. Many Rebel privateers would adhere to those rules. They were as follows: #Every member shall have a vote, when votes are called, and equal share of provisions found. #All booty shall be doled out fairly, under the watch of a group elected by the crew. Defrauding the ship and crew shall be punished by marooning. #No gambling or intoxicants on duty. #Weapons and vacuum suits to be kept clean and serviceable at all times. #No minors or idle family. #Desertion of one's battle station is punishable by marooning or death, at vote of the crew. #No fighting on board. Personal disputes are to be settled planetside by duel. #No retirement or disbanding until every crew member has shared 100000 credits. The crippled shall receive 80000 credits out of the public stock, and for lesser wounds proportionally. #The captain, engineer, and weapons officer shall receive two shares.
