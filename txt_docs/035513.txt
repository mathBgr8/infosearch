|model=MiniMag PTLStar Wars Battlefront: Prima Official Game Guide |type=Portable missile launcher |culture= |creators= |created= |destroyed= |discovered= |owners= |locations= |cost= |value= |shape= |size= |material= |length= |width= |diam= |height= |weight= |protection= |crew= |capacity=7 missiles |range= |inscription= |markings= |purpose=Anti-armor |heritage= |affiliation=*Galactic Republic *Upland Liberation FrontShatterpoint *Galactic Empire }} The MiniMag shoulder-launched proton torpedo launcher, also known as the MiniMag PTL missile launcher or simply the MiniMag, was a model of portable missile launcher manufactured by Krupx Munitions. Like the PLX-1 portable missile launcher, the MiniMag shoulder-launched proton torpedo launcher's firing chamber was specifically designed for proton torpedoes (hence the acronym), though it was capable of firing other ordnance. Unlike the PLX, however, the PTL did not support multiple firing settings or launch torpedoes in the 'smart' Gravity-Activated Mode.Star Wars: Battlefront The PTL had a capacity for seven missiles. Produced by Krupx Munitions before the Clone Wars, the MiniMag remained in production for many decades. It was used by the Grand Army of the Republic during the aformentioned conflict. A pair of MiniMags were used by the Republic-backed Upland Liberation Front during the concurrent Summertime War on the planet Haruun Kal. They were also carried by Imperial shock troopers and Imperial Marines during the Galactic Civil War.Star Wars: Battlefront II The MiniMag shoulder-launched proton torpedo launcher first appeared in the 2003 Star Wars Legends novel Shatterpoint, written by Matthew Stover.
