Blastonecrosis
967

Blastonecrosis was a highly lethal disease. Symptoms included fatigue and loss of appetite. Blastonecrosis could be masked by the consumption of lotiramine, which hid the tracer enzymes marking the disease's presence in the body. In addition to causing severe bacta allergies in all patients, the purposely contaminated bacta from Lot ZX1449F caused roughly two percent of those exposed to contract blastonecrosis.
