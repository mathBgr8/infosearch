At some point, an indoor marketplace became buried on Krykis IV, later being excavated by archaeologists of the Galactic Empire. They presented the find as the "Voorlach Temple"&mdash;which shared its name with the celestial body VoorlachPlatt's Smugglers Guide of the Duluur sector's Voorlach system&mdash;and took the common market goods retrieved from the structure, which were instead claimed to be religious artifacts, on a tour through three sectors. The true nature of the "Voorlach Temple" was later revealed, and in a lecture given at the Galactic Museum on the Core Worlds planetThe Essential Atlas Coruscant around 11 ABY, is set shortly after the re-establishment of the New Republic on Coruscant following the final defeat of the resurrected Emperor Palpatine, which The New Essential Chronology dates to 11 ABY. the xenoarchaeologist Doctor Corellia Antilles noted the Krykis IV incident as her "favorite" among such affairs. Krykis IV was mentioned in Timothy O'Brien's article "From the Files of Corellia Antilles," which was published as part of the fourteenth issue of the Star Wars Adventure Journal in August 1997. The 2009 reference book The Essential Atlas placed the Krykis system, and thereby Krykis IV, in grid square M-13.
