San Bernardino, California |death= |listedpronouns= |nationality=American |occupation=Artist |companies= |website= }} Rick Berry (born June 2, 1953 in San Bernardino, California) is an American artist who digitally painted the covers of two Star Wars paperbacks in the year 2000: Hero's Trial and Jedi Eclipse, the two installments of James Luceno's Agents of Chaos Duology. As a digital painter, Berry is credited with creating the world's first digitally painted book cover in 1984 for William Gibson's Neuromancer. His art is exhibited in museums and galleries all over the world and can be found in the private collections of Neil Gaiman, Stephen King and George R. R. Martin. Category:Cover artists
