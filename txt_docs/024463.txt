Sling racing
2610

Sling racing was a sport that involved lightweight, modified airspeeders navigating through mountains and canyons at break-neck speeds. It gained popularity after the outlawing of Podracing. Sling racing involved lightweight (200 kg) repulsorlift airspeeders, the sling racers, each having as much as 6 small repulsorlift drive units individually throttled. The speeders however were maneuverable with wing and airfooil mechanics. Sling racing took place among dangerous and narrow canyons, the vehicle had to take advantage of the canyon winds; the pilot adjusted the wings and foils accordingly to the currents as well as individually throttle the drives. The challenge involved the crudeness of controls, the requirement of predicting wind currents and the reflexes of responding to them. Rookies usually couldn't complete one course. Sling racing was known since before the Separatist Crisis, and became more popular while pordracing declined and eventually was outlawed. A few races included artificial obstacles (pillars, energy fields or repulsor mines). There were easier courses available for amateur pilots.
