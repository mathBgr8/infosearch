Cardooine Chills
1185

Cardooine Chills, named after the planet, was a virus that caused congestion, coughing, fatigue and body aches, as well as an insatiable appetite. The virus had relatively stable structure, and those afflicted by it generally developed an immunity, allowing the virus to strike only once in a lifetime. Moreover, it could be prevented through the use of vaccines.
