|published in=Star Wars Gamer 8 |reprinted in=Hyperspace |pages= |timeline=27 ABYThe Essential Reader's Companion |series=Star Wars: The New Jedi Order |preceded by= |followed by= }} "The Apprentice" is a short story written by Elaine Cunningham and is the thirteenth installment of the New Jedi Order series set in the Star Wars universe. Set during the events of Dark Journey, this short story expands upon details briefly mentioned in The New Jedi Order: Dark Journey. The story by Elaine Cunningham was first published in Star Wars Gamer 8 on the 1st of January 2002 by Wizards of the Coast and was shortlisted for the Prix de la Plume Noire by the French Committee for Experimental Literature. The story follows Jaina Solo as she travels to the planet Gallinore with Jedi Master Kyp Durron, Lowbacca, and Tenel Ka. Officially, the group is assisting local scientists in studying the bioengineered technology of the Trickster, a Yuuzhan Vong living ship previously captured by Jaina. Unknown to Gallinore’s authorities and to Tenel Ka, Jaina and Kyp are also transporting a Hapan prisoner who had collaborated with the Yuuzhan Vong. The man possesses a coral slave implant, which Jaina intends to have removed and studied in hopes of adapting Yuuzhan Vong biotechnology for use against its creators. In exchange for his cooperation, the prisoner is offered a conditional pardon. To avoid detection, Jaina and Kyp infiltrate a research facility through its ventilation system, lowering their body temperatures to bypass thermal sensors. During the infiltration, the prisoner attempts to escape, nearly overpowering Jaina using Hapan martial techniques. In response, Jaina releases a burst of Force lightning to subdue him. Although Kyp intervenes, the disturbance activates the facility’s security systems, flooding the tunnels with coolant. The group narrowly escapes through a drainage vortex and encounters security personnel, whom Jaina and Kyp incapacitate. To preserve the secrecy of the operation, Jaina uses the Force to erase the guards’ recent memories under Kyp’s guidance, demonstrating a notable aptitude for the technique. They proceed to the laboratory of Sinsor Khal, a disgraced former Jedi who has since become a bioengineer. Recognizing Jaina’s Force sensitivity, Khal agrees to surgically remove and study the coral slave implant. After the operation, Jaina uses the same memory-altering technique on Lowbacca, erasing his knowledge of her involvement in the covert mission to ensure it remains completely hidden. Throughout the story, Jaina reflects on her decision to apprentice herself to Kyp Durron.
