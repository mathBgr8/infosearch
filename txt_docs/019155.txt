He relayed Viceroy Nute Gunray's orders to destroy the Radiant VII to OWO-3 and was later transferred to the Droid Control Ship Vuutun Palaa, dying when it was destroyed during the Battle of Naboo. thumb|left|250px|[[Sil Unch, Tey How, and Jull Dremon seated on the Saak'ak bridge]] By the year 32 BBY, Lieutenant Jull Dremon served as Chief Gunner aboard the Trade Federation flagship, the modified Lucrehulk-class LH-3210 cargo freighter Saak'ak. Shortly before the Invasion of Naboo, Dremon relayed Viceroy Nute Gunray's orders to destroy the Republic Diplomatic Corps Consular-class space cruiser Radiant VII to a battle droid gunner designated OWO-3. Dremon was later assigned by Gunray to the Droid Control Ship Vuutun Palaa when the Saak'ak left the Naboo system, where the Chief Gunner was killed when it was destroyed by Anakin Skywalker during the Battle of Naboo. Just before ordering its destruction, Jull Dremon commented that the Radiant VII red livery only made it a better target. A Neimoidian, he had mottled gray skin and red eyes. Jull Dremon was originally created for The New Essential Guide to Vehicles and Vessels, a Star Wars Legends reference book written by W. Haden Blackman and released on September 30, 2003. Dremon was retconned into the 1999 film Star Wars: Episode I The Phantom Menace by the Rogues Gallery recurring feature published in Star Wars Insider 147, and his place on the right side of the Saak'ak and Vuunta Palaa bridges was set in stone by Leland Chee. *Star Wars: Episode I — The Phantom Menace 1 *Star Wars: Episode I — The Phantom Menace 4 *Inside the Worlds of Star Wars: Episode I *The New Essential Guide to Vehicles and Vessels *Dressing a Galaxy: The Costumes of Star Wars *Star Wars: Complete Locations *The Complete Star Wars Encyclopedia Category:Gunners Category:Individuals of unspecified gender Category:Neimoidians Category:Trade Federation military members
