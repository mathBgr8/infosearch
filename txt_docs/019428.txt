|class=InsectoidThe New Essential Guide to Droids |subspecies= |races= |height= |length= |wingspan= |mass= |skincolor=Blue-green |haircolor= |feathers= |eyecolor= |distinctions=Multiple life cycles |lifespan=Multi-stage; the pupae form lasting only two standard days |origin=Shikitari |habitat= |diet= |language= }} The Shikitari were a rare, dim-witted insectoid species native to the planet Shikitari in the Unknown Regions. They possessed a multi-stage life cycle, and during the final years of the Old Republic, very few individuals were aware of the Shikitari, with fewer still considered experts on their unique physiology. This dim-witted insectoids possessed a kind of unique physiology and had multi-stage life cycle. Shikitari were born in egg clusters suspended in a poisonous protective gel that warded off predators. As they hatched, the developing pupae fused to one another, growing into a single combined lifeform. Individual Shikitari developed specialized body parts, becoming eyes, mouths, feelers, and so on. Shikitari pupae grew in a body style that mimicked their guardian – in the case of those raised by humanoids, they adopted a similar morphology. However, this stage of the life cycle was extremely short, lasting only two standard days, after which they became encased in a hardened chrysalis from which a dozen harmless creatures, known as flutterscouts, emerged. During the final years of the Old Republic,The Essential Reader's Companion very few individuals knew of Shikitari insectoids, and fewer still were counted as experts on the them specific physiology. The Shikitari were rare, subsentient aliens found on a lush jungle world of the same name in the Unknown Regions. Shortly after the outbreak of the Clone Wars,The New Essential Chronology a Devaronian conman decided to carry out a scam that involved a Shikitari insectoid with the unlikely name of Carl. This Shikitari was a collection of pupae, amalgamated into a humanoid form that was perfectly suited for gladiatorial combat. The Devaronian entered Carl into the Rattataki battle games and pocketed a sizable price for him, snickering to himself that within two days his buyer would be the proud owner of a hardened chrysalis from which twelve harmless flutterscouts would emerge. Nevertheless, Carl died in the Cauldron arena before he could transition to the next biological stage. At some point, the Shikitari were engaged in a war with the Chiss Ascendancy. During the conflict, a group of heroes were mistaken for the enemy combatants or hostile smugglers by a Chiss carrier.The Unknown Regions The unnamed insectoid character first appeared in the sixth chapter of Star Wars: Clone Wars, an animated television micro-series produced by Cartoon Network StudiosStar Wars: Clone Wars Volume One and originally aired on November 14, 2003. Just over two and a half years later, the reference book The New Essential Guide to Droids mentioned a «Shikitari insectoid»; however, it did not confirm which specific character was being referred to. Finally, Shikitari Carl was later given a name and short backstory in The Complete Star Wars Encyclopedia, a 2008 reference book written by Stephen J.
