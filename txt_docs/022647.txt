Young Jedi Knights: Lightsabers
15913

Young Jedi Knights: Lightsabers is a Star Wars Legends young-adult novel written by Kevin J. Anderson and Rebecca Moesta. It is the fourth book in the Star Wars: Young Jedi Knights series, and it was first published by Berkley Boulevard in March 1996. The novel was reprinted in Young Jedi Knights: The Rise of the Shadow Academy (1996) and Young Jedi Knights: Jedi Sunrise (2003). Luke Skywalker has come to a grim conclusion. With the threat of a new generation of Dark Jedi being trained at the Shadow Academy, he knows the New Republic will need all the fighters it can muster. At last, it is time for the young Jedi Knights to build their lightsabers. But there is more to mastering the ancient weapon than mere swordsmanship. Each student must learn about the mysterious bond between Jedi and lightsaber. For Jacen and Jaina, success will mean growing stronger in the Force. But for one of their friends, failure will mean tragedy... With the growing threat of the Second Imperium and the Dark Jedi being trained at the Shadow Academy, Luke decides, against his better judgment, to accelerate the training of his Jedi academy students, and asks them to build and start using their own lightsabers. Unfortunately, Tenel Ka has hastily constructed an inferior weapon. The young Jedi Knights proudly rise to the challenge, but tragedy strikes when during a sparring session with Jacen Solo, Tenel's blade dissipates, causing Jacen to accidentally sever her left arm just above the elbow. Jacen is horrified that he has maimed his friend. Tenel is spirited back to her homeworld of Hapes to recover. On Hapes,Tenel Ka is watched over by her grandmother, Ta'a Chume, who is determined to keep her safe amid court intrigue and assassinations attempts by a treacherous ambassador. She is once again exposed to the old woman's schemings, as she tries to initiate her to Hapan politics, and persuade her to renounce her Jedi training. Jacen, Jaina and Lowbacca arrive later in a welcome respite, but this does not last; after an unknown assassin detonates a bomb in the royal palace, Ta'a Chume determines that their lives are all in danger, and she and the Jedi trainees retire to an isolated island in the middle of the Hapan ocean. Even there, however, they are not safe; they barely escape another assassination attempt from a group of Bartokk killers. Upon their return to the mainland, the Queen Mother orders Ambassador Yfra, who was responsible for the attacks, arrested. After the ambassador is exposed and arrested, Tenel Ka has determined that she wishes to continue her training, and returns to Yavin 4 with her friends, and constructs a new lightsaber. Meanwhile, Zekk trains under Tamith Kai, becoming one of the top students at the Shadow Academy. The Shadow Academy is devoted to rebuilding the Empire to power under its leader, the inexplicably alive Emperor Palpatine. To prove his skills, Zekk engages the other top student, Vilas, in a lightsaber duel and kills him. The book mentioned that Luke Skywalker didn't face Brakiss since he left Jedi academy. Yet in 17 ABY in The New Rebellion novel during Almanian Uprising Luke dueled Brakiss on Telti. Augwynne Djo is mistakenly listed as the mother of Teneniel Djo and continues this by saying that she is the grandmother of Tenel Ka, instead as grandmother and great-grandmother respectively.
