**Royal Advisory Council *Galactic EmpireLeia, Princess of Alderaan *First Order Legacy of Vader 4 |organization=*Trade Federation |type=Monarch |term=Two years,Queen's Shadow renewable once |powers= |duties=Elected ruler of the Naboo }} The Monarch of Naboo was the elected monarch, such as a king or queen, of the Royal House of Naboo, the monarchy that governed the human denizens of the planet. The Monarch of Naboo was an elected monarch who stood as the leader of the Royal House of Naboo, governing the human population of the planet Naboo. They served for two-year terms which were renewable once.Queen's Shadow The people of Naboo often elected young women, believing they possessed a form of pure, childlike wisdom that the adults lacked.Star Wars: Absolutely Everything You Need to Know Theoretically, however, virtually anyone could be elected the new king or queen.The Star Wars Book Due to tradition and to protect their loved ones, Naboo monarchs adopted new identities when they began to campaign for the position, changing at least their last name. Even so, some monarchs proved to be less protective of their anonymity than others. They could return to their previous identity after their term endedQueen's Peril or chose to retain their new name. The monarch was aided by the Naboo Royal Advisory Council. The proper form of address for a former queen of Naboo was "M'Lady."Star Wars: Complete Vehicles (2018) Around 382 BBY,Star Wars: The High Republic Character Encyclopedia dates the events of The High Republic: Convergence to 382 BBY. a queen served as the monarch of Naboo. That queen owned a yacht, which the gambler Axel Greylark wanted to buy with his inheritance.The High Republic: Convergence Queens of Naboo included Padmé Amidala, who served two terms, the first of which included the Invasion of Naboo; Amidala's predecessor Queen Sanandrassa, who was herself preceded by Réillata, who served one term before successfully running again to serve a second term after Amidala's tenure; Jamillia, who served shortly before the outbreak of the Clone Wars; Neeyutnee, who served during the war; and Apailana, who served during the early days of the Galactic Empire. During the Invasion of Naboo, Viceroy of the Trade Federation Nute Gunray claimed the throne until he was overthrown by Amidala in the Battle of Naboo. Whilst the position of monarch carried with it actual power and an active role in governing the planet and its people, the establishment of the Galactic Empire saw the position reduced to that of a mere figurehead as all meaningful authority was transferred to the newly appointed Moff Quarsh Panaka. In the process, the Empire supplanted the authority of the planet's monarch. Queen Dalné, who served around 3 BBYStar Wars: Timelines dates the events of Leia, Princess of Alderaan to 3 BBY. and was heavily resentful of her reduced authority, nevertheless reluctantly went along with it rather than challenge the Empire and risk Imperial retribution for defiance. Naboo continued to prosper under the Empire and did not suffer, even if its citizens knew their safety and wealth depended on accepting the loss of their freedoms. Nonetheless, Naboo did experience a moment of Imperial punishment when the Sith Lord Darth Vader&mdash;secretly the former Jedi Anakin Skywalker and husband of the deceased Amidala&mdash;descended upon Naboo, seemingly embarking on a crusade to wipe away all memory of his dead wife. Vader cut down anyone who dared reference her memory, although her legacy as a beloved queen would survive his rampage. Sosha Soruna served as queen around the time following the Battle of Endor.Shattered Empire 2 In the wake of the Empire's fall, some people across the galaxy&mdash;having already been well aware of the fact that Naboo did not suffer under Imperial rule&mdash;felt that the planet should be punished, accusing certain citizens of profiting from the Empire. Even though Soruna and others on Naboo regarded Palpatine with distain,Shattered Empire 2 the Verunas, one of the former ruling families of Naboo, did profit greatly under Imperial rule. As new factions arose and distrust spread, Naboo's galactic influence was destroyed. All the while, the Verunas began annexing territory across Naboo and charging rent for its people to continue living there, securing a new source of income and killing those who rejected their authority. In 34 ABY, establishes that the events of Star Wars: Legacy of Vader take place immediately after the events of , which takes place in 34 ABY per Star Wars: Timelines. Supreme Leader Kylo Ren, the grandson of Vader and Amidala, ventured to Naboo to continue his study into his grandfather's background with Vader's former servant, Vaneé. While on world, Vaneé told Ren of the monarchy and Naboo's fall from grace, all while trying to make the Supreme Leader view the past as a fuel from which he could gather strength. Ren, however, regarded the past as a weakness and&mdash;unimpressed with how Naboo held so tightly onto the memory of its glory days&mdash;ordered a First Order attack on Naboo, declaring himself the new King of Naboo in the process. The 2016 reference book Star Wars Character Encyclopedia: Updated and Expanded states that King Ars Veruna preceded Padmé Amidala as monarch of Naboo,Star Wars Character Encyclopedia: Updated and Expanded with Star Wars: Complete Locations (2016) adding that he ruled for 13 years.Star Wars: Complete Locations The novel Tarkin (2014) previously stated that Naboo was ruled by a king fifteen years before the rise of the Galactic Empire,Tarkin which would correspond to 34 BBY,Star Wars: Timelines dates the events of Tarkin to 14 BBY. The flashback takes place twenty years prior to the main narrative, which can be calculated to 34 BBY. and the the fifty-fourth issue of the magazine Star Wars Helmet Collection (2018) also lists him as monarch before being assassinated. However, the novel Queen's Shadow (2019) states that monarchs are limited to two two-year terms, and its prequel Queen's Peril (2020) instead names Amidala's predecessors as Queens Sanandrassa and Réillata, who both served for one term, placing their reigns in 34 BBY giving no room for the king mentioned in Tarkin. The later editions of the Character Encyclopedia and Complete Locations, Star Wars: Character Encyclopedia, Updated and Expanded Edition and Star Wars: Complete Locations, New Edition remove the mentions of Veruna.Star Wars: Character Encyclopedia, Updated and Expanded Edition'Star Wars: Complete Locations, New Edition King Ars Veruna originally served as the monarchs of Naboo for 13 years in Legends before being murdered by Darth Plagueis.Darth Plagueis "Database," a department in the 2019 magazine Star Wars - Das offizielle Magazin 96, provides a different order for the Monarchs of Naboo. It mistakenly states that Ekay came after Réillata, when Ekay actually came before. It also places Veruna after Ekay, and maintains that he was Amidala's predecessor. It does not mention Ameé or Sanandrassa, as the magazine predates these characters. Queen's Shadow (2019) established that monarchs of Naboo serve for two-year terms. However, "Naboo," a 2020 issue of the French Star Wars Encyclopedia, states that a term was four years as was the case in Legends.
