The Floating Fish
1734

The Floating Fish was a casino owned and operated by Mussat Nasrabi on Cloud City, Bespin. Located on Level 42 in the Tourist District, it was registered with the Cloud City Gambling Guild. The casino was small but high-class, a two-storied establishment fronting onto an elegant plaza. Its external windows were polarized to provide privacy for its clients. Inside, it was tastefully decorated, with ornate pillars and a number of fish filled globes hanging from the ceiling. The casino gained a reputation for being clean and honest, and provided many games, such as sabacc and Trin sticks. The Floating Fish had a broad clientèle, with numerous Mon Calamari, Quarren, Duros, and Sullustans frequenting it.
