Code of the Sith
3345

}} thumb|250px|The Sith Code in [[Aurebesh]] The Code of the Sith, also known as Qotsisajak,Guardians of the Whills was a mantra that expressed the core beliefs of the Sith. It was an inversion of the Jedi Code, a set of rules for members of the Jedi Order. It notably taught its followers that it is for the strong to destroy the weak.Darth Vader: Sith Lord Peace is a lie. There is only Passion. Through Passion, I gain Strength. Through Strength, I gain Power. Through Power, I gain Victory. Through Victory my chains are Broken. The Force shall free me. In some versions of the Code, the initial line "Peace is a lie" was repeated after "The Force shall free me." The Code of the Sith first appeared in Star Wars canon in the Star Wars: The Clone Wars episode "Altar of Mortis." The Code of the Sith was originally created by David Gaider, who wrote the Korriban segment of the Star Wars Legends 2003 video game Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic.Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic Initially, Gaider had asked Lucasfilm what the Sith's philosophy was or if they had a code like the Jedi did.
