|published in=Tales from the Mos Eisley Cantina |reprinted in= |pages=15 |timeline=0 BBY (35 GrS) |series= |preceded by= |followed by= }} "Be Still My Heart: The Bartender's Tale" is a short story about Wuher by David Bischoff from Tales from the Mos Eisley Cantina, which was published by Bantam Spectra in August 1995. It takes place in 0 BBY. Wuher, a droid-hating bartender, is on his way to work at Chalmun's Spaceport Cantina. He desires to make a drink that would please Jabba the Hutt. In an alley, he meets a strange droid, C2-R4, who begs for Wuher to save him from the Jawas. Wuher curses the droid and all his kind, then marches off in a fury. At the cantina that day, Greedo is shot and killed by Han Solo. Wuher has an enhanced sense of smell, and decides that Greedo would be the perfect ingredient for his special drink. He remembers that the droid he saw earlier is equipped with a special grinder, and races off to find him. When he does, he tells the droid to come with him. He feeds Greedo's corpse to the droid, and adds it to his drink.
