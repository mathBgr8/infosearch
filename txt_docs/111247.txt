*John Whitman Dark Forces: Jedi Knight audio drama |narrator=Ken Hiller |producer=Tom Voegeli |director=Peter Moore |cover artist= |illustrator= |publisher=HighBridge Audio |release date=1998 |rereleased= |media type=*Cassette tape *Compact disc Dark Forces: The Collector's Trilogy |run time=1 hour 34 minutes(94 minutes)Dark Forces: Jedi Knight audio drama |discs= |cassettes=2 |pages= |isbn=9781565112766 |timeline=5 ABYThe Essential Reader's Companion places the Dark Forces: Jedi Knight novella, upon which this audio drama is based, in 5 ABY. |series=Star Wars: Dark Forces novellas |preceded by=Rebel Agent audio drama |followed by= }} Dark Forces: Jedi Knight was an audio dramatization based on William C. Dietz's novel of the same title adapted by John Whitman. The performance was directed by Peter Moore and featured music from the original movies' musical score by Star Wars composer John Williams. It was released November 1, 1998 by HighBridge Audio as a two cassette tape set. In Jedi Knight, Jerec, the Dark Jedi, brings his forces to Ruusan in the final part of his search for the Valley of the Jedi. Destroying anything that gets in his way, Jerec reaches the Valley just ahead of Kyle Katarn and Jan Ors. With the help of the indigenous inhabitants of the planet, Kyle and Jan confront Jerec's Jedi and prepare for a final showdown with Jerec. If Jerec defeats Kyle, the unlimited power contained in the Valley will enable him to rule the galaxy. Are Kyle's untrained Jedi powers enough to defeat Jerec?
