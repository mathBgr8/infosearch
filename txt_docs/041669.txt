Around the time of the start of the Clone Wars in 22 BBYThe New Essential Chronology dates the events of to 22 BBY. between the Confederacy of Independent Systems and the Galactic Republic, she frequented the Uscru Entertainment District of her homeworld. In that year, shortly before the war, Bringbit visited the district the night of the attempted assassination of Senator Padmé Amidala. The assassin Zam Wesell crashed her speeder there, throwing her pursuer, Jedi Anakin Skywalker, off the speeder. A surprised Bringbit and others surrounded Skywalker and watched on as he got up and ran off towards Wesell. Bringbit possessed light skin and blonde hair. When she visited the district, Bringbit wore a dark colored top, dark colored gloves, and two armbands on her left arm. Lillea Bringbit first appeared in the 2002 prequel trilogy film Star Wars: Episode II Attack of the Clones. The character was portrayed by Lucasfilm costume archivist Gillian Libbert at the prompting of Visual Effects Supervisor John Knoll. As the character, Libbert wore an outfit cobbled together from various sources. While filming, Libbert had a tendency to overreact, causing retakes and Knoll telling her to tone her performance down. Libbert did not realize how prominent Bringbit would be in the scene and upon seeing herself in the film, she was mortified. Bringbit was first named in "Who's Who in the Outlander Club: It's Ladies' Night," an article written by Daniel Wallace and published in the seventy-fifth issue of Star Wars Insider on April 6, 2004.
