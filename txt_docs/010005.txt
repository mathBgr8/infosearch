Retroactive continuity
56195

}} Retroactive continuity&mdash;commonly contracted to the portmanteau word retcon&mdash;refers to deliberately changing previously established facts in a work of serial fiction. The change itself is referred to as a retcon, and the act of writing and publishing a retcon is called "retconning." When George Lucas re-edited the original Star Wars trilogy, he made changes directly to the source material, rather than introduce new source material that contradicted the contents of the previous material. However, the Star Wars prequels qualified as "new source material," and many fans have pointed out instances which apparently retcon elements of the original trilogy. Although the concept is older, Note: This book seems to be the first printed use of the concept. the term "retroactive continuity" was popularized in 1983 by Comic Book writer Roy Thomas, and the shortened form "retcon" on Usenet in the late '80s. *In A New Hope, Obi-Wan Kenobi told Luke Skywalker that his father was betrayed and murdered by a pupil of his, Darth Vader. &mdash; Obi-Wan Kenobi: "A young Jedi named Darth Vader, who was a pupil of mine until he turned to evil, helped the Empire hunt down and destroy the Jedi Knights. He betrayed and murdered your father." The Empire Strikes Back reveals Darth Vader to be Luke Skywalker's father. &mdash; Darth Vader: "No. I am your father." In Return of the Jedi, Obi-Wan explained to Luke what he meant by telling him that Vader murdered his father, informing what he said was true, "from a certain point of view", since the Anakin that Obi-Wan remembered fondly "was destroyed" when he fell to the dark side of the Force, and therefore no longer considers him "Anakin" (this is supported by Obi-Wan only calling Vader "Darth" in A New Hope and not by his real name). While the in-universe explanation holds up fairly well, whether or not this was actually a retcon out of universe is one of the most hotly debated topics concerning the original trilogy.
