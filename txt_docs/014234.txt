It was home to a group of indigenous fighters that was affiliated with the Galactic Republic. During the Clone Wars, the Republic leadership was reluctant to allow widespread use of the kama, a command skirt and a potentially controversial symbol traditionally associated with the Mandalorian warrior culture, in the ranks of the Grand Army of the Republic. That changed when it was pointed out that the indigenous fighters of Rotas V also wore kamas, and the skirts subsequently worn by the Republic's clone trooper commanders were modeled after those of the Rotas V fighters.Star Wars: The Complete Visual Dictionary, 2006 edition Rotas V was first mentioned in the 2005 reference book Star Wars: Revenge of the Sith: The Visual Dictionary, which was authored by James Luceno.Star Wars: Revenge of the Sith: The Visual Dictionary The 2009 reference book The Essential Atlas placed the Rotas system, and therefore Rotas V, in grid square S-14. *Star Wars: Revenge of the Sith: The Visual Dictionary *Star Wars: The Complete Visual Dictionary, 2006 edition *The Complete Star Wars Encyclopedia *Star Wars: The Complete Visual Dictionary, 2016 edition Category:Planets in the Outer Rim Territories Category:Planets in the Slice Category:Terrestrial planets Category:Yminis sector locations
