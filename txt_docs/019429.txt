|birth= |death=22 BBY, Rattatak |species=Shikitari |gender= |pronouns=He/him |height= |mass= |hair= |feathers= |eyes= |skin=Blue-green |cyber= |families= |parents= |partners= |siblings= |children= |affiliation= |masters= |apprentices=*Devaronian conman *Unnamed owner }} Carl was a Shikitari insectoid who was purchased to participate as a gladiator in free-for-all mortal brawls in the Cauldron arena, the largest combat pit on the planet Rattatak, shortly after the outbreak of the Clone Wars. However, Carl was quickly killed by a Bugnaught droid, which burned him alive. This Shikitari insectoid was born on a jungle world in the Unknown Regions and lived during the final years of the Old Republic.The New Essential Chronology He belonged to a rare subsentient species, notable for its multi-stage life cycle. At one point, a Devaronian conman learned of their specific physiology and decided to carry out a scam involving a Shikitari insectoid. One of them, who was given the unlikely name Carl, was a collection of pupae that had amalgamated into a humanoid form and was perfectly suited for gladiatorial combat. Since the Shikitari stage as a single lifeform was extremely short, the Devaronian took advantage of the widespread ignorance surrounding their unique physiology and sold Carl for a sizable sum, snickering to himself that in two days his buyer would be the proud owner of a hardened chrysalis from which twelve harmless creatures would emerge. 280px|left|thumb|A Bugnaught droid incinerated Carl After being purchased, Carl was immediately entered into the Rattataki gladiatorial games, and in this way he ended up in The Cauldron. It was the largest gladiatorial pit on Rattatak, filled with violent warriors, where war barons and generals sought out the best combatants who would win their wars. But it was a rare gladiator indeed who could survive multiple fights there. Therefore, shortly after the outbreak of the Clone Wars, Carl, like many other gladiators, entered a deadly free-for-all battle, facing off in particular against the combat droid L8-L9.The New Essential Guide to Droids However, an encounter with a Bugnaught droid proved decisive for the Shikitari. Carl lifted the apparently defenseless machine over his head, roaring furiously. It then proceeded to unleash its flamethrower arm, killing Carl. The alien, which looked like a hulking insectoid, was in fact a collection of pupae that had amalgamated into a humanoid form capable of combat. That single organism, known as Carl, was perfectly suited for gladiatorial combat but remained dim-witted, unable to plan his moves in battle, and therefore relied on his brute strength. In battle, his body was covered only by a simple brown loincloth. Carl first appeared as an unnamed background character in the sixth chapter of Star Wars: Clone Wars, an animated television micro-series produced by Cartoon Network StudiosStar Wars: Clone Wars Volume One and originally aired on November 14, 2003. The character was later given a name and short backstory in The Complete Star Wars Encyclopedia, a 2008 reference book written by Stephen J.
