It was the homeworld of the sentient humanoid species known as the Yatir. During the Galactic Civil War, the Yatir were sympathetic to the Rebel Alliance. At some point before the Battle of Hoth of 3 ABY,The New Essential Chronology the Galactic Empire finally subjugated Absit and the Yatir, with the Imperial Army vanguard armor regiment known as the Imperial Hammers Elite Armor Unit being instrumental in the operation. The Empire subsequently used the native population of Absit for enslaved labor.The Essential Atlas Years later, Captain Zgorth'sth of the New Republic noted the Imperial subjugation of Absit in a threat assessment report focusing on various special units. Absit was first mentioned in the 1989 Imperial Sourcebook, written by Greg Gorden and published by West End Games for Star Wars: The Roleplaying Game.Imperial Sourcebook The 2009 reference book The Essential Atlas placed the Absit system, and therefore the planet itself, in grid square I-19, although the system was also erroneously placed in grid square J-13 as part of the Inner Rim in the Appendix section of the book. "Twinkle, Twinkle Little Star, How I Wonder Where We Are," a 1990 roleplaying game source article published in Voyages SF 13, placed the Absit system&mdash;as well as the Dalchon, Mediraan, "Ryluth" (a misspelling of the Ryloth system), Tatoo, and Yavin systems&mdash;in a sector named the "Outer Rim Territory." Since that article was released outside of the Lucas Licensing process, it was considered non-canonical with respect to the Star Wars Legends continuity.
