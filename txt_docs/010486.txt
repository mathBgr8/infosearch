They were a favorite of General Grievous, who used them as his bodyguards during the Clone Wars, though they were also known to accompany other high-ranking Separatist personnel including Count Dooku. MagnaGuards were equipped with electrostaffs that could be used against Jedi lightsabers and were capable of continuing a fight even with the loss of one or multiple limbs or even their heads. They were worthy competitors even to the most skilled Jedi; one was even able to back Obi-Wan Kenobi into a corner. While formidable opponents in close-quarters, they were also capable of piloting Rogue-class Porax-38 starfighters and using blasters and RPS-6 rocket launchers, and were often seen wearing cloaks.Star Wars: The Clone Wars film MagnaGuards usually fought in pairs of two, one to occupy the Jedi's lightsaber and one to attack from the unguarded flanks or behind. The presence of MagnaGuards indicated that high-ranking commanders were present, and gave the droids automatic authority over other battle droids deployed. thumb|left|180px|A MagnaGuard with electrostaff powered down Developed by Holowan Mechanicals, which was a part of Holowan Laboratories, the IG-100 MagnaGuard&mdash;also known simply as the MagnaGuard, or the magnadroid&mdash;was designed to look intimidating. Measuring 1.95 meters tall, each model was distinguished by a color: black, alabaster, blue, and gray, with the sinister, dull red or yellow glare of the droids' photoreceptor eyes, their expressionless faces, and their sheer physical mass, all contributed to their fearsome appearance. Developers at Holowan Mechanicals modeled the droid's head after the legacy Krath droids, drawing influence from its appearance. Created to the cyborg's specifications, the MagnaGuards resembled the powerful Kaleesh warriors from General Grievous' past.Star Wars Day-at-a-Time Calendar 2019 In the same way that his own facemask paid tribute to his past as a Kaleesh warrior, Grievous ordered and made certain that the MagnaGuards who served under him were outfitted with cloaks with mumuu marking and half-hoods in honor of his original faithful Izvoshra bodyguards, as well as an homage to the traditions of his planet Kalee. Additionally, this helped give the IG-100 droids an even more menacing appearance. During development, the MagnaGuards were cleverly named the IG-series 100 Prototype Self-Motivating Heuristically Programmed Combat Droid. Two important parts from that name marked them unique from typical battle droids. They were self-motivated and required no droid control programming. When assigned a task, they completed it sensibly and cleverly overcame any challenges that arose. Grievous specifically valued this heuristic programming feature in the MagnaGuards, allowing them to learn from their experiences, and personally trained the droids in everything that he had learned to assure their fighting effectiveness, and then utilized them as sparring partners to stay sharp. Even the surviving sustained dents and scars from their rigorous training regimen with Grievous. The droids were so successful that Count Dooku used them as bodyguards, and they were assigned to several sites throughout the galaxy, while Grievous was almost always accompanied by at least two at his side. The MagnaGuards quickly gained the same fearsome reputation as their master. thumb|right|250px|IG-100 MagnaGuards were adept with many melee weapons, including electro-whips. The electrostaff, the MagnaGuard's standard weapon, supplemented their instruction in the seven classic forms of lightsaber combat. Constructed from phrik metal, the staff was capable of withstanding lightsaber attacks, something that originally caught many Jedi fatally off guard. Despite typically carrying electrostaffs, the MagnaGuard's combat systems and human-like hands enabled them to use a variety of different weapons. The IG-100 droids were known to carry additional weaponry, particularly for range combat, with a bias for the significantly explosive. Several variations of MagnaGuard, with a range of colors and integrated equipment, include blasters, RPS-6 rocket launchers, precision laser darts, tow-cables, magnetic or extensible limbs, and even personal cloaking devices. They were also great pilots and most often flew Rogue-class Porax-38 starfighters. A MagnaGuard could lose an arm or even both legs and still pose a threat, making it difficult to defeat them. Thanks to several backup mechanisms and a spare photoreceptor in the chest, the MagnaGuards could lose their heads and continue to fight. Jedi and clone troopers eventually learned to target their vital systems, although even this was difficult, the IG-100s were well armored, with duranium employed in both their main frame and armor plating, the same duranium plates that served General Grievous so well. It was difficult to even hit one hard enough to knock it down or stagger it, as their feet were magnetically anchored and their heels extended for greater stability. With less communication required, the IG-100 droids had only the most basic vocoder, giving them deep, harsh voices to acknowledge their master's orders. IG-100s appeared to occasionally use an audible form of droid language or could speak in Galactic Basic Standard. When in distress, such as when pinned to the ground, they also make yelping sounds. As the Clone Wars progressed, the MagnaGuards that survived received several nicks and scars to their body armor, and Grievous made sure those were left readily apparent, as the battle-scarred contributed to their scary image, to show their enemy that they were confronting battle veterans. Any attacker attempting to reach Grievous would have to fight such trained bodyguards as these. The formidable droids were taught to battle in groups and rarely worked alone, typically with at least two or three, and created a barrier around Grievous that was difficult to get through. Grievous frequently ordered MagnaGuards to soften up an opponent,Darth Maul — Son of Dathomir 1 sacrificing them to allow him to easily kill later on. One or two engaged a Jedi, while the other waited for an opportunity to strike them down. Even if the IG-100s failed to kill their opponent, the enemy that eventually reached Grievous would barely be a genuine threat, as those who survived were often too weary to provide much more than amusement for the cyborg general. They served as scouts and assassins, as well as to enforce the will of others. MagnaGuard were terrifying on their own, but the threat they posed was that Grievous, or Dooku, was never far behind them. After the Republic and the Jedi intervened in the fight between his people and the Yam'rii, Grievous was offered by San Hill, Chairman of the InterGalactic Banking Clan, the chance to become an enforcer, which he accepted. Grievous demanded a more intelligent personal guard after being forced to command the IGBC droids he despised. In response, Count Dooku commissioned the creation of the IG-100 MagnaGuards, who were also created with the express intention of fighting Jedi. They were both destroyed when the Jedi caused the crystal to explode. The MagnaGuards, much like the majority of the Separatist droid contingents, nearly vanished from the Galactic scene after the Clone Wars. It's unclear whether they were automatically deactivated with the other Separatist droids or if they were eliminated individually later. The majority of units were destroyed or taken out of service, while others were purchased by collectors or repurposed for new usages.
