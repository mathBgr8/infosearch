Holocron continuity database
11496

thumb|300px|Screenshot of the Holocron in 2012 }} The Holocron continuity database is the database used internally by Lucas Licensing to keep track of all of the fictional elements created for the Star Wars universe. It is maintained by Leland Chee on FileMaker Pro software, and was created in January 2000, initially to support the new Star Wars roleplaying game being created by Wizards of the Coast. Starting from a core of material imported into the database from the Behind the Magic CD-ROM, it has since grown substantially as new information has been added. The first article, titled "Welcome to the Holocron!", was created on February 9, 2000, and 22 years later, the Holocron reached the major milestone of 100,000 entries, on February 16, 2022. One of the entries is about the band Jefferson Starship, who were featured within the The Star Wars Holiday Special.|archiveurl=https://archive.ph/Z2Kzl}} As of February 3, 2026, the Holocron had 120,000 entries. According to Chee, before the creation of the Holocron, Lucasfilm kept track of continuity physically on black binders, internally nicknamed the "Star Wars Bible." There are no plans to release any portion of the Holocron to the public. One reason cited by Chee is: }} A rare glimpse at the Holocron was seen at the Celebration IV "30 Years of Star Wars Publishing and Continuity" panel. At Celebration Anaheim in 2015, Chee reiterated that Lucas Licensing will never make the Holocron public, because it includes forthcoming content. According to Chee, the Holocron continuity database provides services to all departments within Lucasfilm, it does not belong to any specific department. Fields for entries in the database include "Keywords," "Era of influence" (e.g., "the New Republic"), "Classification" (e.g., "location"), "Subtype" (e.g., "System:planet", "Vehicle Type:speeder", "Device:Medical", "Slang:Curse", "Alien:Mammalian"), "Brief description," "Size," and "Special abilities." Alien-specific fields include "Diet" and "Disposition," and for organizations, "Goals," though these are not used often. For astronomical objects, information for region, sector, system, and grid coordinates is tracked. Over time, fields like foreign language translation and timeline have become obsolete, replaced with other systems. Continuity-related fields such as "Legends," "LEGO/Visions" are also used.
