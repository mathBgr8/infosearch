|leader=Rajah Ubooki |subgroup= |headquarters=Scardia Station |locations=First Church of the Dark Side |formed= |founded=3 or 4 ABY |fragmented=5 ABYThe Star Wars Spy Game: SPIN Declassified places the Bombardment of Scardia Station between the Trioculus affair and the Battle of Mindor, which The New Essential Chronology dates both to 5 ABY. |reorganized= |dissolved= |restored= |affiliation=*Galactic Empire **Imperial Intelligence }} The Church of the Dark Side was a religious organization devoted to the worship of the Dark Side of the Force that came into prominence during the Imperial Era and was sanctioned by the Galactic Empire. Secretly, the Church of the Dark Side was just a undercover propaganda division of the Imperial Intelligence in mockery of the Prophets of the Dark Side, a dark side cult as part of a elaborate ploy in order to tighten their grip over the Imperial populace. thumb|left|200px|The [[Rajah Ubooki|false Kadann, one of the impostors in the Church of the Dark Side.]] Some time prior to the Battle of Endor, a rift occurred in the ranks of the Secret Order of the Emperor, the collective body the members of which held the title Prophets of the Dark Side. Some of the Prophets sided with Supreme Prophet Kadann, who warned of potential catastrophe at a upcoming battle; some joined Emperor Palpatine in rejecting Kadann's warnings. Kadann's followers withdrew to Bosthirda, whereas defectors such as Lord Cronal, Sariss, and Merili remained with the Galactic Empire. Prior to the Battle of Endor, Imperial Intelligence formed a propaganda machine known as the Church of the Dark Side. The Church was sanctioned on Imperial Center as the official Imperial religion by acting-Emperor Sate Pestage and secretly patronized by the resurrected Emperor in hiding on Byss. The Church established branches throughout the galaxy. Former Prophets such as Cronal, Sariss, and Merili claimed devotion to the quasi-religion, using it as a means to manipulate converted trade barons and military officers, such as Grand Admirals Ishin-Il-Raz and Peccati Syn. Ultimately, the purpose of the Church was to sow dissension amongst the various rival warlords, preventing any one individual from rising to power over the entire Empire, thus ensuring that, when the time came, the Imperial throne would remain empty and waiting for the return of Palpatine. The Church even included a supposed "holy place", Scardia Station, where a number of Imperial Intelligence agents, none really Force-sensitive, publicly posed as the Prophets of the Dark Side. These false Prophets included impersonators of Kadann, Jedgar, and Gornash, individuals hiding on Bosthirda at this time. These Prophets were sponsored by Sate Pestage, himself ordered to be murdered at the instigation of Intelligence Director Ysanne Isard. thumb|right|250px|The Church's headquarters, [[Scardia Station.]] The false Prophets continually labored to manipulate events so that their own prophecies would come true. The "prophecies" of the pseudo-Kadann, gathered by the pseudo-Jedgar in the holy book Secrets of the Dark Side, failed one after another, however, and they ended up at odds with both the Grand Moffs and the warlords. Their phony claims about Trioculus' heritage were exposed as lies when the existence of Palpatine's true son, Triclops, was revealed. Later, the imitator of Supreme Prophet Kadann attempted to take over Imperial Center, but Ysanne Isard successfully defended the planet. His attempt largely failed because of lack of support from the warlords, many of whom held grudges against him--for the most part, actual grudges against the true Prophet the con man was imitating. The success of pseudo-Kadann's imitation was his downfall. He deceived Grand Admiral Afsheen Makati into thinking he was the real Kadann, whom Makati hated. Using intelligence given him by Azrakel, who also sought to gain revenge on the real Kadann, Makati brought every ship at his disposal to destroy Scardia Station, killing the impostor. As for other prominent members of the Church, Sariss met a violent end, while Merili faded into obscurity, and Cronal, the original Blackhole, was destroyed, with Sate Pestage taking over the role of Blackhole. In addition, the Church of the Dark Side had secretly been manipulated by Palpatine (who had been revived after his death at Endor), with one of their decrees, pertaining to the Glove, actually being leaked to them specifically so Palpatine could use Vader's glove to restore his Force abilities.
