Trith prancer
891

Trith prancers were domesticated riding animals that had been gradually genetically engineered since around 25,000 BBY. They had smooth gray-blue fur, six legs, and a back that served as a natural saddle with upward-jutting shoulder bones. They made flute-like noises.
