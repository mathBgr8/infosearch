|published in=Star Wars Tales 14 |reprinted in= |pages=8 |timeline= |series= |preceded by= |followed by= }} "The Emperor's Court" is a non-canonical comic book from Star Wars Tales 14. Han Solo is in court for killing Greedo. The plaintiff is Greedo's mother, Neela. After a few alien witnesses are called, Judge Palpatine declares their testimonies have no effect given that they are nonhuman. Han shows a disk of Greedo shooting first and missing, but it was obviously faked (a reference to the re-release when Greedo shot first). Han is sentenced to life imprisonment frozen in carbonite and is sent to Boba Fett. The segment is a parody of The People's Court. This spoof plays on several of the changes in the 1997 Special Edition of A New Hope, primarily on the subject of Greedo firing first at Han Solo instead of Han getting off the only shot. Another allusion to the Special Edition is Han protesting Lak Sivrak's story by saying that Sivrak was not even present at the time, as he was replaced in the Special Edition by Ketwol. The 8D smelter droid LU-L-N is a parody of reporter Doug Llewelyn, who reported on the television series The People's Court. Han Solo's continual rewinding of the hologram of Greedo firing at him and repeating of the line "back and to the right" is a reference to the Oliver Stone film JFK.
