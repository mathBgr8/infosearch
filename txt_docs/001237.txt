thumb|right|200px 1138 is a number that appears in products that are related to George Lucas and his works. This recurrent in-joke in Star Wars films and LucasArts computer games originates in Lucas' short student film Electronic Labyrinth: THX 1138 4EB, which was later reworked into his first theatrical release, THX 1138. The number appears in several Star Wars productions in honor of the director's previous works. Many elements in Star Wars include 1138 in their names, and it is a frequent easter egg in Star Wars products: *In the film , when an Imperial officer questions Luke Skywalker and Han Solo about Chewbacca, Luke attempts to fool him by claiming it is a "Prisoner transfer from Cell Block 1138." **Shortly afterward, Han says to Luke, "We gotta find out which cell this princess of yours is in. Here it is. 2187." **When C-3PO asks what he and R2-D2 should do if they are discovered, the first line on the monitor behind them reads "THX/1138". *In the film , shortly before the Battle of Hoth, General Rieekan orders Rogues 10 and 11 to Station 3-8. *In , 1138 is inscribed on the side of Boushh's helmet. thumb|right|250px|1138 in The Phantom Menace *In , the identification number 1138 is printed on the back of the comlink booster pack of the B1 battle droid that Jar Jar pushes over the droid control ship is shut down. The number is written in Trade Federation Script, but can easily be deciphered even without a guide. *In , 1138 is printed on the back of the booster back of a B1 battle droid that is firing at opposing Jedi while Mace Windu deflects a blaster shot into a OOM command battle droid during the Battle of Geonosis. This number is also written in Trade Federation Script, but is easily deciphered without a guide.
