RH7 CardShark
3847

The RH7 CardShark was a sabacc-dealing droid made by LeisureMech Enterprises. They were installed in casinos across the galaxy as a replacement for organic dealers. The RH7 CardShark was a dealer droid specialized for the card game sabacc. The droid hung down from the ceiling, with a wide, rounded base bolted directly above the gaming table. The main section of the droid consisted of the dealer mechanism, which could raise and lower itself to a maximum extension of one and half meters. The CardShark was equipped with specialized sensor arrays, an orifice resembling a mouth that was used to store sabacc cards, and two manipulator arms to deal the cards with. The droid also had a Card-flux Pulse Emitter installed to randomize the values on the sabacc cards. Six surveillance eyes ringed the rounded base that were used to monitor the sabacc players' hands, watching for any evidence of cheating. If the droid discovered that a player was using a skifter card, two heavy grasper arms descended to remove the player. Modified force pikes capable of delivering moderate stun shocks could be deployed in order to keep other players away from the cheater, or to protect the dealer droid until bouncers could respond to the incident. RH7 CardSharks were intended to replace organic dealers in gambling establishments. The dealer droid controlled the sabacc game, announcing the house rules and the variant being played, such as Bespin Standard or Empress Teta Preferred, before play began. During the game, the CardShark shuffled and dealt the cards, and initiated rounds of betting. During play, the pulse emitter randomly sent out electronic pulses to the cards to shift their values unless they had been placed in the gaming table's interference field. RH7 CardSharks were manufactured by LeisureMech Enterprises. The Crystal Jewel casino on the planet Coruscant employed RH7 CardSharks. In some situations, the droid was directed to cheat the players at the direction of the casino to ensure the house won.
