In 23 BBY, they won the annually-hosted Mobquet Presents: Fastest Land Beings race held on the world Teyr. The next year, Pumav placed first in the same competition's quadruped division and also finished seventh in the meet's biped race, which was reported on by HoloNet News CoCo District Edition. Hailing from the planetThe Essential Guide to Planets and Moons Selonia, Pumav was a Selonian who lived during the Galactic Republic's reign, competing as a sprinter in competitions by racing on either two or four limbs. In 23 BBY, is set on the date 13:4:18, which corresponds to 22 BBY according to the reasoning here. As the article establishes that Pumav won an iteration of the annually-hosted Mobquet Presents: Fastest Land Beings race one year before its publication, they must have been in the competition in 23 BBY. the Selonian won the quadruped race in the prestigious Mobquet Presents: Fastest Land Beings racing meet, held annually in the city Pyre Folas on the worldShield of Lies Teyr. The next year, Pumav repeated as the Mobquet Presents' quadruped champion and also finished seventh in the meet's biped race, losing to the Teek Yeep, who easily outran Pumav and the rest of the competitors. The Selonian received a medal for the victory and later posed for a picture along with Yeep and the meet's hexaped race winner, Yopaxtul. The events of that year's meet, including Pumav's participation, were reported on by an edition of HoloNet News CoCo District Edition. Pumav had black eyes and was covered in a mixture of black, white, and gray fur. The Selonian was skilled at racing on varying amounts of their limbs comparative to other biped and quadrupeds in the galaxy by 23 BBY. In 22 BBY's Mobquet Presents meet, they wore a gray head covering, a reflective yellow visor over their eyes, a gray waist sash, and a brown belt with several pockets. Pumav appeared in "New Galaxy's Fastest Hexaped," an article written by Pablo Hidalgo and Paul Ens and published in HoloNet News Vol. 531 #52 on the HoloNet News website on April 18, 2002.
