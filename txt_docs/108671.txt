Seeking the approval of Emperor Palpatine following his appointment to the rank in 2 BBY, Il-Raz commanded several successful missions from the bridge of the Emperor's Disciple, including the Massacre of Myomar and the bombardment of Shalam. When Palpatine was killed at the Battle of Endor in 4 ABY,The New Essential Chronology places the Battle of Endor in the year 4 ABY. Il-Raz spiralled into a suicidal depression. Eight months later, the grand admiral acted on his emotions, flying the Emperor's Disciple into the firestorm of the Denarii Nova, killing all 37,000 people aboard, including himself. His death was later referenced in The Essential Chronology, a historical document compiled by the New Republic. The Emperor's Disciple was first indirectly mentioned in the 2000 reference book The Essential Chronology, written by Kevin J. Anderson and Daniel Wallace, as the flagship of Grand Admiral Il-Raz. The 2003 article "Who's Who: Imperial Grand Admirals," also written by Wallace along with Abel G. Peña, and published in Star Wars Insider 66, was the first source to name the ship.
