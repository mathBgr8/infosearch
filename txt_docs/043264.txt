|birth= |death=22 BBY, Rattatak |species=Rodisar |gender=Male |pronouns=He/him |height= |mass= |hair= |feathers= |eyes=Blue, Black |skin=Pale |cyber= |families= |parents= |partners= |siblings= |children= |affiliation=*Personal followers *Zygerrian slavers *Rattataki war barons |masters= |apprentices= }} Nilo was a male Rodisar from the planet Rodis, where he had served as a general during the planet's seasonal wars. During the final years of the Old Republic, he was kidnapped and sold to Rattataki war barons. As a result, Nilo became a gladiator in the Cauldron arena, where he was killed in his very first battle. A Rodisar from the battlefields of Rodis, the Outer Rim Territories' &mdash; Based on corresponding data for Rodis system planet that experienced seasonal wars among its primitive inhabitants. Nilo had once been a general in the so-called winter wars. Though strong and a capable brawler, in truth, Nilo was a lackluster military leader. He had climbed the corrupt military ranks of Rodis by bribing politicians, while most of his actual military expertise had come from his command staff. Nilo and his command staff were later kidnapped in a Zygerrian slaver run, and most of his followers were killed trying to protect their general. The slavers sold Nilo to Rattataki war barons, impressing them with tales of his supposed military exploits. 280px|left|thumb|General Nilo fighting a gladiator Shortly after the outbreak of the Clone Wars,The New Essential Chronology Nilo in this way ended up in The Cauldron arena. It was the largest gladiatorial pit on the planet Rattatak, filled with violent warriors, where war barons and generals sought out the best combatants who would win their wars. In his first fight, Nilo faced an armored gladiator. At the same time, Asajj Ventress entered the fray and, with the assistance of fire shells launched by the combat droid L8-L9, killed most of the remaining combatants. Nilo was struck by a fiery blast, and he did not emerge alive. Nilo was a male who belonged to the reptilian species. Despite having little tactical skill, Nilo climbed the military ranks of his homeworld through bribes and pay offs, eventually gaining notoriety for his many «war exploits», which in fact were due more towards his command staff than Nilo himself. Though Nilo appeared to be a strong and capable brawler, ex-general saw his first real combat in the Cauldron gladiator pit, from which he did not emerge alive. 180px|right|thumb|Nilo was based on an unused Gamorrean Guard concept Nilo first appeared as an unnamed background character in the sixth chapter of Star Wars: Clone Wars, an animated television micro-series produced by Cartoon Network StudiosStar Wars: Clone Wars Volume One and originally aired on November 14, 2003. The character was later given a name and short backstory in the personal Databank entry on the official Star Wars website, StarWars.com, in 2011. Nilo's appearance was based on unused concept drawings&mdash;done by the artist Nilo Rodis-Jamero&mdash;of the Gamorrean guards during the production of Star Wars: Episode VI Return of the Jedi.
